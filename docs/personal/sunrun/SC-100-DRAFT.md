> On November 7, 2025, Sunrun technicians serviced the solar system at my home at 5 Caladium, Rancho Santa Margarita, CA 92688 and disconnected it. They did not tell me. I have two Sunrun systems at this property: a 2012 prepaid Sunrun PPA (paid $20,000 up-front for 20 years of power) and a 2017 customer-owned system I bought through Costco and Sunrun installed. Combined capacity is 11.16 kW.
>
> The disconnection was total. From November 7, 2025 through at least April 16, 2026 — over five months — the system produced essentially zero exportable kWh, confirmed by my SDG&E NEM meter (Meter #06688420, Account #0036 1837 1728 9). I only discovered this when my SDG&E bills jumped far above normal. Sunrun opened internal Case #18181148 on November 10, 2025 but did not contact me about it until January 16, 2026, and even then only said they "detected an issue" — never that the system was producing zero power.
>
> Sunrun then scheduled itself to come out on April 22, 2026, without me choosing that date. On April 15, 2026, I emailed Sunrun "Need urgent fix." On April 16, 2026, Sunrun dispatched technicians to my home without prior confirmation or notice. That visit partially restored the 2012 PPA system but the 2017 system is still not producing normally as of the date of this claim.
>
> The excess electricity I paid to SDG&E during the five-month outage, documented month-by-month in my SDG&E bills, totals approximately $3,600. I expect additional excess charges through full restoration and my annual NEM true-up in November 2026. My 2012 PPA also contains a Section 8 Guaranteed Output clause that obligates Sunrun to refund the prepaid value for kWh the system failed to produce.
>
> I sent Sunrun a formal written demand on April 16, 2026 and a detailed factual follow-up on April 19, 2026. Sunrun's Service Pipeline Specialist Katherine Wilson responded with settlement offers of $450 and then $500, while asserting that a contract clause about "Supplemental Energy" caps their liability. That clause does not apply to a five-month total outage caused by Sunrun's own technicians disconnecting the system. Sunrun has refused to pay the documented $3,600 in excess utility charges, the Section 8 production guarantee shortfall, or anything reasonably related to my actual damages.
>
> Sunrun Installation Services, Inc. holds California Contractors State License #750184 and Sunrun, Inc. holds #969975. Their workmanship and failure to notify me of their own service-visit disconnection is the direct cause of my loss.
>
> I am asking the court to award me $10,000 to cover the documented excess utility charges, additional charges accruing while the 2017 system remains broken, the contractual Section 8 production-guarantee refund, and my time and inconvenience over the past six months.
