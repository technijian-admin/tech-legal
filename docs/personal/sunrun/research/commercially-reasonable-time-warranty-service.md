## "Reasonable Time" Benchmarks in California

### Statutory — Song-Beverly Act
**Civ. Code § 1793.2(b):** "Where … service or repair of the goods is necessary because they do not conform with the applicable express warranties, **service and repair shall be commenced within a reasonable time** by the manufacturer or its representative … . Unless the buyer agrees in writing to the contrary, **the goods shall be serviced or repaired so as to conform to the applicable warranties within 30 days.**"

→ **30 days is the default California consumer-warranty benchmark.** Solar PPAs may not be "consumer goods" within Song-Beverly, but the 30-day benchmark is routinely cited by analogy.

### Civ. Code § 1657
"If no time is specified for the performance of an act required to be performed, a reasonable time is allowed."

### CACI 3205
Failure to begin repairs within reasonable time / complete within 30 days = breach. Applied in *Mocek v. Alfa Leisure, Inc.* (2003) 114 Cal.App.4th 402, 406 (RV warranty); *Robertson v. Fleetwood Travel Trailers* (2006) 144 Cal.App.4th 785, 798 (commercially reasonable repair attempts).

## Implied Covenant of Good Faith and Fair Dealing — *Foley v. Interactive Data Corp.* (1988) 47 Cal.3d 654
Every California contract contains the implied covenant. A party "may not do anything which will deprive the other of the benefits of the agreement." Sunrun's 67-day silence + 5-month-and-counting non-restoration of the 2017 system = textbook frustration of bargain.

**But:** *Foley* limits implied-covenant breach to **contract** damages outside the insurance context. Confirmed in:
- *Cates Construction v. Talbot Partners* (1999) 21 Cal.4th 28 — no tort recovery for surety's bad-faith breach.
- *Freeman & Mills v. Belcher Oil* (1995) 11 Cal.4th 85 — no "tortious breach of contract" cause of action.

→ Implied-covenant claim still useful: it (a) extends contract damages beyond the four corners, (b) supports specific performance / injunction, (c) bolsters fraud narrative for jury.

## What Counts as "Reasonable" — Cases

| Case | Time | Holding |
|---|---|---|
| *Mocek v. Alfa Leisure* (2003) 114 Cal.App.4th 402 | 4 separate repair attempts, 60+ days OOS | Unreasonable. |
| *Krotin v. Porsche Cars N. Am.* (1995) 38 Cal.App.4th 294, 302 | Multiple attempts, 30+ days | Triggered Song-Beverly remedy. |
| *Schreidel v. American Honda* (1995) 34 Cal.App.4th 1242 | 4+ repair attempts | "Reasonable number" exceeded. |
| *Lukather v. General Motors* (2010) 181 Cal.App.4th 1041 | 30 days OOS | Statutory presumption of unreasonableness. |
| *Brand v. Hyundai Motor America* (2014) 226 Cal.App.4th 1538 | Single visit but defect persisted | Repair must actually fix. |

**Five months without restoration of the 2017 system is unreasonable as a matter of law under any of these benchmarks.**

## Application to Sunrun
