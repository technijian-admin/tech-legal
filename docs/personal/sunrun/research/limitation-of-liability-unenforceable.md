## Headline — *New England Country Foods, LLC v. Vanlaw Food Products, Inc.* (Cal. April 24, 2025) S282968

**Categorical rule:** "Parties may not contract away liability for willful injury to the person or property of another." § 1668 invalidates **not only complete liability waivers but also any provision that limits damages — including caps on direct damages and exclusions of consequential damages — for willful misconduct.** Most important § 1668 ruling in 60 years. Directly defeats:
- 2012 PPA § 15 ($1M cap, consequentials excluded)
- 2017 contract ($22,474.07 cap, consequentials excluded)

— **to the extent** the homeowner pleads willful concealment, fraud, or violation of statute (CLRA / UCL / Bus. & Prof. § 7169).

## Civ. Code § 1668
"All contracts which have for their object, directly or indirectly, to exempt any one from responsibility for his own fraud, or willful injury to the person or property of another, **or violation of law, whether willful or negligent**, are against the policy of the law."

Three exemption-defeating triggers: fraud (any kind); willful injury; **violation of law (willful OR negligent)**. The third trigger means even *negligent* CLRA/UCL/Bus. & Prof. § 7169 violations void the cap.

## How to Plead Around the Caps
1. Plead **fraud-by-concealment as a tort** (not just breach by fraud-in-performance). § 1668 doesn't save caps against tort fraud.
2. Plead **CLRA, UCL, Bus. & Prof. § 7169** — § 1668's "violation of law" trigger.
3. Argue caps fail *Tunkl* on their face for the public-interest reasons above.

## Risks
- *Food Safety Net Servs. v. Eco Safe Sys.* (2012) 209 Cal.App.4th 1118, 1126 — LoL enforceable for ordinary negligence between *sophisticated commercial parties*. Distinguishable (residential consumer adhesion).
- *NECF* applies only to willful injury / fraud / statutory violations — never let the case be characterized as pure contract.
