**Date:** April 17, 2026 | **For:** Sunrun matter, 5 Caladium

## Baseline
- 9 U.S.C. § 2 + *AT&T Mobility LLC v. Concepcion* (2011) 563 U.S. 333 — FAA preempts state rules categorically invalidating class-action waivers.
- *Discover Bank v. Sup. Ct.* (2005) 36 Cal.4th 148 — held class waivers unconscionable; **abrogated by Concepcion**.
- *American Express v. Italian Colors* (2013) 570 U.S. 228 — class waivers enforceable even if individual claims uneconomic.

## Surviving California Defenses

### 1. Generally Applicable Unconscionability — *Sanchez v. Valencia Holding Co.* (2015) 61 Cal.4th 899
"Procedural and substantive unconscionability must both be present" but on a sliding scale. 2012 Sunrun PPA is a standard adhesion contract → **procedural** unconscionability satisfied at low level. Look for **substantive** harshness:
- One-sided JAMS designation;
- Cost-shifting forcing consumer to bear disproportionate fees;
- Carve-outs that benefit only Sunrun (e.g., right to seek injunction in court while consumer is locked into arbitration);
- Hidden in adhesion contract.

JAMS Consumer Minimum Standards (Rule 1.D) cap consumer fees at filing fee; that helps cost issue but doesn't insulate other one-sided terms.

### 2. McGill Public Injunctive Relief — *McGill v. Citibank, N.A.* (2017) 2 Cal.5th 945
**Holding:** Arbitration provision waiving the right to seek public injunctive relief in **any** forum is invalid as a matter of California law and **not preempted** by the FAA. Confirmed: *Blair v. Rent-A-Center* (9th Cir. 2019) 928 F.3d 819.

**Application:** Plead UCL/CLRA public injunctive relief (enjoin Sunrun's "system issue" template statewide, mandate disclosure protocol). If arbitration clause forecloses that, the *waiver* is void, severable, and the public-injunction claim proceeds in court while individual claims arbitrate.

### 3. Cal. Code Civ. Proc. § 1281.97 (Effective 2020)
If drafting party (Sunrun) fails to pay arbitration fees within 30 days of due date, consumer may withdraw and proceed in court. **Practical leverage** — Sunrun has a documented history (per public dockets) of fee-payment delays.

### 4. Small-Claims Carve-Out — Most PPA Arb Clauses
Most JAMS consumer clauses (and the AAA model) preserve the right to file in small claims as long as the dispute stays in small claims. Cal. Code Civ. Proc. § 116.220 jurisdictional limit = **$12,500 for natural persons** (raised eff. Jan 1, 2024). Direct out-of-pocket damages (~$5–10K) sit below this. Risk: Sunrun can remove to arbitration if homeowner appeals adverse small-claims judgment to superior court (which triggers de novo trial, not arbitration in most clauses — verify the 2012 PPA § 16 language).

### 5. Class-Action Waiver
Post-*Concepcion* enforceable in CA. Don't plead class. Plead individual + public injunction (McGill).

## Bottom Line for This Matter
- Individual damages claims (CLRA, fraud, breach) **will go to JAMS arbitration**.
- Small claims is viable as an alternative for direct economic damages.
- **Public injunctive relief survives in court under McGill.**
- Arbitration is *not* a deal-killer; CLRA fees and § 3294 punitives are still available in JAMS, and Sunrun's institutional repeat-player exposure (multiple awards = adverse precedent) is strong settlement leverage.

## Risks
- *Viking River Cruises v. Moriana* (2022) 596 U.S. 639 — narrow PAGA carve-out, not relevant here.
- Severability clause in PPA § 16 may save the arbitration clause even if class waiver/injunction waiver is voided.
