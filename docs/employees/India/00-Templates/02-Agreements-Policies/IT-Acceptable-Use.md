# IT ACCEPTABLE USE POLICY — INDIA

**Policy Owner:** Head of IT / CISO (in consultation with HR Head – India)
**Effective Date:** <Date>
**Version:** 2.0
**Applies to:** All employees, contractors, consultants, interns, and any third parties accessing Technijian IT Services Pvt. Ltd. ("Company") IT systems, networks, devices, applications, or data.

---

## 1. PURPOSE AND SCOPE

**1.01** This Policy sets out the rules for the use, protection, and monitoring of Company IT systems, networks, devices, applications, and data (collectively, **"Company IT Assets"**).

**1.02** The Policy is designed to safeguard the Company and its clients against unauthorized access, data loss, cyber threats, and liability under applicable laws, including:

(a) the **Information Technology Act, 2000** ("IT Act") and the rules framed thereunder, including the Information Technology (Reasonable Security Practices and Procedures and Sensitive Personal Data or Information) Rules, 2011 ("SPDI Rules");
(b) the **Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023** ("DPDP Act"), upon notification and enforcement;
(c) the Indian Penal Code, 1860 and the Indian Contract Act, 1872 (including Section 72A IT Act read with contractual confidentiality obligations);
(d) client contractual obligations, including data-protection addenda, Business Associate Agreements (HIPAA), and SOC 2 obligations, where applicable.

**1.03** This Policy is read together with the Employee's Non-Disclosure Agreement, Code of Conduct, and the Employee Handbook.

## 2. DEFINITIONS

**2.01** **"Confidential Information"** has the meaning given in the Employee's Non-Disclosure Agreement.

**2.02** **"Personal Data"** means any data about a natural person that is capable of identifying that person, as further defined in the DPDP Act and the SPDI Rules.

**2.03** **"Sensitive Personal Data or Information"** has the meaning given in Rule 3 of the SPDI Rules (including passwords, financial information, health conditions, sexual orientation, medical records, biometric information).

## 3. AUTHORISED USE

**3.01** Company IT Assets are provided **primarily for business purposes**.

**3.02** **Limited and reasonable personal use** of Company IT Assets (including occasional personal email and browsing) is permitted, subject to the conditions of this Policy and provided that such use does not:

(a) interfere with work duties;
(b) consume excessive resources;
(c) violate any other Company policy;
(d) expose the Company to legal, regulatory, reputational, or security risk.

## 4. PROHIBITED USE

**4.01** The Employee shall **not**:

(a) share, disclose, or reuse passwords, MFA codes, access tokens, encryption keys, or any other credentials;
(b) install, download, or use any software, tool, browser extension, or AI agent on Company IT Assets without prior written approval from IT;
(c) access, store, transmit, or distribute content that is illegal, obscene, defamatory, discriminatory, or harassing, including pornography, hate speech, or content prohibited under Section 67, 67A, 67B of the IT Act;
(d) use personal email, personal cloud storage, or personal messaging services (including but not limited to Gmail, Yahoo Mail, Outlook personal accounts, Google Drive, Dropbox, iCloud, personal OneDrive, WhatsApp Web/Desktop, Telegram, Signal, or personal GitHub accounts) to store, transmit, or process Company or client data;
(e) connect personal devices (laptops, USBs, external drives, personal mobile phones) to the Company network or to any Company endpoint without written approval from IT and without the required endpoint controls being installed;
(f) bypass or disable security controls, including VPN, MFA, endpoint protection (antivirus / EDR), data loss prevention (DLP), firewall rules, or web filtering;
(g) engage in any activity prohibited under **Section 43 of the IT Act** (including but not limited to unauthorized access to a computer or computer system; download, copy, or extraction of data; introduction of viruses or contaminants; denial of access; disruption; tampering; charging services of another; destroying, deleting or altering information; stealing, concealing, destroying or altering source code);
(h) engage in any activity prohibited under **Section 65 of the IT Act** (tampering with computer source documents) or **Section 66 of the IT Act** (computer-related offenses, including hacking with dishonest or fraudulent intent);
(i) disclose Confidential Information or Personal Data in breach of **Section 72 of the IT Act** (breach of confidentiality and privacy) or **Section 72A of the IT Act** (disclosure of information in breach of lawful contract, which carries imprisonment up to 3 years and/or fine up to Rs. 5,00,000);
(j) use Company IT Assets or Company data for personal gain, including for any outside employment, consultancy, or business activity (see the Non-Compete and Non-Solicitation Agreement for moonlighting restrictions);
(k) share Company IT Assets with family members or any unauthorised person;
(l) use Company IT Assets to solicit, canvass, or advertise for political or commercial purposes unrelated to the Company.

## 5. SECURITY REQUIREMENTS

**5.01** The Employee shall:

(a) enable and use **Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)** on every Company account capable of supporting it;
(b) **lock the screen** when away from the workstation (Windows key + L, or equivalent);
(c) use only **Company-issued devices** or explicitly Company-approved devices for Company work;
(d) **encrypt** all Company-issued laptops (BitLocker / FileVault) and store data only in Company-approved storage locations;
(e) keep devices **patched and up to date** as instructed by IT;
(f) not bypass automatic software updates;
(g) report any **suspected phishing, malware, lost or stolen device, or security incident** immediately to IT (security@technijian.com) and to HR (hr-india@technijian.com);
(h) use only Company-approved communication, collaboration, and file-sharing tools (e.g., the Company's Microsoft 365 tenant, the Company-issued chat platform, and the Company GitHub organisation);
(i) respect least-privilege principles and not request or retain access to data that is not needed for the Employee's role;
(j) handle Sensitive Personal Data or Information strictly in accordance with the SPDI Rules and the DPDP Act, including applying the data-classification rules set out by the Company's CISO.

## 6. EMAIL, MESSAGING, AND SOCIAL MEDIA

**6.01** Company email and messaging accounts may be used only in a manner consistent with Company policies, brand guidelines, and professional standards. Auto-forwarding to personal accounts is prohibited.

**6.02** Employees shall not publish Company confidential information, client names, contract terms, or other non-public information on social media, forums, code repositories (including public GitHub/StackOverflow pastes), or public AI services.

**6.03** Use of public generative-AI tools (including, but not limited to, ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, Copilot) with Company or client data is permitted **only** via Company-approved enterprise tenants with data-processing protections in place. Free consumer tiers of generative-AI tools must not be used with any Company or client data.

## 7. MONITORING AND EMPLOYEE CONSENT

**7.01** The Company monitors Company IT Assets and activity thereon, including but not limited to: network traffic, internet browsing, email metadata and content, file access, endpoint activity, application usage, login and location data, and system logs, for the purposes of:

(a) cybersecurity, incident response, and threat detection;
(b) compliance with applicable law, including the IT Act, the DPDP Act, and client contractual obligations;
(c) audit, investigation, and legal proceedings;
(d) operational management, capacity planning, and productivity oversight;
(e) protection of Confidential Information and intellectual property.

**7.02** By using Company IT Assets, the Employee **acknowledges** the Company's monitoring practices and **consents** to such monitoring as a condition of access. This consent is informed, specific, and given freely as part of the employment relationship, and extends to any Personal Data of the Employee processed through Company IT Assets.

**7.03** In line with the DPDP Act (upon notification), the Company will:

(a) provide a **Privacy Notice** describing the categories of Employee Personal Data processed, purposes of processing, lawful basis, recipients, retention periods, and Employee rights;
(b) process Employee Personal Data only for specified, lawful, and legitimate purposes;
(c) implement reasonable security safeguards;
(d) honour Employee rights under the DPDP Act (including access, correction, and grievance redressal) subject to statutory limitations.

**7.04** The Employee shall have no reasonable expectation of privacy in respect of use of Company IT Assets. Personal data on Company IT Assets may be accessed by the Company to the minimum extent necessary for the purposes set out in Clause 7.01.

## 8. USE OF PERSONAL DEVICES (BYOD)

**8.01** Where the Employee is permitted to use a personal device for Company work (e.g., personal mobile phone for Company email):

(a) the Employee must install the Company's mobile device management (MDM) profile;
(b) the device must be passcode-protected and encrypted;
(c) the Company may remotely wipe Company data (and only Company data, through containerisation) from the device at the end of employment or on loss/theft;
(d) the Employee shall not store Company data outside the Company-managed container or Company-approved applications.

## 9. SEPARATION AND RETURN OF ASSETS

**9.01** On separation (resignation, termination, retirement, or otherwise), the Employee shall:

(a) return all Company-issued devices, access cards, and credentials;
(b) delete all Company data from any personal device or personal account;
(c) not retain, download, or transmit Company data to any personal or third-party storage;
(d) co-operate with the Company's handover and wipe procedures.

**9.02** Following separation, all access to Company IT Assets will be revoked; any attempt to access Company IT Assets post-separation constitutes unauthorized access under Section 43 of the IT Act and will be treated accordingly.

## 10. CONSEQUENCES OF BREACH

**10.01** Violation of this Policy may result in:

(a) disciplinary action up to and including **termination for cause**;
(b) recovery of damages, including legal costs on a full-indemnity basis;
(c) withholding of full-and-final settlement amounts pending investigation;
(d) civil or criminal prosecution under the IT Act, the DPDP Act, the Indian Penal Code, or other applicable law;
(e) reporting to clients and regulators, where required by contract or law.

## 11. POLICY REVIEW

**11.01** This Policy will be reviewed at least once every twelve (12) months by the Head of IT / CISO in consultation with the HR Head – India, and updated in line with changes in applicable law (including DPDP Act rulemaking), client contractual requirements, and Company practice.
