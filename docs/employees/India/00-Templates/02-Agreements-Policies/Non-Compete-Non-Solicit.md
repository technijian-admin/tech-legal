# NON-COMPETE (IN-SERVICE) AND NON-SOLICITATION AGREEMENT

> **Important Legal Note — Section 27 of the Indian Contract Act, 1872**
>
> Under Section 27 of the Indian Contract Act, 1872, every agreement by which any person is restrained from exercising a lawful profession, trade, or business of any kind is, to that extent, **void**. Indian courts have consistently held that **post-employment non-compete covenants are unenforceable**. However, (a) **in-service non-compete** obligations (during the subsistence of the employment contract), (b) **non-solicitation of employees and clients**, and (c) **reasonable confidentiality / IP assignment** obligations continue to be enforceable, as affirmed by the Supreme Court in *Niranjan Shankar Golikari v. Century Spinning & Mfg. Co. Ltd.* (AIR 1967 SC 1098) and subsequent Indian jurisprudence.
>
> This Agreement is drafted to operate within these enforceable boundaries.

## 1. DEFINITIONS

**1.01** **"Competing Business"** means any business, service, or activity that is substantially similar to, or directly competes with, any service or business then being provided or being developed by the Company or its affiliates, including managed IT services, cybersecurity services, cloud services, helpdesk and support services, IT consulting, and staff augmentation.

**1.02** **"Restricted Clients"** means (a) clients, customers, and prospects of the Company or its affiliates to whom the Employee provided services or had material access to, or (b) with whom the Employee had material dealings, in each case during the **twelve (12) months** immediately preceding the Separation Date.

**1.03** **"Restricted Personnel"** means any person employed or engaged by the Company or its affiliates during the **twelve (12) months** immediately preceding the Separation Date (including employees, contractors, consultants, interns, and retainers).

**1.04** **"Separation Date"** means the last working day of the Employee with the Company, for any reason (resignation, termination, retirement, non-renewal, or otherwise).

## 2. IN-SERVICE NON-COMPETE (DURING EMPLOYMENT)

**2.01** During the subsistence of the Employee's employment with the Company, the Employee shall not, directly or indirectly, whether as employee, consultant, advisor, partner, director, agent, shareholder (other than as a passive investor holding less than 2% of the equity of a listed company), or in any other capacity:

(a) engage in any Competing Business;
(b) provide services to any client, prospect, vendor, or competitor of the Company without the prior written consent of the Company; or
(c) undertake any secondary or outside employment ("moonlighting"), paid or unpaid, without the prior written consent of the Company.

**2.02** The Employee acknowledges that the restrictions in Clause 2.01 are reasonable, are necessary to protect the Company's legitimate business interests, and do not extend beyond the term of employment.

## 3. NON-SOLICITATION OF EMPLOYEES AND CONTRACTORS (12 MONTHS POST-SEPARATION)

**3.01** For a period of **twelve (12) months** from the Separation Date, the Employee shall not, directly or indirectly, whether alone or jointly with or through any other person or entity:

(a) solicit, induce, entice, or encourage any Restricted Personnel to leave their employment or engagement with the Company;
(b) hire, employ, engage, or offer to hire, employ, or engage any Restricted Personnel; or
(c) interfere with or disrupt the Company's relationship with any Restricted Personnel.

**3.02** The restriction in Clause 3.01(b) shall not apply to a general advertisement of employment not specifically directed at Restricted Personnel and to which a Restricted Person responds unsolicited.

## 4. NON-SOLICITATION OF CLIENTS (12 MONTHS POST-SEPARATION)

**4.01** For a period of **twelve (12) months** from the Separation Date, the Employee shall not, directly or indirectly, whether alone or jointly with or through any other person or entity:

(a) solicit, canvass, or approach any Restricted Client for the purpose of providing services which are the same as, or substantially similar to, services then provided by the Company;
(b) interfere with, or attempt to divert, any business between the Company and any Restricted Client; or
(c) accept business from any Restricted Client in respect of services which are the same as, or substantially similar to, services then provided by the Company, where such business was solicited in breach of Clause 4.01(a).

**4.02** The restrictions in Clause 4.01 are limited to Restricted Clients and to the specific services that compete with the Company's services. They are not, and shall not be construed as, a post-employment non-compete covenant.

## 5. NON-USE OF CONFIDENTIAL INFORMATION

**5.01** The Employee shall not, during the term of employment or at any time thereafter, use, disclose, or exploit any Confidential Information of the Company or its clients to compete with the Company, to solicit any Restricted Client, or to solicit any Restricted Personnel.
