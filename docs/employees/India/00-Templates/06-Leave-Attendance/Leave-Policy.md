**Technijian IT Services Pvt. Ltd.** | Leave Policy

---

## 1. Purpose & Applicability

This policy governs all forms of leave and time-off available to employees of Technijian IT Services Pvt. Ltd. ("the Company"), registered in Panchkula, Haryana. It is framed in conformity with:

- **Punjab Shops & Commercial Establishments Act, 1958** (as extended to and in force in the State of Haryana) — governing statutory Earned Leave, weekly rest, working-hour limits
- **Maternity Benefit Act, 1961** (as amended by the Maternity Benefit (Amendment) Act, 2017) — governing maternity, adoption/commissioning leave, creche, and work-from-home rights
- **Industrial Employment (Standing Orders) Act, 1946** — where applicable
- **Code on Wages, 2019** and **Code on Social Security, 2020** — applicable subject to notification of enforcement dates by the Central / Haryana State Government (pending as of date of this policy)

This policy applies to all regular full-time employees — on probation, confirmed, and fixed-term — of the Company in India.

## 2. Leave Year

The leave year is the **calendar year**, from **1 January to 31 December**.

## 3. Types of Leave

All day-based entitlements assume the Company standard working week of Monday to Friday, 9 hours/day including 1-hour lunch.

| # | Type | Entitlement | Accrual | Carry-Forward | Encashment |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 3.01 | Earned / Privilege Leave (EL) | 18 working days / year | 1.5 days / completed month of service | Up to 30 days accumulated | Yes — on separation, at Basic salary, per Shops & Est. Act |
| 3.02 | Casual Leave (CL) | 7 days / year | On confirmation of the leave year | Nil — lapses 31 December | Nil |
| 3.03 | Sick Leave (SL) | 7 days / year | On confirmation of the leave year | Up to 30 days accumulated | Nil |
| 3.04 | Maternity Leave | 26 weeks (paid) for first 2 surviving children; 12 weeks for 3rd and subsequent | As per Maternity Benefit Act, 1961, Sec. 5 | n/a | n/a |
| 3.05 | Adoption / Commissioning Mother Leave | 12 weeks (paid) from date of handover / surrogacy | As per Maternity Benefit (Amendment) Act, 2017 | n/a | n/a |
| 3.06 | Paternity Leave (policy) | 5 working days, within 3 months of child's birth | Per event, up to 2 children | Nil | Nil |
| 3.07 | Bereavement Leave (policy) | 3 working days per occurrence (immediate family) | Per event | Nil | Nil |
| 3.08 | Marriage Leave (policy) | 5 working days, once during service | One-time | Nil | Nil |
| 3.09 | Public / National Holidays | As per Haryana State Gazette + Company annual HR calendar | Fixed list issued each January | n/a | n/a |
| 3.10 | Compensatory Off (Comp-Off) | 1 day per full day of approved weekend / public-holiday work | Manager-approved, logged in HR system | Must be availed within 90 days of earning; otherwise lapses | Nil |

Statutory note: The Company's EL entitlement of 18 days is more generous than the statutory floor under the Punjab S&E Act (1 day for every 20 days worked, typically ~15 days/year). Statutory floor is the minimum; Company policy overrides only upward.

There is no statutory entitlement under Indian law to paternity, bereavement, marriage, or general sick leave — items 3.06, 3.07, 3.08 and SL at 3.03 are extended as a matter of Company policy and may be revised at the Company's discretion with reasonable notice.

## 4. Accrual & Pro-ration

### 4.01 Mid-year Joiners

EL, CL, and SL entitlements for an employee joining mid-year are pro-rated by completed months of service in the leave year, rounded to the nearest half-day.

Formula: `Entitlement = Annual Quota × (Months remaining in leave year ÷ 12)`

### 4.02 Confirmation Dependency

- EL accrues from date of joining for all employees.
- CL and SL become available from date of joining but are subject to pro-ration as above.
- Maternity and Adoption/Commissioning leave are governed by the eligibility rules of the Maternity Benefit Act, 1961 (minimum 80 days of service in the 12 months preceding the expected date of delivery).

### 4.03 Leave Without Pay (LOP)

Where paid leave balance is insufficient, the remainder may be availed as Loss of Pay (LOP) with prior approval of the reporting manager and HR. LOP days will be deducted at `(Monthly Gross ÷ Working Days of the month)`.

## 5. Maternity Benefits — Additional Provisions

Pursuant to the Maternity Benefit Act, 1961 and the Maternity Benefit (Amendment) Act, 2017:

### 5.01 Medical Bonus

Rs. 3,500 medical bonus is payable where pre-natal confinement and post-natal care are not provided by the Company free of charge.

### 5.02 Creche Facility

The Company will provide and maintain a creche facility within a prescribed distance once the employee headcount reaches or exceeds **50 employees**, as required under Sec. 11A. Women employees are permitted up to 4 visits per day to the creche, including rest intervals.

### 5.03 Work-From-Home (Post-Maternity)

Pursuant to Sec. 5(5) of the Maternity Benefit Act, a woman employee may, by mutual agreement with the Company, avail work-from-home after expiry of maternity leave where the nature of her role permits. Refer to the Company WFH Policy.

### 5.04 Miscarriage / Medical Termination of Pregnancy

6 weeks of paid leave immediately following the date of miscarriage / MTP, on production of medical certificate (Sec. 9).

### 5.05 Tubectomy

2 weeks of paid leave immediately following the date of tubectomy operation (Sec. 9A).

### 5.06 Nursing Breaks

Until the child attains 15 months, two nursing breaks per working day in addition to the usual rest interval.

## 6. Application Process

| Leave Type | Advance Notice | Documentation |
|---|---|---|
| EL | At least 7 working days before start; block EL (>5 days) 15 days' notice | HR system leave request |
| CL | At least 48 hours in advance | HR system leave request |
| SL | Same day notification to manager before shift start; retrospective application on return | Medical certificate for >2 consecutive days |
| Maternity | At least 8 weeks before expected leave start | Medical certificate / letter from qualified practitioner |
| Adoption | As soon as handover date is known | Adoption order / surrogacy agreement |
| Paternity | At least 7 days' notice where foreseeable | Birth certificate (submitted post-facto) |
| Bereavement | Immediate intimation to manager | Nil required |
| Marriage | At least 15 days' notice | Wedding invitation |
| Comp-Off | Prior manager approval to work on off-day; availment via HR system | Approval trail |

All leave requires reporting-manager approval, except statutory Maternity, Miscarriage, and Tubectomy leave under the Maternity Benefit Act, which are a matter of right subject to eligibility and notice.

## 7. Unapproved Absence

### 7.01

Absence of **3 (three) or more consecutive working days** without prior approval or credible communication to the reporting manager / HR shall constitute unauthorized absence.

### 7.02

Unauthorized absence will be treated as misconduct under the Disciplinary policy and will invite a **Show Cause Notice**, after which the matter may escalate to Charge Sheet, Inquiry, and termination for cause as warranted under the Industrial Employment (Standing Orders) Act, 1946 and the Company's Disciplinary policy.

### 7.03

Absence of **7 (seven) or more consecutive working days** without intimation may, at the Company's discretion, be treated as voluntary abandonment of service.

## 8. Encashment on Separation

### 8.01

On cessation of employment (resignation, retirement, termination other than for cause involving moral turpitude), accumulated EL up to the cap of 30 days is encashed and paid in the Full & Final settlement.

### 8.02

Encashment computation:

`Encashment = (Basic Salary per month ÷ 26) × Unused EL days (capped at 30)`

This formula uses the 26-day divisor consistent with the method prescribed under the Payment of Gratuity Act, 1972 and widely applied under State Shops & Establishments rules.

### 8.03

CL and SL are **not** encashable.

### 8.04

Maternity / statutory leave, if interrupted by separation, is handled per Maternity Benefit Act provisions.

## 9. Combining Leaves

- EL, CL, and SL may be combined with weekly off / public holidays; intervening holidays within a leave block shall not be deducted from the leave account.
- CL and SL may not be availed for more than 5 continuous working days each without manager and HR approval.
- EL and CL may not be combined in a manner that converts CL into a block longer than its 7-day annual limit.

## 10. Public Holidays

The Company will publish, by 15 January of each leave year, the list of observed public holidays (national, State of Haryana, and optional holidays permitted by gazette). This list forms part of this policy by reference.
