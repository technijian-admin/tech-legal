**Technijian IT Services Pvt. Ltd.** | Work From Home (WFH) Policy

---

## 1. Purpose & Scope

This policy establishes the framework under which employees of Technijian IT Services Pvt. Ltd. ("the Company") may perform their duties from a location other than the Company office at [Panchkula, Haryana]. This policy applies to all employees in India. It is framed in consonance with:

- **Information Technology Act, 2000** — data protection, electronic records, reasonable security practices
- **Maternity Benefit (Amendment) Act, 2017, Sec. 5(5)** — work-from-home after maternity leave where role permits
- **Punjab Shops & Commercial Establishments Act, 1958** (as extended to and in force in Haryana) — working-hour limits, weekly rest

## 2. WFH Modes

| # | Mode | Description | Approval |
|---|---|---|---|
| 2.01 | Hybrid | Up to 2 WFH days per working week | Reporting manager |
| 2.02 | Full Remote | Employee works entirely from a designated remote location | HR + Business Head + Director |
| 2.03 | Occasional | Short-term WFH for medical, family, commute, or weather reasons (up to 10 working days per event) | Reporting manager; HR for >5 consecutive days |
| 2.04 | Post-Maternity | Pursuant to Maternity Benefit Act Sec. 5(5), by mutual agreement after maternity leave, for as long as nature of work permits | HR + Reporting manager |

## 3. Eligibility

- Confirmed employees (post-probation) are eligible for Hybrid and Occasional WFH by default.
- Employees on probation may be permitted Occasional WFH with reporting-manager approval.
- Full Remote is granted exceptionally based on business justification and role suitability.
- Eligibility may be withdrawn for performance, disciplinary, or security reasons on reasonable notice.

## 4. Working Hours & Availability

### 4.01

Employees on WFH shall observe the Company working hours applicable to their role / shift (typically Monday to Friday, 9 hours/day including 1 hour lunch), in compliance with the weekly working-hour limit under the Punjab Shops & Commercial Establishments Act, 1958.

### 4.02

Availability on Company messaging, email, and video-conferencing platforms during working hours is mandatory. Prolonged unavailability without prior intimation will be treated on par with unauthorized absence under the Leave Policy.

### 4.03

Attendance shall be marked through the designated HR system / attendance tool on each WFH day. Missed punches shall be regularized via the **Attendance Regularization Form**.

## 5. Workspace & Connectivity Requirements

- Quiet, private workspace free from avoidable interruption; confidential calls must not be overheard by household members or visitors.
- Reliable broadband internet with a minimum of 25 Mbps download speed.
- Power backup sufficient to complete the working day.
- Ergonomic arrangement for workstation, chair, and monitor as far as reasonably practicable.

## 6. Security & Information Protection

WFH is subject to the Company's **IT Acceptable Use Policy**, **Confidentiality / NDA**, and this section. In particular:

### 6.01 Devices

- Employees shall use only the Company-issued laptop / device for performance of their duties. Use of personal devices is prohibited unless expressly authorized in writing.
- Hard-disk encryption, anti-virus / EDR agent, and patch-management agent must remain enabled at all times.

### 6.02 Network

- Connection to Company or client systems must be made only through the Company-approved VPN / zero-trust client.
- Public Wi-Fi (airports, cafes, hotels) shall not be used without active VPN and MFA.
- Home Wi-Fi must be WPA2/WPA3 secured with a strong password; default router credentials must be changed.

### 6.03 Authentication

Multi-factor authentication (MFA) is mandatory for all Company and client systems. Sharing of credentials is prohibited.

### 6.04 Screen & Printed Data

- Screens must be positioned to prevent viewing by household members or visitors, particularly for client work subject to confidentiality obligations.
- Printing of client or Company data is prohibited except where expressly required; printed material must be shredded upon use.

### 6.05 Physical Security

- The laptop, peripherals, and any physical data shall be kept in a secure location when not in use.
- Loss, theft, or suspected compromise of a Company device or credentials must be reported to IT and HR within 24 hours.

### 6.06 Incident Reporting

Any suspected data breach, phishing incident, or security event must be reported immediately per the IT Acceptable Use Policy and the Information-Security Incident Response plan.

## 7. Performance & Deliverables

WFH is a mode of work, not a relaxation of deliverables. Expected output, response times, and quality standards remain identical to office-based work. Deterioration in performance traceable to WFH will be grounds for revoking the WFH arrangement.

## 8. Client & Regulatory Constraints

Certain client engagements, data-classification levels, or regulated workloads (e.g., PII / PHI / PCI data) may require on-premises work. Where a client contract or an applicable law / regulation prohibits WFH for particular work, the on-site requirement supersedes this policy for that scope.

## 9. Non-Compliance

| # | Conduct | Consequence |
|---|---|---|
| 9.01 | First instance of missed deliverables, availability lapse, or procedural breach during WFH | Verbal warning by reporting manager; written caution |
| 9.02 | Repeated lapses | Revocation of WFH privileges; return to full on-site work |
| 9.03 | Security violation (credential sharing, VPN bypass, unauthorized device, data leakage) | Immediate revocation + Show Cause Notice + Disciplinary proceedings under the Standing Orders / Disciplinary policy |
| 9.04 | Data breach attributable to gross negligence or wilful misconduct | Termination for cause + recovery of damages as permitted by law |
