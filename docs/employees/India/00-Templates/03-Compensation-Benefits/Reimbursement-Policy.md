# Expense Reimbursement Policy — India

**Policy Owner:** Head of HR, India
**Approver:** Director, Technijian IT Services Pvt. Ltd.
**Effective Date:** __________
**Version:** 1.0
**Review Cycle:** Annual

## 1. Purpose

1.1 This Policy governs the reimbursement of business-related expenses incurred by employees of Technijian IT Services Pvt. Ltd. (the "**Company**") in the bona fide course of their duties, in compliance with the Payment of Wages Act, 1936, the Income-tax Act, 1961, and the Central Goods and Services Tax Act, 2017 ("**CGST Act**").

## 2. Scope

2.1 Applies to all employees (confirmed, on probation, contract staff and interns) of the Company working from the India office or on assigned travel.
2.2 Does **not** apply to fixed allowances forming part of CTC (HRA, Special Allowance, etc.), which are paid through payroll.

## 3. Governing Principles

3.1 Only **bona fide, reasonable, and business-necessary** expenses are reimbursable.
3.2 Every claim must be supported by a **valid tax invoice** (with supplier GSTIN, recipient name "Technijian IT Services Pvt. Ltd." and Company GSTIN where required), or an original printed/digital receipt.
3.3 Claims must be submitted within **thirty (30) days** of incurrence. Claims older than 60 days will not be entertained except with written approval of the Director.
3.4 Employees are expected to prefer the most economical option consistent with business needs and personal safety.

## 5. Submission Process

5.1 Raise the claim in the Company expense system within thirty (30) days of incurrence.
5.2 Attach scanned/digital copies of all original tax invoices and receipts. Retain physicals until reimbursement is credited.
5.3 Manager approves within three (3) working days; Finance disburses with the next payroll cycle.
5.4 Where the Company is entitled to claim **GST input tax credit** under Sections 16 and 17 of the CGST Act, the tax invoice must bear the Company's correct GSTIN, legal name and registered address. Claims lacking compliant GST documentation may be reimbursed without the GST component.

## 6. Prohibited Expenses

6.1 Personal expenses (including personal laundry, movies, in-room entertainment, spa, gym).
6.2 Alcohol, except client business meals **pre-approved in writing** by the reporting manager and, in all cases, subject to local legality.
6.3 Gifts to clients, vendors, public officials or their family members **without prior written Compliance approval**. All gifts must comply with the Company's Anti-Bribery & Anti-Corruption standards and the Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988.
6.4 Payments in cash where a compliant electronic channel and tax invoice are available. Claims involving cash payments above limits notified under the Income-tax Act may be disallowed.
6.5 Expenses related to a spouse, family member or non-employee travelling with the employee, unless specifically pre-approved.

## 7. Non-Compliance and Fraudulent Claims

7.1 Submission of **false, inflated, or duplicate** claims, or claims with fabricated invoices, constitutes grave misconduct.
7.2 Consequences include recovery of the amount, disciplinary action under the Company's disciplinary framework and the Industrial Employment (Standing Orders) Act, 1946 (as applicable), and may include **termination for cause**. The Company may, where warranted, initiate civil and criminal proceedings.

## 8. Records

8.1 Reimbursement records are retained for a minimum of **eight (8) years** in line with record-keeping requirements under the Income-tax Act, 1961 and the CGST Act, 2017.

## 9. Review and Amendment

9.1 The Company reserves the right to amend this Policy, category limits, and approval hierarchy from time to time. Changes will be communicated via the Employee Handbook and HR notice.
