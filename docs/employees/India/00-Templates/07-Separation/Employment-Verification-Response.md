## Drafting Notes (delete before issuance)

**When to use:**

1. A prospective employer / background-verification agency writes asking to confirm employment of a current or former employee.
2. A bank, lender, or government department writes seeking employment verification (often alongside a Salary Certificate request).
3. A consulate / embassy seeks verification for a visa application.

**Critical compliance points:**

- **Never respond to an unsolicited phone call** with employment details. Require a written request on the Verifier's letterhead with their direct contact details.
- **Always check for written consent** from the Subject Employee for any data point in §2. Verbal consent is insufficient under the DPDP Act, 2023.
- **Be consistent across requests.** Inconsistent verification responses (e.g., "by mutual agreement" to one verifier and "for cause" to another) for the same separation are a tribunal red flag and a defamation risk.
- **Never share the reason for a disciplinary action** unless: (a) the Subject Employee has consented, AND (b) Counsel has reviewed. Risk of defamation under §499/500 IPC and tortious interference with prospective business advantage.
- **For ex-employees who left under §70 retrenchment:** state "by mutual cost-reduction retrenchment per §70 of the IR Code, 2020" if asked about reason — never volunteer "performance" or "redundancy" framing without sub-employee consent.
- **For ex-employees terminated for cause:** disclose only with explicit consent OR if a public regulator (e.g., SEBI, ICAI for fraud) requires it. Otherwise decline politely citing the data-protection policy.

**Pre-issuance gates:**

- [ ] Written verification request on Verifier letterhead received and filed
- [ ] Subject Employee written consent received and filed (where any §2 disclosure)
- [ ] Cross-check current/former employment record + dates against payroll
- [ ] Cross-check separation mode against the Confirmation register and exit documents
- [ ] HR Head sign-off
- [ ] File response in employee folder + Verifier-correspondence log
