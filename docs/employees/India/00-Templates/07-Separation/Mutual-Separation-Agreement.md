## Drafting Notes (delete before issuance)

**When to use this template:**

1. **Negotiated EL release** — when an employee with a large EL balance (e.g., Rahul Uniyal's 89-day balance) is amenable to a discounted F&F in exchange for a clean release. Draft this Agreement to embed the discounted EL number into §2.01(c) and the offsetting ex-gratia in §2.01(g), with the §4.01 release executing immediately on payment.
2. **High-risk separations** — for confirmed long-tenure or sensitive employees (female + post-maternity, senior leadership, mid-disciplinary), where a unilateral §70 retrenchment carries tribunal-claim risk. The mutual-release framing converts retrenchment exposure into a contractual settlement.
3. **Voluntary exits with severance** — when the Company offers severance over and above statutory dues to incentivize an amicable exit.
4. **Inquiry-pending exits** — where a Show Cause / Charge Sheet is in flight but both Parties prefer a clean exit to a contested disciplinary outcome. Coordinate the inquiry-withdrawal terms in §4.01(d) and §8.

**Critical legal points:**

- Tribunals scrutinise releases that waive statutory minimums, particularly for low-paid workers and where the employee did not have access to independent counsel. The **§11.01 voluntariness acknowledgment** and the **7-day consideration window** are the most important defences against a future challenge.
- The ex-gratia in §2.01(g) **must be substantive** — minimum 25-50% above the statutory floor — for the release in §4.01 to be defensible. A token ex-gratia of ₹1 will not survive.
- If the Employee is a "workman" under §2(zr) IR Code, ensure §2.01(f) (statutory retrenchment compensation) is fully paid as a separate line item — releases of statutory retrenchment dues without payment are void.
- POSH carve-out in §4.01(d) is non-negotiable — pending sexual-harassment complaints continue regardless of any private settlement.
- This Agreement DOES NOT replace the Relieving Letter or Experience Letter — issue those separately on the Separation Date so the Employee has the standard exit documentation.
