**2.00 Order of Suspension**

2.01 Pending completion of a domestic inquiry into the allegations of misconduct leveled against you, you are hereby placed **UNDER SUSPENSION** with effect from the close of business on <DD Month YYYY>.

2.02 **This suspension is NOT a punishment.** It is a precautionary administrative measure intended to preserve the integrity of the Company's records, systems, witnesses, and workplace pending the outcome of the domestic inquiry, and shall not be treated as a finding of guilt.

**3.00 Conditions During Suspension**

3.01 You shall **not** enter the Company's premises except as expressly required by the Inquiry Officer or HR, and only upon prior written permission.

3.02 You shall **not** access Company systems, email, cloud drives, code repositories, client data, or any other Company confidential information. Your credentials will be disabled with effect from the date of this Order.

3.03 You shall remain **available and reachable** at the address, email, and mobile number on record, and respond to any communication from HR or the Inquiry Officer **within 24 (twenty-four) hours**.

3.04 You shall not represent yourself as an active employee of the Company or engage any client, vendor, or third party on the Company's behalf.

3.05 You shall not approach, contact, or attempt to influence, directly or indirectly, any witness, complainant, or fellow employee in connection with the subject matter of the inquiry. Any such conduct shall itself constitute a separate act of grave misconduct.

3.06 You shall surrender to HR, on or before <DD Month YYYY>, <HH:MM hrs>, all Company property in your possession, including without limitation: identity card, access card, laptop and charger, mobile device (if issued), SIM card (if Company-funded), data cards, hard-copy documents, keys, and any other items issued to you.

**4.00 Subsistence Allowance**

4.01 During the period of suspension, you shall be paid a **subsistence allowance** computed on the following basis, consistent with the common-law standard applied by Indian courts (*Hotel Imperial* line of authorities) and the spirit of the Industrial Employment (Standing Orders) Central Rules, 1946 (Schedule I-B), and as may be varied by any certified Standing Orders specific to the Company and the Punjab Shops and Commercial Establishments Act, 1958 (as applicable in Haryana / Panchkula):

    4.01.1 **First 90 (ninety) days of suspension:** 50% of Basic Wages + applicable dearness allowance (if any).
    4.01.2 **Beyond 90 days, up to 180 days:** 75% of Basic Wages + applicable dearness allowance, provided that the delay in completion of inquiry is not attributable to your conduct.
    4.01.3 **Beyond 180 days:** Rate to be reviewed by the Disciplinary Authority, taking into account the reason for delay.

4.02 If the delay in conclusion of the inquiry is attributable to you (e.g., non-appearance, non-cooperation, seeking repeated adjournments), the subsistence allowance may be restricted to 50% throughout, at the Company's discretion.

4.03 Statutory deductions (EPF under the Employees' Provident Funds and Miscellaneous Provisions Act, 1952; ESI under the Employees' State Insurance Act, 1948 where applicable; TDS under the Income-tax Act, 1961; Professional Tax; Labour Welfare Fund under the Haryana Labour Welfare Fund Act, 1981) shall continue to be deducted and remitted on the subsistence allowance.

4.04 Group health, EPF, and ESI cover shall continue during suspension, subject to the Company's policy and applicable law.

**5.00 Duration and Review**

5.01 The suspension shall remain in force until conclusion of the domestic inquiry and communication of the final Order by the Disciplinary Authority.

5.02 The suspension order shall be **reviewed every 90 (ninety) days** by the Disciplinary Authority to assess whether it continues to be necessary.

**6.00 Outcome**

6.01 If the charges are **not proved** at inquiry, the suspension shall be set aside, and you shall be reinstated with such back-wages and consequential benefits as the Disciplinary Authority directs, consistent with applicable law and the Inquiry Report.

6.02 If the charges are **proved**, the suspension period shall be treated in accordance with the final Order of the Disciplinary Authority.
