## Preamble

1.01 Technijian IT Services Pvt. Ltd. ("**Company**") is committed to providing every person at the workplace a safe, respectful, and dignified environment free from sexual harassment, irrespective of gender, sexual orientation, role, or engagement type.

1.02 This Policy is framed in compliance with the **Sexual Harassment of Women at Workplace (Prevention, Prohibition and Redressal) Act, 2013** ("**POSH Act**") and the **Sexual Harassment of Women at Workplace (Prevention, Prohibition and Redressal) Rules, 2013** ("**POSH Rules**"), and draws on the guidance laid down by the Hon'ble Supreme Court of India in *Vishaka & Ors. v. State of Rajasthan* (1997) 6 SCC 241 and its progeny.

1.03 While the POSH Act is gender-specific (protecting aggrieved women), the Company extends equivalent protection to all persons at the workplace through this Policy. Where the complainant is an aggrieved woman under the POSH Act, the statutory procedure prescribed by the Act shall apply; in other cases, an equivalent internal procedure adopting the same principles of natural justice and confidentiality shall apply.

---

## 1. Purpose, Scope, and Applicability

2.01 **Purpose.** To prevent, prohibit, and redress sexual harassment at the workplace and to secure for every person a work environment consistent with Articles 14, 15, 19(1)(g), and 21 of the Constitution of India.

2.02 **Scope.** This Policy covers all conduct occurring at the "workplace" as defined in Section 2(o) of the POSH Act and in Clause 3.03 below, including conduct during the course of employment, while traveling for work, and within virtual/remote work settings.

2.03 **Applicability.** This Policy applies to:
(a) all employees of the Company (regular, confirmed, on probation, part-time, fixed-term, deputed);
(b) contractors, sub-contractors, consultants, and their personnel deployed to work for or with the Company;
(c) interns, trainees, apprentices, and students on assignment;
(d) visitors, customers, clients, vendors, and third parties who interact with Company personnel at the workplace;
(e) Directors, officers, and managers of the Company.

2.04 **Virtual workplace.** Consistent with the *Ministry of Women & Child Development advisory* and evolving jurisprudence post-2020, "workplace" includes any virtual or online environment (video calls, chat platforms, email, collaboration tools, social media used for work) used to conduct Company business. Sexual harassment occurring through such media is within scope.

---

## 2. Definitions

3.01 **"Sexual harassment"** bears the meaning set out in **Section 2(n) of the POSH Act** and includes any one or more of the following unwelcome acts or behavior (whether directly or by implication):
(a) physical contact and advances;
(b) a demand or request for sexual favors;
(c) making sexually colored remarks;
(d) showing pornography;
(e) any other unwelcome physical, verbal, or non-verbal conduct of a sexual nature.

3.02 **"Aggrieved woman"** means, per **Section 2(a) POSH Act**, in relation to a workplace, a woman, of any age whether employed or not, who alleges to have been subjected to any act of sexual harassment by the respondent.

3.03 **"Workplace"** means, per **Section 2(o) POSH Act**, any place visited by the employee arising out of or during the course of employment, including transportation provided by the employer; and includes the Company's offices, client sites, off-site meetings, training venues, dwelling place or house for a dwelling-place-based worker, and virtual/online environments used for Company work.

3.04 **"Employee"** bears the meaning set out in **Section 2(f) POSH Act**.

3.05 **"Respondent"** means a person against whom the aggrieved woman has made a complaint under Section 9 of the POSH Act.

3.06 **"IC"** / **"ICC"** means the Internal Committee (formerly styled Internal Complaints Committee) constituted by the Company under **Section 4 POSH Act**. The terms are used interchangeably.

3.07 **"Presiding Officer"** means a woman employee at a senior level at the workplace appointed as the head of the IC under **Section 4(2)(a) POSH Act**.

## 3. Prohibited Conduct

4.01 The Company prohibits sexual harassment in any form. Without limitation, the following are deemed prohibited conduct:
(a) **Physical:** unwelcome touching, pinching, patting, grabbing, brushing against, hugging, kissing, blocking normal movement, assault.
(b) **Verbal:** sexually suggestive comments, jokes, innuendo, requests for sexual favors, propositions, comments about body/appearance, repeated unwanted invitations, threats.
(c) **Non-verbal:** leering, staring, suggestive gestures, displaying sexually explicit images/screensavers/posters, sending sexually explicit messages or media, stalking.
(d) **Quid pro quo:** implied or express promise of preferential treatment, threat of detrimental treatment, interference with work, or any action affecting present or future employment prospects in exchange for sexual favors — expressly covered under **Section 3(2) POSH Act**.
(e) **Hostile work environment:** conduct that has the purpose or effect of interfering with work performance or creating an intimidating, offensive, or humiliating work environment.

4.02 Any of the above occurring through the Company's virtual/online environment is equally prohibited.

## 5. Complaint Procedure (Section 9 POSH Act + Rule 6 POSH Rules)

6.01 **Who may file.** Any aggrieved woman as defined in Section 2(a) POSH Act. Where the aggrieved woman is unable to file the complaint herself on account of physical incapacity, mental incapacity, death, or otherwise, a complaint may be filed by persons listed in **Rule 6(1) POSH Rules**.

6.02 **Mode.** A written complaint in six (6) copies along with supporting documents and the names and addresses of witnesses shall be submitted to the IC, per **Rule 6(1) POSH Rules**. Electronic submission to icc-india@technijian.com is permitted, and physical copies may be provided thereafter.

6.03 **Timeline.** Complaint shall be filed within three (3) months from the date of the last incident, per **Section 9(1) POSH Act**. The IC may, for reasons to be recorded in writing, extend the time limit by a further period not exceeding three (3) months, per the proviso to Section 9(1).

6.04 **Assistance in drafting.** Where the complainant is unable to make a complaint in writing, the Presiding Officer or any Member of the IC shall render reasonable assistance to the complainant to put the complaint in writing, per **Section 9(2) POSH Act**.

6.05 **Acknowledgement.** The IC shall acknowledge receipt of a complaint in writing within seven (7) working days.

---

## 6. Conciliation (Section 10 POSH Act)

7.01 Before initiating inquiry, the IC may, at the request of the aggrieved woman, take steps to settle the matter between her and the respondent through conciliation, per **Section 10(1) POSH Act**.

7.02 **No monetary settlement** shall be made as a basis of conciliation, per the proviso to Section 10(1).

7.03 Where settlement is arrived at, the IC shall record the settlement and forward it to the employer to take action as specified, and provide copies to both parties, per **Section 10(2)–(3)**.

7.04 Where the aggrieved woman informs the IC that any term or condition of settlement has not been complied with by the respondent, the IC shall proceed to conduct an inquiry under Section 11, per **Section 10(4)**.

---

## 7. Inquiry (Section 11 POSH Act + Rule 7 POSH Rules)

8.01 Where conciliation is not opted for, or fails, the IC shall proceed to make inquiry into the complaint in accordance with the service rules and **Section 11(1) POSH Act**, and with respect to non-employee respondents, in accordance with the POSH Rules.

8.02 The inquiry shall be completed within a period of **ninety (90) days** from the date of receipt of complaint, per **Section 11(4) POSH Act**.

8.03 The IC shall follow the **principles of natural justice** — both parties shall be afforded a reasonable opportunity of being heard, of cross-examining witnesses, and of presenting their case.

8.04 The IC shall have the powers of a civil court under **Section 11(3)** for (a) summoning and enforcing attendance, (b) requiring discovery and production of documents, and (c) any other matter as prescribed.

8.05 Where allegations disclose a cognizable criminal offence (e.g., under **IPC Sections 354, 354A, 354B, 354C, 354D, 509**; or the **Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita, 2023** as applicable), the IC shall inform the complainant of her right to file a criminal complaint with the police and shall extend assistance.

---

## 8. Interim Relief (Section 12 POSH Act)

9.01 During the pendency of an inquiry, the IC may, on written request of the aggrieved woman, recommend to the employer any of the following, per **Section 12(1) POSH Act**:
(a) transfer the aggrieved woman or the respondent to any other workplace;
(b) grant leave to the aggrieved woman up to a period of three (3) months (in addition to entitled leave);
(c) restrain the respondent from reporting on the work performance of the aggrieved woman or writing her confidential report, and assign such duties to another officer;
(d) grant any other relief as may be prescribed.

9.02 The leave granted under Clause 9.01(b) shall be in addition to the leave the complainant would be otherwise entitled to, per **Section 12(3)**.

---

## 9. Confidentiality (Section 16 POSH Act + Section 17 Penalty)

10.01 Notwithstanding anything contained in the Right to Information Act, 2005, the contents of the complaint, identity and addresses of the aggrieved woman, respondent, witnesses, any information relating to conciliation and inquiry proceedings, recommendations of the IC, and action taken by the employer shall **not be published, communicated, or made known** to the public, press, or media in any manner, per **Section 16 POSH Act**.

10.02 Any person entrusted with the duty to handle or deal with the complaint, inquiry, or recommendations who contravenes Section 16 shall be liable for penalty per **Section 17 POSH Act**, in accordance with the service rules, which may include recovery of a sum as may be prescribed.

10.03 All IC records shall be stored in a secure, access-controlled location. Digital records shall be encrypted and access-logged.

---

## 10. Outcomes and Action

11.01 **Where allegation is proved** (Section 13(3) POSH Act), the IC shall recommend to the employer:
(a) action for sexual harassment as misconduct in accordance with the service rules, including but not limited to — written warning, censure, withholding of promotion/increment, suspension, termination for cause, recovery of compensation;
(b) deduction from the salary or wages of the respondent such sum as it may consider appropriate to be paid to the aggrieved woman, per **Section 13(3)(ii)** and **Section 15**.

11.02 **Determination of compensation** per **Section 15 POSH Act** shall consider:
(a) mental trauma, pain, suffering, and emotional distress;
(b) loss in career opportunity;
(c) medical expenses incurred by the victim;
(d) income and financial status of the respondent;
(e) feasibility of payment in lump sum or in instalments.

11.03 **Where allegation is not proved** (Section 13(2)), the IC shall recommend no action.

11.04 **False or malicious complaint** (Section 14 POSH Act). Where the IC arrives at a conclusion that the allegation was malicious, or that the complainant knowingly made a false complaint, or produced forged or misleading documents, the IC may recommend action against the complainant in accordance with service rules. The mere inability to substantiate or provide adequate proof shall not attract action under this clause. A **separate inquiry** shall be conducted in accordance with **Rule 7(6) POSH Rules** before any such finding.

11.05 The employer shall act on the recommendations of the IC within sixty (60) days of receipt of the report, per **Section 13(4)**.

## 11. Appeal (Section 18 POSH Act)

12.01 Any person aggrieved by the recommendations of the IC under Sections 13(2), 13(3), or 14, or the non-implementation thereof, may prefer an **appeal** to the appropriate Industrial Tribunal / Labour Court as notified under the **Industrial Disputes Act, 1947** having jurisdiction, **within ninety (90) days** of such recommendations, per **Section 18(1)–(2) POSH Act**.

---

## 12. Annual Report (Section 21 and Section 22 POSH Act)

13.01 The IC shall, in each calendar year, prepare, in such form and at such time as prescribed, an annual report and submit the same to the Company.

13.02 The Company shall forward a copy of the annual report to the **District Officer** (Panchkula, Haryana) by the end of January of each year, per **Section 21**.

13.03 The Company shall include in its annual report the number of cases filed, if any, and their disposal under this Act, per **Section 22**.

---

## 13. Training and Awareness (Section 19(c)–(g) POSH Act)

14.01 The Company shall:
(a) conduct POSH orientation for every new hire as part of induction;
(b) conduct annual refresher training for all employees;
(c) conduct specialized capacity-building training for IC members;
(d) display the penal consequences of sexual harassment and the composition of the IC at conspicuous places in the workplace;
(e) provide facilities to the IC for dealing with complaints and conducting inquiry;
(f) assist the IC in securing attendance of respondent and witnesses;
(g) make available such information to the IC as it may require.

---

## 14. Protection Against Retaliation

15.01 The Company shall ensure that no complainant, witness, IC member, or supporter of a complainant suffers any form of retaliation, victimization, or adverse action on account of the complaint, testimony, or involvement.

15.02 Retaliation shall itself constitute misconduct and grounds for disciplinary action up to and including termination for cause.

---

## 15. Governing Law and Jurisdiction

16.01 This Policy is governed by the laws of India, in particular the **Sexual Harassment of Women at Workplace (Prevention, Prohibition and Redressal) Act, 2013** and the **Sexual Harassment of Women at Workplace (Prevention, Prohibition and Redressal) Rules, 2013**, and shall be interpreted consistently therewith.

16.02 Courts at **Panchkula, Haryana** shall have jurisdiction over matters arising out of or in connection with this Policy, subject to the appellate jurisdiction of the Industrial Tribunal under Section 18 POSH Act.
