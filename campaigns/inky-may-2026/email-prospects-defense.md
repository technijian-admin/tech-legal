**Why This Matters Under CMMC 2.0 and DFARS 252.204-7012**

CMMC 2.0 Level 2 (which applies to any contractor handling CUI) requires implementation of **NIST SP 800-171** practices. Email security sits squarely at the intersection of multiple control families:

- **AC.L2-3.1.1 / 3.1.2** — Limit access to authorized users; control email flow of CUI
- **AT.L2-3.2.1 / 3.2.2** — User awareness and training for phishing threats
- **IA.L2-3.5.3** — Multifactor authentication (protecting email accounts from takeover)
- **IR.L2-3.6.1** — Operational incident response (detection of email-borne threats)
- **SC.L2-3.13.8** — Cryptographic protection of CUI in transmission (email encryption)
- **SI.L2-3.14.2 / 3.14.7** — Malicious code protection and identification of unauthorized use

**DFARS 252.204-7012** requires "adequate security" on all covered contractor information systems and 72-hour incident reporting through DIBNet — a clock that starts the moment an email-borne compromise is discovered.

And the enforcement side is no longer hypothetical: DoJ's Civil Cyber-Fraud Initiative is now actively pursuing False Claims Act cases against contractors who misrepresent their cybersecurity posture in federal contracts.
