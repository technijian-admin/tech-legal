**Why This Matters for a HIPAA Covered Entity**

The **HIPAA Security Rule** (45 CFR § 164.312) requires technical safeguards for electronic Protected Health Information (ePHI), including **access control** (§ 164.312(a)), **audit controls** (§ 164.312(b)), **integrity controls** (§ 164.312(c)), and **transmission security** (§ 164.312(e)). Email is the most common channel where all four requirements intersect — and phishing attacks remain the **single largest source of HIPAA breaches** reported to HHS Office for Civil Rights.

**HITECH Act** amplifies the consequences: unauthorized access to ePHI triggers mandatory breach notification to affected patients, HHS, and — for breaches of 500+ records — local media. Civil penalties run up to **$71,162 per violation**, capped at **$2.13 million per year** for identical violations (2025 adjusted). California's **Confidentiality of Medical Information Act (CMIA)** stacks additional state-level penalties and private rights of action on top of federal HIPAA exposure.

Standard Microsoft 365 / Google Workspace filtering doesn't meet the HIPAA "reasonable and appropriate" safeguards standard — OCR has repeatedly cited healthcare providers for failing to deploy advanced phishing detection after a breach.
