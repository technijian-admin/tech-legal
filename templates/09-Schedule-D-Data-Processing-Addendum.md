# SCHEDULE D — DATA PROCESSING ADDENDUM

**Attached to Master Service Agreement [MSA-XXXX]**
**Effective Date:** [DATE]

This Data Processing Addendum ("DPA") supplements the Master Service Agreement (the "MSA") between **Technijian, Inc.** ("Technijian") and **[CLIENT NAME]** ("Client") and governs Technijian's processing of Personal Information on behalf of Client. In the event of a conflict between this DPA and the MSA on matters of personal-data processing, this DPA prevails. In the event of a conflict between this DPA and an Annex to this DPA on a matter the Annex specifically addresses, the Annex prevails.

---

## 1. DEFINITIONS

Capitalized terms not defined here have the meanings given in the MSA or in the applicable data protection law.

**1.01.** "Applicable Data Protection Law" means all laws and regulations applicable to the processing of Personal Information under this DPA, including the California Consumer Privacy Act of 2018, as amended by the California Privacy Rights Act ("CCPA"), the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act and HITECH Act ("HIPAA") if Annex 1 is executed, the EU General Data Protection Regulation 2016/679 and the UK GDPR ("GDPR") if Annex 2 is executed, and any other applicable U.S. state privacy law (e.g., VCDPA, CPA, CTDPA, UCPA).

**1.02.** "Personal Information" means information that identifies, relates to, describes, is reasonably capable of being associated with, or could reasonably be linked, directly or indirectly, with a particular consumer, household, or natural person, as defined under the CCPA, and includes "personal data" under the GDPR and "Protected Health Information" under HIPAA where applicable.

**1.03.** "Process" or "Processing" has the meaning given under the applicable Data Protection Law and includes any operation performed on Personal Information.

**1.04.** "Business" and "Service Provider" have the meanings given under the CCPA. For purposes of this DPA and to the maximum extent permitted by Applicable Data Protection Law, Client is the "Business" (and where applicable the "Controller") and Technijian is the "Service Provider" (and where applicable the "Processor").

**1.05.** "Subprocessor" means any third party engaged by Technijian to Process Personal Information on Client's behalf in connection with the services, including offshore personnel and affiliated entities.

**1.06.** "Security Incident" has the meaning given in MSA Section 10.02.

**1.07.** "Data Subject" means an identified or identifiable natural person whose Personal Information is Processed under this DPA, including a "consumer" under the CCPA.

## 2. SCOPE AND ROLES

**2.01. Scope.** This DPA applies to Technijian's Processing of Personal Information on behalf of Client in connection with the services described in Schedule A, Schedule B, and any SOW.

**2.02. Roles.** Client is the Business / Controller and determines the purposes and means of Processing. Technijian is the Service Provider / Processor and Processes Personal Information solely on Client's documented instructions and as set forth in this DPA.

**2.03. Documented Instructions.** Client's documented instructions consist of: (a) the MSA and its Schedules and SOWs; (b) this DPA and its Annexes; (c) the Processing Description in Annex 0; and (d) any subsequent written instructions issued by Client to Technijian's designated contact at contracts@technijian.com or privacy@technijian.com.

**2.04. Technijian's Compliance.** Technijian shall comply with all obligations applicable to a Service Provider (and Processor where applicable) under Applicable Data Protection Law, including the obligations specified in Cal. Civ. Code § 1798.140(ag) and 11 CCR § 7051.

## 4. SERVICE PROVIDER OBLIGATIONS

**4.01. Purpose Limitation.** Technijian shall Process Personal Information solely:

(a) For the specific business purposes identified for each category of Personal Information in Annex 0;

(b) As reasonably necessary and proportionate to perform those business purposes; and

(c) As otherwise permitted under 11 CCR § 7050(b).

**4.02. Prohibited Processing.** Technijian shall NOT:

(a) Sell or Share Personal Information (as those terms are defined under the CCPA);

(b) Retain, use, or disclose Personal Information for any purpose other than for the business purposes set forth in Annex 0, including not retaining, using, or disclosing it for any commercial purpose other than providing the services;

(c) Retain, use, or disclose Personal Information outside of the direct business relationship between Technijian and Client;

(d) Combine Personal Information received from or on behalf of Client with Personal Information received from or on behalf of any other person, or collected from Technijian's own interaction with the Data Subject, except as permitted under 11 CCR § 7050(c) (e.g., to perform the services);

(e) Use Personal Information for cross-context behavioral advertising or targeted advertising;

(f) Process Personal Information for the purpose of training generative AI or large language models (whether Technijian's own or any third party's) without Client's prior written consent specific to that purpose; or

(g) Permit any Subprocessor to do any of the foregoing.

**4.03. Certification.** Technijian certifies that it understands the restrictions set forth in this Section 4 and will comply with them. This certification is made for purposes of Cal. Civ. Code § 1798.140(ag)(1)(B).

**4.04. Notification of Inability to Comply.** Technijian shall notify Client in writing within **five (5) business days** if Technijian determines it can no longer meet its obligations under Applicable Data Protection Law or this DPA. Upon such notification, Client may take reasonable and appropriate steps to stop and remediate the unauthorized Processing, including but not limited to suspending the affected services or terminating this DPA without penalty.

## 5. DATA SUBJECT RIGHTS

**5.01. Cooperation.** Technijian shall reasonably cooperate with and assist Client in responding to verifiable Data Subject requests under Applicable Data Protection Law, including requests to know, access, delete, correct, port, opt out of sale/sharing, opt out of automated decision-making, and limit use of sensitive personal information.

**5.02. Timing.** Technijian shall provide such cooperation within **ten (10) business days** of Client's written request, or sooner if reasonably required for Client to meet its statutory deadlines.

**5.03. Direct Requests.** If Technijian receives a Data Subject request directly, Technijian shall (a) not respond to the substance of the request other than to acknowledge receipt and direct the Data Subject to Client; and (b) forward the request to Client at the contact identified in Annex 0 within **three (3) business days** of receipt.

## 6. SUBPROCESSORS

**6.01. Authorization.** Client provides general written authorization for Technijian to engage Subprocessors to Process Personal Information, subject to the conditions in this Section 6 and the list of approved Subprocessors in **Annex 3 (Subprocessor List)**.

**6.02. Offshore Subprocessors and India Disclosure.** Client expressly acknowledges and authorizes Technijian's engagement of personnel located in India (currently operating from Technijian IT Services Pvt. Ltd., Panchkula, Haryana) as Subprocessors for Virtual Staff services, technical support, software development, and related services. India is not subject to a U.S. federal adequacy determination; cross-border safeguards (Standard Contractual Clauses or comparable mechanisms) are addressed in Annex 2 if EU/UK Personal Information is in scope.

**6.03. Flow-Down Obligations.** Technijian shall enter into a written agreement with each Subprocessor that imposes data protection obligations at least as protective as those in this DPA, including the prohibitions in Section 4.02.

**6.04. Liability for Subprocessors.** Technijian remains fully liable to Client for the acts and omissions of its Subprocessors with respect to Personal Information.

**6.05. Changes to Subprocessors.** Technijian shall provide Client with at least **thirty (30) days** prior written notice of the addition or replacement of any Subprocessor that will Process Personal Information. Client may object to a new Subprocessor within fifteen (15) days of the notice on reasonable data protection grounds, in which case the Parties shall work in good faith to find a mutually agreeable resolution. If no resolution is reached within thirty (30) days of Client's objection, Client may terminate the affected services without payment of the wind-down fee under MSA Section 2.03(b) (unrecoverable third-party costs under Section 2.03(a) remain payable).

## 7. SECURITY MEASURES

**7.01. Technical and Organizational Measures.** Technijian shall implement and maintain the technical and organizational measures set forth in **Annex 4 (Security Measures)**, which shall be no less protective than the measures required under MSA Section 10.03 and applicable industry standards (NIST Cybersecurity Framework, CIS Controls, or equivalent).

**7.02. Personnel.** Technijian shall ensure that all personnel (including Subprocessor personnel) with access to Personal Information: (a) are bound by written confidentiality obligations at least as protective as those in this DPA; (b) receive periodic training on data protection requirements relevant to their role; and (c) are granted access on a least-privilege, need-to-know basis with periodic access reviews.

**7.03. Updates.** Technijian may update Annex 4 from time to time, provided that the updated measures remain at least as protective as those replaced.

---

## 8. SECURITY INCIDENT RESPONSE

**8.01. Notification.** Technijian shall notify Client of a confirmed Security Incident affecting Client's Personal Information without unreasonable delay and in no event later than **forty-eight (48) hours** after Technijian becomes aware of the Security Incident, in accordance with MSA Section 10.02.

**8.02. Information Provided.** The notification shall include, to the extent then known: (a) the nature of the Security Incident, including categories and approximate number of affected Data Subjects and records; (b) the likely consequences; (c) the measures taken or proposed to address the Security Incident, including measures to mitigate adverse effects; and (d) the contact point for further information.

**8.03. Cooperation.** Technijian shall cooperate with Client's investigation, containment, remediation, and notification activities, including providing reasonable forensic information, log data, and timeline documentation.

**8.04. Regulatory Notifications.** Where Applicable Data Protection Law requires Client to notify a regulator or affected Data Subjects, Client retains sole responsibility for those notifications, with Technijian's cooperation under Section 8.03. Where Applicable Data Protection Law requires Technijian (e.g., as a Business Associate under HIPAA) to make a notification, Technijian shall coordinate with Client to the extent permitted.

## 9. AUDIT AND COMPLIANCE

**9.01. Records.** Technijian shall maintain records of its Processing activities sufficient to demonstrate compliance with Applicable Data Protection Law and this DPA, and shall make those records available to Client upon reasonable written request.

**9.02. Audit Rights.** Upon **thirty (30) days** prior written notice and no more than once per twelve (12) month period (except after a confirmed Security Incident, in which case as needed), Client (or a mutually agreed independent auditor bound by confidentiality obligations) may audit Technijian's compliance with this DPA. Audits shall be conducted during normal business hours, shall not unreasonably interfere with Technijian's operations, and shall not require Technijian to disclose information about other customers, employee personal information unrelated to the Processing, or Technijian's confidential security architecture beyond what is reasonably necessary to verify compliance.

**9.03. Audit Substitute.** In lieu of an on-site audit under Section 9.02, Technijian may satisfy its audit obligation by providing Client with the results of a recent independent third-party audit covering the relevant controls (e.g., SOC 2 Type II, ISO 27001 certification, HITRUST certification), provided the report is dated within the prior twelve (12) months.

**9.04. Costs.** Each Party shall bear its own costs for routine audits under Section 9.02. If an audit identifies a material non-conformity caused by Technijian, Technijian shall bear the reasonable cost of the audit and shall remediate the non-conformity within a mutually agreed timeline.

**10.01. U.S. Processing.** Personal Information will be Processed in the United States and (subject to Section 6.02) in India. Government Access Requests.** If Technijian receives a legally binding request from a government authority for disclosure of Client's Personal Information, Technijian shall, to the extent legally permitted: (a) notify Client without undue delay; (b) challenge the request if there are reasonable grounds; (c) provide only the minimum amount of Personal Information legally required; and (d) maintain a record of all such requests for Client's review on request.

## 11. DATA RETURN AND DELETION

**11.01. During Term.** Upon Client's written request during the term, Technijian shall return or delete Personal Information specified by Client within **thirty (30) days**, except as required to be retained by Applicable Data Protection Law.

**11.02. Upon Termination.** Within **thirty (30) days** of termination of the MSA or the affected service, Technijian shall, at Client's election, securely delete or return all Personal Information in its possession or under its control, in accordance with MSA Section 10.05 and using methods consistent with NIST SP 800-88. This obligation extends to Subprocessors.

**11.03. Backups and Retained Copies.** Technijian may retain copies of Personal Information only as required by Applicable Data Protection Law, in routine system backups that cannot be deleted without unreasonable effort, or in outside legal counsel's archival files. All such retained copies remain subject to the obligations of this DPA for as long as they are retained.

**11.04. Certification.** Technijian shall certify deletion (or return, as elected) in writing upon Client's written request.

## 12. LIABILITY

**12.01. Cap.** The Parties' liability for breaches of this DPA is governed by MSA Section 5, including the Enhanced Cap under MSA Section 5.03 for breaches of data protection obligations. For avoidance of doubt, breach of this DPA constitutes a breach of MSA Section 10 for purposes of the Enhanced Cap.

**12.02. Indemnification.** The indemnification obligations under MSA Section 6 apply to claims arising from breach of this DPA.

**12.03. Regulatory Fines.** Each Party shall bear its own regulatory fines and penalties to the extent such fines or penalties are attributable to its own non-compliance with Applicable Data Protection Law. Where a fine or penalty is attributable to both Parties' non-compliance, it shall be allocated proportionally to fault as determined by the regulator or by a court.

## 13. TERM, MODIFICATION, AND PRECEDENCE

**13.01. Term.** This DPA is effective as of the date set forth above and continues for the term of the MSA. Provisions that by their nature should survive (including Sections 8, 9, 11, and 12) survive termination.

**13.02. Modification for Legal Change.** If a change in Applicable Data Protection Law materially affects either Party's obligations under this DPA, the Parties shall negotiate in good faith to modify this DPA to comply with the change. If the Parties are unable to reach agreement within ninety (90) days, either Party may terminate the affected services without penalty.

**13.03. Precedence within DPA.** In the event of a conflict between provisions of this DPA, the order of precedence (highest to lowest) is: (a) Annex 1 (HIPAA BAA) for HIPAA matters; (b) Annex 2 (GDPR / SCCs) for EU/UK matters; (c) the body of this DPA; (d) Annex 0 (Processing Description); (e) other Annexes.

## ANNEX 4 — SECURITY MEASURES

Technijian shall implement and maintain at least the following security measures:

### A.4.1 Access Controls

- Role-based access control (RBAC) with least-privilege defaults
- Multi-factor authentication for all administrative access to systems containing Personal Information
- Quarterly access reviews with documented removal of unneeded access
- Separation of duties between development, operations, and security

### A.4.2 Encryption

- Personal Information encrypted in transit using TLS 1.2 or higher
- Personal Information encrypted at rest using AES-256 or equivalent
- Encryption keys managed in a key management system with documented rotation policy

### A.4.3 Network Security

- Firewall protection at network perimeter
- Network segmentation separating production, management, and development environments
- Intrusion detection / prevention monitoring
- Vulnerability scanning at least monthly with documented remediation SLAs

### A.4.4 Endpoint Security

- Endpoint protection (antivirus / EDR) on all systems with access to Personal Information
- Automatic security patching with documented exception process
- Disk encryption on all laptops and mobile devices

### A.4.5 Logging and Monitoring

- Centralized log collection for systems Processing Personal Information
- Security event monitoring with 24/7 alerting on critical events
- Log retention sufficient to support incident investigation (minimum 13 months for security logs)

### A.4.6 Personnel Security

- Background checks for personnel with access to Personal Information, where permitted by law
- Confidentiality and data protection terms in employment / contractor agreements
- Annual security and privacy awareness training

### A.4.7 Incident Response

- Documented incident response plan with defined roles and escalation procedures
- Annual tabletop exercises
- Notification procedures consistent with Section 8 of this DPA

### A.4.8 Business Continuity

- Documented backup and recovery procedures
- Tested disaster recovery plan
- Geographically redundant infrastructure for production services

### A.4.9 Vendor Management

- Subprocessor risk assessments before onboarding
- Annual review of Subprocessor compliance with this DPA's flow-down requirements

### A.4.10 Updates

Technijian may update this Annex 4 from time to time, provided that updated measures remain at least as protective as those replaced. Material reductions in security require Client's prior written consent.
