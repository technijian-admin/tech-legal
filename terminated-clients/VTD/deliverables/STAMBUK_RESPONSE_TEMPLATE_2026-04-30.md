> ### 1. Document architecture

The online-services line items (AV protection, patch management, secure DNS, my-remote, backup storage, etc.) bill for the same calendar month the invoice is dated. > ### 4. Response to the Period 1 / averaging argument
>
> Your April 6, 2026 letter argued that the averaging mechanism in Under Contract ¶¶ 3-4 "fully collected" Period 1 (May 2023 – April 2024) excess via Period 2's elevated billing rate, and that Mr. Jain's June 24, 2025 termination email supports limiting Technijian's recovery to the 2024-2025 period. We respectfully disagree on both grounds — first as a matter of contract mechanics, and second as a matter of the data.
>
> **On the contract mechanics.** Paragraphs 3 and 4 of the Under Contract section describe an operational placeholder-and-reconciliation mechanism that exists because of the 30-day advance-billing convention in Other Terms ¶ 2. When the 12-month cycle is about to close, the first invoice for the next cycle must already be issued before the prior cycle's final month closes — at which point only the first 11 months of actual data (N − 1) are available to compute the new monthly average. The first month of the new cycle is therefore charged at the **previous cycle's average as a placeholder** (paragraph 3). Once the 12th month closes and the full prior-period actuals are known, the difference between the placeholder rate and the true new average is reconciled on the **next month's invoice via a one-time adjustment line** (paragraph 4): an extra charge if the new average is higher, a credit if it is lower. The adjustment applies to a single placeholder month and is settled on a single subsequent invoice. It is a smoothing mechanism for the cycle transition, not a 12-month elevated rate that "collects" prior unbilled hours over the following year. Paragraph 5 — "any hours that exceeded the previous under contract period average" — is the separate, distinct mechanism that addresses unbilled actual hours at termination.
>
> **On the data.** Independently, the actual billing system applied the averaging mechanism below the contract-mandated rate at both cycle boundaries, not above it. P2 monthly billed quantity for offshore support was 76.77 hours per month against P1's actual monthly average of 81.86 hours per month — a deficit of 5.10 hours per month. P3 monthly billed quantity for offshore support was 96.35 hours per month against P2's actual monthly average of 114.87 hours per month — a deficit of 18.52 hours per month. The averaging under-billed Vintage Design relative to the contract math at every boundary the mechanism was applied. A premise that the averaging "fully collected" the prior period therefore does not survive contact with the documents now in your possession.
>
> Second, on Mr. Jain's June 24, 2025 email: the same paragraph your letter quotes goes on to describe a separate cancellation calculation tied to "all the hours billed to you versus the actual hours of support given," with any unbilled actual hours billed at $150 per hour. Mr. Jain's email attached a spreadsheet showing those hours "since we started working with you" — the multi-period total, not a "last 12 months only" figure — and arrived at 1,143.98 unbilled hours × $150 = $171,597. The corrected figure with full reconciliation across the 27-month period is the 1,567.88 hours summarized in section 2 above.
>
> Course of performance and ratification are independently dispositive. Vintage Design received 168 weekly invoices and 28 monthly invoices over the 27-month relationship. Postmaster receipts confirm delivery of every transmission. Aside from 13 out-of-office auto-replies from Ms. Garcia, our records reflect zero substantive objection from Vintage Design to any invoice, time-entry record, or POD allocation during the entire performance period. Cal. Comm. Code § 1303(b); *Cobb v. Pacific Mut. Life Ins. Co.* (1935) 4 Cal.2d 565; *Gleason v. Klamer* (1980) 103 Cal.App.3d 782. Ms. Garcia's signature on the May 4, 2023 Client Monthly Service Agreement and her continued performance through July 2025 constitute ratification of the billing methodology under *Rakestraw v. Rodrigues* (1972) 8 Cal.3d 67, 73.
