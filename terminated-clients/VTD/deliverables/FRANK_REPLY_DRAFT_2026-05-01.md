## Q&A — All Five Highlighted Items

### 1. June 2, 2023 time entry attachment — onshore hours show much more than 2.75 hours in the summary

The 2.75-hour figure in the earlier summary captured only the Tech Support (onshore) hours recorded on the June 2, 2023 monthly invoice attachment for that single file. Our billing system operates on a two-source model: a monthly invoice email containing a time-entry spreadsheet reporting the prior month's actuals, and a separate weekly invoice email (also sent to Vintage Design's finance contact each week) capturing additional support hours logged after the monthly attachment was generated.

When both sources are combined, the full onshore hours for May 2023 reconcile to approximately **33.72 hours** — which is what Vintage Design would have seen across the June 2, 2023 monthly attachment and the corresponding weekly entries for that period. The 2.75-hour figure represents a subset only and is not the correct per-month total.

This two-source pattern applies throughout the 27-month service period. The ShareFile data room contains both the monthly invoice emails (138 files) and the weekly statement emails (369 files). The monthly and weekly time-entry attachments together form the complete record of hours worked each month.

### Broader note on why totals may not tie exactly between the summary spreadsheet and the individual monthly invoices

There are two structural reasons the per-month actuals in the earlier summary do not always match the figures on individual monthly invoice attachments:

**First**, some hours appear on weekly invoice attachments rather than the monthly one. Hours logged after the monthly attachment was generated are captured on the next weekly statement. The aggregate monthly total requires combining both sources. Vintage Design received both sets of emails throughout the engagement, so the underlying data is in the ShareFile data room.

**Second**, the contract's averaging mechanism produces a one-time billing adjustment at each 12-month cycle boundary. Billing for the first month of a new cycle uses the prior cycle's average as a placeholder — because the 12th month of the prior cycle has not yet closed when that invoice is generated. Once the prior cycle fully closes and the new average is computed, the delta between the placeholder rate and the true new average is applied as a one-line adjustment on the next month's invoice. This reconciliation line is not additional hours worked; it is a rate correction. It will appear as a separate line item on certain invoices and should be read alongside the hour counts, not as additional hours.
