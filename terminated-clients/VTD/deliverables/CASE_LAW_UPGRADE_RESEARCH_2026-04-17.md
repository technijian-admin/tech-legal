# Case Law Upgrade Research — Technijian v. Vintage Design

**Prepared:** 2026-04-17
**Memo under review:** `CASE_LAW_RESEARCH_MEMO.md` dated 4/14/2026
**Matter:** AAA Commercial Arbitration — *Technijian, Inc. v. Vintage Design*
**Scope:** California authority 2020–2026 potentially superior to authority cited in the existing 30-issue memo
**Status:** All citations to be Shepardized before citing in any filing

---

## Bottom line

The 4/14/2026 memo's authority is in better condition than a generic "2000–2019 citations" concern would suggest. Course-of-performance, § 1717 reciprocity, § 3287(a) prejudgment interest, and business-records doctrine are settled and stable — no material 2020–2026 opinion improves on them. The three meaningful upgrades are *Gormley* (A), *Iyere* (A), and *Hawkins* (A). The three meaningful adverse authorities to pre-empt are *Bannister*, *Honchariw*, and *Graylee* — all distinguishable on commercial / non-consumer / sophisticated-parties / course-of-performance-ratified facts.

## Grade A — Cite in the memo

### 1. *Gormley v. Gonzalez* (2022) 84 Cal.App.5th 72 → **§ 2 (Liquidated Damages / § 1671(b))**

- **Holding:** In a non-consumer contract (there, a settlement agreement), § 1671(b) creates "a general rule favoring the enforcement of liquidated damages provisions"; challenger bears the burden; LD upheld where parties were represented and amount was a reasonable forecast.
- **Why it belongs in the memo:** Pairs with *Ridgley* and sharpens the burden-shift argument. Dramatically strengthens the presumption-of-validity framing in a commercial setting — exactly the Technijian/Vintage Design posture (two represented businesses, DocuSigned agreement). Cite *before* *El Centro Mall*.

### 2. *Iyere v. Wise Auto Group* (2023) 87 Cal.App.5th 747 → **§ 17 (E-Signature / UETA)**

- **Holding:** Bare "I don't recall signing" is insufficient to dispute authenticity of a signature; authentication may be "carried in any manner," including circumstances surrounding execution.
- **Why it belongs:** First post-2020 published California appellate decision to rebalance the e-sig authentication burden toward the proponent. Cite alongside *Fabian* and *J.B.B. Investment Partners* to show the full doctrinal spectrum.
- **Caveat:** *Iyere* concerned a physical signature; the court expressly distinguished electronic ones. Use it for the *burden framework* ("carried in any manner") rather than as direct e-signature authority.

### 3. *People v. Hawkins* (2002) 98 Cal.App.4th 1428 → **§ 23 (Admissibility of Ticketing Records)**

- **Holding:** Backstops the Evid. Code § 1552 presumption that printed representations of computer-generated information are presumed accurate.
- **Why it belongs:** Pre-2020 but stronger than *Aguimatang* alone on the narrow "presumption covers the printed record" point. Add alongside *Aguimatang* and *Jazayeri* to pre-empt any "the presumption doesn't cover accuracy of content" objection.

## Grade B — Consider

### 4. *Constellation-F, LLC v. World Trading 23, Inc.* (2020) 45 Cal.App.5th 22

- **Holding:** A lease provision imposing a 150% holdover rent is not an unenforceable penalty under § 1671; § 1671 targets "unfair and unreasonable coercion arising from an imbalance of bargaining power."
- **Why worth considering:** Useful analogy — the $150/hr cancellation rate reflects arms-length allocation between businesses, not coercion. Not as directly on-point as *Gormley*; use as supporting citation.

### 5. *Ramirez v. Charter Communications, Inc.* (2024) 16 Cal.5th 478

- **Holding:** Discovery limitations in arbitration agreements are permissible so long as "adequate" to vindicate rights; unconscionability assessed at time of formation, not hindsight; multiple unconscionable provisions trigger a **qualitative** severance analysis.
- **Why worth considering:** Leading 2024 California Supreme Court arbitration decision. Employment-origin but the timing rule and qualitative severance framework generalize. Cite defensively in § 15 if Vintage attacks the AAA clause on unconscionability grounds.

## Grade C — Adverse; have ready to distinguish

### 6. *Bannister v. Marinidence Opco, LLC* (2021) 64 Cal.App.5th 541 (e-signature authentication)

- **Holding:** Employer failed to authenticate e-signature under § 1633.9(a) where "Client ID"/pin was not employee-specific; HR staff may have signed for the employee.
- **Vintage playbook:** If Erica Garcia denies signing the 5/4/2023 Agreement.
- **Distinguish:** Garcia's DocuSign was tied to her Vintage corporate email; the Certificate of Completion (Envelope ID B679C550-E41C-4E31-BE34-7F8FFF437C3D) shows unique authentication, IP, and timestamp; 27 months of paid, ratified performance independently settle attribution under § 1633.9(a).

### 7. *Honchariw v. FJM Private Mortgage Fund, LLC* (2022) 83 Cal.App.5th 893 (late fees as penalty)

- **Holding:** Late-payment fees assessed against full loan principal violated § 1671 as unenforceable penalties. Cal. Supreme Court denied review 12/21/2022.
- **Vintage playbook:** Likely citation for the penalty argument against the cancellation fee.
- **Distinguish:** *Honchariw* penalized a one-time default triggering fees on the *entire* balance (classic disproportionality). The Technijian rate applies only to hours *actually worked* at overage, tied to contemporaneously measured usage, and sits at or below the Agreement's own "Out of Contract" rates ($150/$200/hr).

### 8. *Graylee v. Castro* (2020) 52 Cal.App.5th 1107 (§ 1671(b) stipulated-judgment LD invalid)

- **Holding:** A stipulated-judgment LD provision violated § 1671(b) because no reasonable relationship to anticipated damages.
- **Vintage playbook:** Backup penalty argument.
- **Distinguish:** *Graylee* involved a residential-tenancy stipulated judgment, not a negotiated commercial-services rate tied to contemporaneously measured hours between sophisticated commercial parties.

## Awareness only

### 9. *Hohenshelt v. Superior Court* (2025) (CCP § 1281.97/.98 fee-payment deadlines)

FAA does not preempt; nonpayment waives only when willful, grossly negligent, or fraudulent. Relevant to fee administration *once arbitration is under way*, not to enforceability of the arbitration agreement.

### 10. *New England Country Foods, LLC v. Vanlaw Food Products* (2025) (§ 1668 willful injury)

§ 1668 bars contractual limitation-of-liability for willful injury. Only relevant if Vintage pivots to a § 1668 theory characterizing the cancellation fee as an exculpatory provision.
