# Chandigarh UT — Minimum Wages and Labour Welfare Fund

**Last verified:** 2026-05-06.

This page covers two state-level wage / welfare instruments applicable to Technijian's Chandigarh UT establishment:

1. **Minimum wages** — notified by Chandigarh Administration under the Code on Wages, 2019.
2. **Punjab Labour Welfare Fund Act, 1965** — extended to Chandigarh.

---

## Part A — Chandigarh UT Minimum Wages

### Legal basis

- **Code on Wages, 2019** (in force from 21-Nov-2025), §6 and §8 — appropriate Government (here, Chandigarh Administration) to fix and revise minimum rates of wages for various scheduled employments / skill categories.
- Pre-code rules under the Minimum Wages Act 1948 (Punjab Minimum Wages Rules) **continue as transitional rules** until replaced.

### Rates effective 1-October-2025

The Chandigarh Administration's most recent notification (Department of Labour) sets minimum wages **per month** at the following rates, effective from **1-October-2025**:

| Skill category | Monthly minimum wage (₹) |
|---|---|
| **Highly Skilled** | 15,637 |
| **Skilled — I** | 15,237 |
| **Skilled — II** | 15,012 |
| **Semi-Skilled — I** | 14,812 |
| **Semi-Skilled — II** | 14,712 |
| **Unskilled** | 14,562 |
| **Staff Class I** (clerical / supervisory at high level) | 15,347 |
| **Staff Class II** (clerical at intermediate level) | 14,987 |
| **Staff Class III** (basic clerical) | 14,837 |

> **Caveat**: Chandigarh UT revises minimum wages approximately every 6 months, typically with effect from 1 April and 1 October each year. Re-verify the current rate at the [Chandigarh Administration Department of Labour website](https://chandigarh.gov.in/) before payroll cycles. Variable Dearness Allowance (VDA) is added separately and revised semi-annually.

### Components (typical structure)

A Chandigarh minimum wage is composed of:
- **Basic** rate (per the table above), AND
- **Variable Dearness Allowance (VDA)** — linked to All-India CPI (Industrial Workers); revised twice yearly.

Total minimum wage = Basic + VDA.

### Skill classification

The skill classification is set by reference to **National Industrial Classification** and Punjab/Chandigarh administrative practice:

- **Unskilled**: simple physical labour without prior training (cleaning, helper, security guard – static).
- **Semi-Skilled**: limited training (data-entry operator, junior office assistant, machine operator).
- **Skilled**: significant training (programmer, senior technician, accountant, system administrator).
- **Highly Skilled**: extensive training + experience (senior IT engineer, project lead).
- **Staff Class I/II/III**: supervisory and administrative classifications.

For an IT/ITeS establishment, most engineering positions fall in **Skilled or Highly-Skilled** category. Support and admin staff in **Staff Class** or **Semi-Skilled / Skilled**.

### Equal wages (Chapter V of Code on Wages)

- No discrimination in wages or recruitment on grounds of gender for same / similar work.
- Trainees: 75% of the relevant skill-category wage; cannot be lower than unskilled rate; trainee period maximum 1 year.

### Working hours, overtime, payment of wages

For minimum-wage employees:
- Working hours not to exceed 9/day, 48/week (per state S&E Act + Code on Wages).
- Overtime: at least double the ordinary rate.
- Wages payable monthly, before 7th of next month.

### Payslip & records

- A wage slip showing breakup of basic, VDA, allowances, deductions must be issued every wage period.
- Wages register (Form G) maintained.

### Penalties for under-payment

- First offence: fine up to **₹50,000** (Code on Wages §54).
- Repeat within 5 years: imprisonment up to 3 months + fine up to **₹1,00,000**.

### Practical implications for Technijian Chandigarh

- Even the lowest-paid Technijian Chandigarh employee must receive **at least ₹14,562/month** (Unskilled rate, before VDA).
- Most Technijian employees (developers, support engineers, business roles) are well above the minimum wage. The minimum-wage check is operationally relevant for:
  - **Apprentices** (75% of category wage; cannot be below unskilled rate).
  - **Office boys / housekeeping** (Unskilled).
  - **Security guards** if engaged directly (Unskilled).
  - **Junior data-entry / admin** (Semi-Skilled).
  - **Trainees and probationers** in lower-band roles.
- For minimum-wage compliance under the **Code on Wages 2019** (post-21-Nov-2025), the wage definition (§2(y)) requires Basic + DA ≥ 50% of total CTC. **The whole CTC structure must be audited** — see [02-central-laws/code-on-wages.md](../02-central-laws/code-on-wages.md) for detail.

## Part B — Punjab Labour Welfare Fund Act, 1965 (Chandigarh)

### Status

The Punjab Labour Welfare Fund Act, 1965 was extended to Chandigarh UT shortly after Chandigarh became a UT. Continues in force; not subsumed by any of the four codes.

### Coverage

- All "establishments" (factories, shops, commercial establishments, motor transport undertakings, plantations, road transport corporations) in Chandigarh UT employing **5 or more** employees.

### Contribution

| Party | Contribution |
|---|---|
| **Employee** | **₹5 per month** (deducted from wages) |
| **Employer** | **₹20 per month** (per employee) |
| **Total per employee per month** | **₹25** |

(Verify current rates — historically the rates have been revised upward periodically.)

### Frequency

- Contributions deducted from wages and credited to the Labour Welfare Fund.
- Frequency: **half-yearly** (April–September; October–March).
- Deposit timeline: by 15-October (for Apr-Sep period) and 15-April (for Oct-Mar period).

### Deposit method

- Challan-based deposit at designated bank, OR via Chandigarh Labour Department's online portal (where active).
- Form A return submitted half-yearly.

### What the Fund is used for

The Fund is administered by the Chandigarh Welfare Board to provide:
- Educational stipends for workers' children.
- Marriage assistance.
- Maternity assistance.
- Housing scheme assistance.
- Medical assistance for chronic illness.
- Recreational facilities.

Employees can apply directly to the board for benefits (verify current schemes via Department of Labour, Chandigarh Administration).

### Penalties

- Failure to deposit contribution: interest + penalty under §16 of the Act.
- Falsification of records: prosecution under §17.

### Practical implication for Technijian Chandigarh

- Add ₹25/month/employee (₹5 employee + ₹20 employer) to payroll deductions and remittances.
- Half-yearly remittance via Form A — track in [06-compliance/compliance-calendar.md](../06-compliance/compliance-calendar.md).
- Maintain employee-wise LWF contribution register.
