# HR Topic — Hiring and Onboarding

**Last verified:** 2026-05-06.

This page consolidates the **statutory** and **best-practice** requirements for hiring and onboarding employees at Technijian's Chandigarh / Panchkula offices, mapped to the relevant acts.

---

## Pre-hiring — non-discriminatory recruitment

### Equal opportunity (Code on Wages, Chapter V)

- No discrimination on grounds of **gender** in recruitment.
- "Same / similar work" must receive equal remuneration regardless of sex.
- Job advertisement language: gender-neutral (avoid "preference for male candidate" etc.).

### Disability (Rights of Persons with Disabilities Act, 2016)

- Establishments with **≥20 employees** must adopt an **equal opportunity policy** for persons with disabilities.
- Must register with state PWD commissioner (if applicable).
- 4% government-job reservation does not apply to private sector, but reasonable accommodations must be considered.

### No domicile-based reservation in private hiring

- The Haryana State Employment of Local Candidates Act, 2020 was **struck down** in November 2023 — see [04-haryana-panchkula/local-candidates-act.md](../04-haryana-panchkula/local-candidates-act.md).
- Hire on merit. Maintain hiring records demonstrating bona fide selection criteria.

### Age verification

- **Below 14 — never employ.** Child Labour Act prohibition.
- **14–18 — only in non-hazardous roles**, with restricted hours.
- **At interview** — collect age proof: Aadhaar, PAN, school / college certificate.

## Background verification

### Best practice

Conduct background verification (BGV) for new hires:
- Identity verification (Aadhaar / PAN / passport).
- Education verification (degrees, certifications).
- Previous-employer verification (employment dates, designation, exit reason).
- Address verification.
- Criminal-record check (police verification or commercial vendor).
- Reference checks (2–3 professional references).

### Legal basis under DPDP Act

- BGV processing requires **explicit consent** of the candidate (cannot rely solely on the employment-LU basis since it occurs pre-employment).
- Engage BGV vendor under a **written Data Processor agreement**.
- Vendor must comply with DPDP standards on security, breach notification, sub-processing.

## Offer letter — required contents

A robust offer letter must include:

### 1. Employer identification

- Legal name of Technijian entity in India (e.g., Technijian India Pvt. Ltd. or its registered name).
- Registered office address.
- The specific office/location where the candidate will work (Chandigarh / Panchkula).

### 2. Employee identification

- Name, address, contact details.
- Date of birth, gender (for statutory recordkeeping).
- Aadhaar / PAN reference (or to be furnished on joining).

### 3. Position

- Designation.
- Department / reporting manager.
- Nature of role: full-time / fixed-term / part-time / probationary.
- Job description (or reference to attached JD).

### 4. Date of joining and probation

- Start date.
- Probation period (typically 3–6 months).
- Probation extension / confirmation criteria.

### 5. Compensation (CTC structure compliant with Code on Wages 2019)

- **Basic salary** (≥50% of CTC under §2(y) of Code on Wages).
- Dearness Allowance (if applicable).
- House Rent Allowance.
- Special allowance.
- Other allowances (travel, communication, etc.) — kept ≤50% of total in aggregate to comply with the wage definition.
- **Annual CTC**: total of all components.
- **Gross monthly salary**.
- Variable / performance bonus (if any).

### 6. Statutory deductions

- Employee EPF contribution: 12% of (Basic + DA), up to ceiling.
- Employee ESIC contribution: 0.75% of gross (if applicable).
- Professional Tax: NOT applicable in Chandigarh or Haryana.
- Labour Welfare Fund: ₹5/month (Chandigarh) or ₹31/month (Haryana).
- Income tax (TDS) per employee declarations.

### 7. Working hours

- Standard hours: 9 hrs/day, 48 hrs/week (per state S&E Act).
- Office hours / shift pattern.
- Weekly off (as per establishment).
- Overtime policy (paid at double rate per Code on Wages).

### 8. Leave entitlements

- Earned / privilege leave: ≥15 days/year (state S&E Act).
- Sick leave: ≥7 days/year.
- Casual leave: ≥7 days/year.
- Public holidays: 7 (Chandigarh) / 8 (Haryana) per year.
- Maternity / paternity leave (per Social Security Code).

### 9. Statutory benefits

- EPF / EPS / EDLI (under Social Security Code).
- ESIC (if gross wages ≤ ₹21,000) OR group health insurance.
- Gratuity (after 5 years; 1 year for fixed-term).
- Bonus (if gross ≤ notified ceiling).

### 10. Confidentiality, IP, non-compete

- Confidentiality obligation: during and after employment.
- IP assignment: all work-related IP belongs to Technijian.
- Non-solicitation: no solicitation of clients or employees post-exit (12–24 months).
- Non-compete: enforceable in India only with reasonable scope and duration; courts strike down broad restraints (Section 27 of Indian Contract Act 1872).

### 11. Notice period / termination

- During probation: typically **15 days** notice from either side.
- Post-confirmation: typically **30 days** (junior / mid) or **60–90 days** (senior).
- Garden leave / payment in lieu of notice options.
- Termination for cause: per Code of Conduct / Service Rules.
- Reference IR Code 2020 retrenchment provisions for "workers" (notice + 15-days/year compensation; below 300-worker establishment doesn't need Govt permission).

### 12. Code of Conduct / Service Rules

- Reference to employee handbook / code of conduct.
- Disciplinary procedure.
- Grievance redressal mechanism.

### 13. POSH Act compliance

- Acknowledgement of zero-tolerance policy on sexual harassment.
- IC contact details.
- Mandatory training participation.

### 14. Data privacy notice

- Reference to HR Privacy Notice.
- Lawful basis for processing.
- Employee rights and grievance officer.

### 15. Governing law and jurisdiction

- Governing law: laws of India.
- Jurisdiction: courts in Chandigarh / Panchkula respectively.

### 16. Acceptance and signature

- Signed by Technijian authorised signatory.
- Counter-signed by employee.
- Date of acceptance.

## Probation, confirmation, and fixed-term

### Probation

- Typically 3–6 months.
- Extendable once for valid reason (notice in writing to employee).
- Termination during probation: shorter notice period as per offer letter.
- All statutory benefits continue during probation.

### Confirmation

- On successful completion, issue confirmation letter.
- Update HRMS / records.
- Standard notice period (e.g., 30 days) becomes applicable.

### Fixed-term employment (FTE)

- Permitted under IR Code 2020 (universal availability).
- Same statutory benefits as permanent staff (proportionate to service).
- **Gratuity accrues from 1 year** (not 5).
- Cannot be used to disguise permanent positions to avoid statutory protections.
- Renewal of FTE beyond a reasonable period may be reclassified as permanent by tribunals.
