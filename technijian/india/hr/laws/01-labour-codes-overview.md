# India's Four Labour Codes — Status & Implementation Overview

**Last verified:** 2026-05-06

This page explains the consolidated labour-code reform that is the most important development in Indian employment law since independence. Read this **before** any of the individual code pages.

---

## TL;DR

- Between 2019 and 2020, Parliament enacted **four codes** that consolidate **29 earlier central labour laws**.
- The codes were **notified into force on 21-November-2025** by Ministry of Labour & Employment notification.
- The 29 prior acts (Minimum Wages, Factories, EPF, ESI, Industrial Disputes, etc.) stand **repealed** from that date — but in practice, their substance has been re-enacted inside the codes, often with material changes.
- **Operationalisation requires rules** to be notified by both the central government and each state. Central draft rules published 30-Dec-2025; final central rules expected April 2026. **State rules vary** — Haryana and Chandigarh UT are still finalising.
- During the transition, prudent employers (a) comply with the **code text**, (b) follow **continuing pre-code rules** until expressly replaced, and (c) monitor state notifications closely.

---

## The four codes at a glance

| # | Code | Year enacted | Repeals | Primary subject |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Code on Wages** | 2019 | Minimum Wages Act 1948; Payment of Wages Act 1936; Payment of Bonus Act 1965; Equal Remuneration Act 1976 | All wage-related law: minimum wage, payment timing, bonus, equal remuneration |
| 2 | **Industrial Relations Code** | 2020 | Industrial Disputes Act 1947; Trade Unions Act 1926; Industrial Employment (Standing Orders) Act 1946 | Trade unions, standing orders, lay-off, retrenchment, closure, disputes |
| 3 | **Code on Social Security** | 2020 | EPF Act 1952; ESI Act 1948; Payment of Gratuity Act 1972; Maternity Benefit Act 1961; Employees' Compensation Act 1923; Building & Other Construction Workers Welfare Cess Act 1996; Cine Workers Welfare Fund Act 1981; Unorganised Workers' Social Security Act 2008; Employees Exchanges (CNV) Act 1959 | All social-security benefits |
| 4 | **Occupational Safety, Health and Working Conditions Code (OSH Code)** | 2020 | Factories Act 1948; Contract Labour Act 1970; Inter-State Migrant Workmen Act 1979; Building & Other Construction Workers Act 1996; Mines Act 1952; Plantations Labour Act 1951; Working Journalists Act 1955; Sales Promotion Employees Act 1976; Beedi & Cigar Workers Act 1966; Cine Workers and Cinema Theatre Workers Act 1981; Motor Transport Workers Act 1961; Dock Workers (Safety) Act 1986; Working Journalists (Wages) Act 1958 | Workplace health & safety; conditions of work; contract labour |

The 29-law repeal list is the single most material consequence of the codification.

## Implementation timeline (Indian labour codes)

| Date | Event |
|---|---|
| 8-Aug-2019 | Code on Wages enacted by Parliament |
| 23-Sep-2020 | Industrial Relations Code, Social Security Code, OSH Code enacted |
| 2020–2024 | Pre-publication of draft central + state rules; staggered state rule notifications |
| 21-Nov-2025 | **Codes notified into force** (commencement notification by Ministry of Labour & Employment) |
| 21-Nov-2025 | 29 prior acts **repealed** with corresponding savings |
| 30-Dec-2025 | Central government published **draft central rules** for all four codes for public consultation |
| 31-Dec-2025 to 30-Jan-2026 | 30-day consultation window for IR Code rules |
| 31-Dec-2025 to 14-Feb-2026 | 45-day consultation window for Wage / Social Security / OSH rules |
| ~1-Apr-2026 | Final central rules expected to be notified (timeline indicative) |
| Ongoing | State-level rules being finalised by each state/UT |

> **Consult [Ministry of Labour & Employment portal](https://labour.gov.in/) and [Haryana Labour Department](https://hrylabour.gov.in/) and [Chandigarh Administration Labour Department](https://chandigarh.gov.in/) for the latest notifications.** Some chapters of the codes — especially on social-security extension to gig/platform workers — have separate operationalisation timelines.

## What "in force" means in practice (transitional reality)

The codes' substantive provisions apply from 21-Nov-2025. But many operational details were embedded in **rules** under the old acts (e.g., the Minimum Wages (Central) Rules, Factories (Central) Rules, EPF Scheme 1952, ESI (General) Regulations 1950).

The savings clause in each code provides that **rules and notifications under the repealed acts continue to apply** until corresponding code-rules are published, **to the extent they are not inconsistent with the code**.

Therefore:

| Topic | Applicable law as of 2026-05 |
|---|---|
| Minimum wages | Code on Wages 2019 + state notifications under §8 of the code (Haryana & Chandigarh UT have notified) |
| Working hours / OT | Code chapters + continuing state S&E Acts (since the Punjab S&E Act 1958 is **not** in the 29-act repeal list) |
| EPF & ESI | Social Security Code 2020 + EPF Scheme 1952 + ESI Regulations 1950 (continuing as transitional rules) |
| Gratuity | Social Security Code 2020 (Chapter V) replaces 1972 Act; rules carry forward |
| Maternity | Social Security Code 2020 (Chapter VI) replaces 1961 Act; 26-week paid leave preserved |
| Retrenchment / lay-off | IR Code 2020 (Chapter X & XI); thresholds for Government permission **raised from 100 → 300 workers** |
| Standing orders | IR Code 2020 (Chapter IV); applicability threshold raised to ≥300 workers (was 100) |
| Contract labour | OSH Code 2020 (Chapter XI); applicability threshold raised to ≥50 contract workers (was 20) |
| Factory definition | OSH Code 2020 §2(w); thresholds clarified |
| POSH | Continues unchanged under the 2013 Act (**not** in the 29-act repeal list) |
| Apprentices | Continues unchanged under the 1961 Act (not subsumed) |
| DPDP / data privacy | Continues under the DPDP Act 2023 |

## Key changes that affect Technijian operations

### 1. Uniform definition of "wages"

All four codes now use the **same definition** of *wages* (with minor variations). Critically:

- **Allowances cannot exceed 50% of total remuneration.** If they do, the excess is added back into "wages" for the purpose of EPF, ESI, gratuity, bonus and minimum-wage compliance.
- **Effect:** "Basic + DA" must be at least **50% of CTC**. Most Indian IT/ITeS pay structures historically had basic at 30–40%; these are now non-compliant. Tax-saving allowances (HRA, LTA, conveyance, special allowance) must be re-balanced.
- **Cost impact on Technijian:** EPF, gratuity, and leave-encashment payouts will **increase by 15–25%** when restructured to the 50% rule, because all three are calculated on basic + DA.

### 2. Higher thresholds for "establishment" classifications

| Threshold | Old | New (under codes) |
|---|---|---|
| Standing Orders applicability | ≥100 workers | **≥300 workers** |
| Government permission for retrenchment / closure | ≥100 workers | **≥300 workers** |
| Contract Labour Act applicability | ≥20 contract workers | **≥50 contract workers** |
| Factory definition | 10 workers w/ power, 20 w/o | Substantively unchanged (10 / 20) but definition tightened |

For Technijian's Indian offices (likely <300 workers per location), this means:
- No mandatory Standing Orders → continue to govern employment by **service rules / employee handbook + contract**.
- No prior Government permission needed for retrenchment, but notice + compensation rules apply.

### 3. Single registration & return regime

Each of the four codes provides for **one registration per code** and **simplified returns**, replacing dozens of separate registrations under the 29 repealed acts. Implementation depends on state web-portals being live.

### 4. Fixed-term employment (FTE) made universal

Under the IR Code:
- Fixed-term employees are entitled to the **same statutory benefits** as permanent staff (proportionate to service period).
- **Gratuity eligibility for FTEs is reduced from 5 years to 1 year.** Major change for Technijian's contractor-style hiring.
- No separate "contract worker" via a contractor needed for fixed-duration roles — direct FTE engagement is now legally favoured.

### 5. Social-security extension to gig & platform workers

The Social Security Code introduces a separate framework for **gig workers and platform workers** with fund contributions from aggregators. This is more relevant if Technijian engages independent contractors via aggregator platforms.

### 6. Women in night shifts — relaxed but conditional

The OSH Code expressly permits women to work between 7 PM and 6 AM **with consent and prescribed safety conditions**, formalising what was previously a state-level patchwork (see [04-haryana-panchkula/women-night-shift.md](04-haryana-panchkula/women-night-shift.md) for Haryana's 2025 notification details).

## What Technijian must do during the transition

| Action | Owner | Deadline / cadence |
|---|---|---|
| Audit pay structures to ensure basic + DA ≥ 50% of CTC | Finance + HR | Before 1-Apr-2026 (when central rules expected to crystallise) |
| Re-issue offer letters / appointment letters reflecting code-aligned compensation | HR | Rolling, on next salary revision cycle |
| Update HR policy manual references from old acts to corresponding code chapters | HR + Legal | One-time update |
| Subscribe to Haryana Labour Dept and Chandigarh Administration notification feeds | HR | Ongoing |
| Ensure ICC under POSH Act remains constituted (POSH not affected by codification) | HR | Continuing |
| Review fixed-term contract templates — gratuity now accrues from year 1 | Legal | Before next FTE issuance |
| Confirm registration validity under code regime (typically migrates from old S&E / EPF / ESI registrations) | HR + Compliance vendor | When state portals open |
| Audit contractor engagements vs. ≥50-worker threshold under OSH Code | HR | Quarterly |
