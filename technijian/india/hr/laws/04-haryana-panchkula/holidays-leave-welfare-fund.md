# Haryana — Holidays, Leave, and Labour Welfare Fund

**Last verified:** 2026-05-06.

This page consolidates two state-specific instruments operative in Haryana / Panchkula:

1. **Haryana Industrial Establishments (National and Festival Holidays) Act, 1971** — paid public holidays.
2. **Haryana Labour Welfare Fund Act, 1973** (operationalising the Punjab LWF Act 1965 in Haryana) — welfare-fund contributions.

For working hours and earned leave under the S&E Act, see [shops-and-establishments.md](shops-and-establishments.md).

---

## Part A — Haryana National & Festival Holidays Act, 1971

### Scope

- Applies to every "industrial establishment" in Haryana (which is broadly defined to include factories, shops, commercial establishments, motor transport undertakings, plantations, etc.).
- Technijian Panchkula office is squarely within scope.

### National holidays (mandatory) — 3

Every employee is entitled to **paid leave** on:

| Holiday | Date |
|---|---|
| Republic Day | 26 January |
| Independence Day | 15 August |
| Mahatma Gandhi Jayanti | 2 October |

These are non-derogable.

### Festival holidays (mandatory) — 5 (chosen from the notified list)

The Haryana Government notifies an annual list of festival holidays. The employer **selects 5 holidays** from the notified list to grant as paid leave. Common festivals on the Haryana list:

- Holi
- Ram Navami
- Mahavir Jayanti
- Good Friday
- Buddha Purnima
- Eid-ul-Fitr
- Janmashtami
- Eid-ul-Zuha (Bakrid)
- Independence Day (already national)
- Raksha Bandhan
- Dussehra (Vijayadashami)
- Diwali
- Govardhan Pooja
- Bhai Dooj
- Guru Nanak Jayanti
- Christmas

The Haryana list typically includes ~14–18 festival options each year; the employer publishes the chosen 5.

### Total mandatory paid public holidays in Haryana

**8 days per year** (3 national + 5 festival), as a minimum.

### Procedural requirements

- The list of holidays for the calendar year must be published at the establishment **before 31 December** of the preceding year.
- A copy of the holiday list is filed with the **Inspector of Shops / DLC, Panchkula**.
- Display in conspicuous part of the establishment.

### Working on a holiday

If an employee is required to work on a paid holiday:
- They must be paid **double the ordinary wage** for that day, **OR**
- Be granted a **substitute holiday with wages within 30 days**.

Employer's choice — but choice should be consistent.

### Fall on weekly off

If a holiday falls on a weekly off, no compensatory holiday is required (the holiday is "absorbed" by the off-day).

### Employee opt-out

In some cases, employees in shift / continuous-process establishments may opt to work on holidays for double pay. Document the opt-in.

### Penalties

- Default: fine up to **₹500** for first offence; doubled for repeat. Continuing offence: ₹50/day.

### Restricted holidays

In addition to the mandatory holidays, Haryana publishes a list of "restricted holidays" — employees may take any 2 from this list at their discretion. Employer-friendly: doesn't add cost; provides flexibility.

## Part B — Haryana Labour Welfare Fund Act, 1973

### Status

Continues in force; not subsumed by any of the four codes.

### Coverage

- Every "establishment" in Haryana with **10 or more employees**.
- Includes factories, shops, commercial establishments, motor transport, plantations, etc.
- Note: this differs from Chandigarh / Punjab where the threshold is **5+** employees. **Haryana threshold is 10+**.

### Contribution

| Party | Contribution per employee |
|---|---|
| **Employee** | **₹31 per month** (deducted from wages) |
| **Employer** | **₹62 per month** (per employee) |
| **Total per employee per month** | **₹93** |

> **Caveat**: Haryana revised the LWF rates upward in 2022 — verify current rates at hrylabour.gov.in. Older rates (pre-2022): employee ₹10, employer ₹20, total ₹30.

The contribution is computed on **a per-employee basis**, not as a percentage of wages.

Employees not covered:
- Persons employed primarily in managerial / supervisory capacity drawing wages above the prescribed ceiling (typically ₹15,000–₹25,000 — verify current).
- Persons employed in confidential capacity.

In practice, **most non-executive employees** are covered. Senior management may be exempt.

### Frequency

- Contributions accrue **monthly**.
- Remittance is **half-yearly**:
  - April – September period: deposited by **15-October**.
  - October – March period: deposited by **15-April**.

### Method of deposit

- Online: Haryana Labour Welfare Board portal (where active).
- Or challan-based at designated bank.
- Form A return submitted half-yearly.

### Welfare benefits funded

- Educational assistance (children's stipends, scholarships, books).
- Marriage assistance for daughters of registered workers.
- Maternity assistance.
- Medical assistance.
- Funeral / death benefit.
- Skill-development scholarships.

Employees apply directly to the Welfare Board for benefits (see [hrylabour.gov.in](https://hrylabour.gov.in/)).

### Penalties (§ 16)

- Failure to deposit: simple interest @ 12% p.a. + penalty up to **₹500** (which can become substantial under continuing-default rules).
- Falsification: prosecution.

### Practical implications for Technijian Panchkula

- Add ₹93/employee/month to total payroll cost (₹31 deducted, ₹62 employer's expense).
- Track in HRMS / payroll vendor system.
- Ensure half-yearly deposit by 15-Oct and 15-Apr.
- Maintain employee-wise LWF contribution register.
- File Form A return.
- Update HR policy manual to inform employees of benefits accessible via the Welfare Board.

## Comparison: Chandigarh vs Haryana on holidays + LWF

| Topic | Chandigarh UT | Haryana (Panchkula) |
|---|---|---|
| Mandatory national holidays | 3 (same) | 3 |
| Festival holidays | 4 | 5 |
| Total mandatory paid public holidays | 7 | **8** |
| LWF act | Punjab LWF Act 1965 (extended) | Haryana LWF Act 1973 |
| LWF threshold | ≥5 employees | **≥10 employees** |
| Employee LWF contribution | ₹5/month | **₹31/month** |
| Employer LWF contribution | ₹20/month | **₹62/month** |
| Total LWF | ₹25/employee/month | **₹93/employee/month** |
| LWF remittance frequency | Half-yearly | Half-yearly |

(Verify all figures at the relevant state portal; rates revised periodically.)
