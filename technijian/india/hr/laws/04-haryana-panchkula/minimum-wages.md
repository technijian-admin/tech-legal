# Haryana — Minimum Wages

**Last verified:** 2026-05-06.
**Operative notification:** Haryana Government, Labour Department, **Notification No. 2/25/26-2 Lab dated 9-April-2026**, fixing revised minimum wages effective **1-April-2026**.

---

## Legal basis

- **Code on Wages, 2019** (in force from 21-Nov-2025) — §6 and §8 — appropriate Government (Haryana) to fix and revise minimum rates of wages.
- Pre-code rules under the Minimum Wages Act 1948 (Haryana Minimum Wages Rules) **continue as transitional rules** until replaced.

The 9-April-2026 notification was issued explicitly under **§8(3) of the Code on Wages, 2019** — among the first major Haryana notifications under the new code.

---

## Rates effective 1-April-2026

Haryana applies a **single** statewide rate for each skill category (no zone-based variation).

| Skill category | Per-month minimum (₹) | Per-day minimum (₹) |
|---|---|---|
| **Unskilled** | **15,220.71** | 585.41 |
| **Semi-Skilled** | **16,780.74** | 645.41 |
| **Skilled** | **18,500.81** | 711.56 |
| **Highly-Skilled** | **19,425.85** | 747.14 |

These rates apply to **all establishments** (factories, shops, commercial establishments, contract labour) in Haryana, including Technijian's Panchkula office.

### Components

- **Basic** rate (per the table above).
- VDA component is built into these rates (the previous practice of separate Basic + VDA was consolidated for the April 2026 revision).

### Revision pattern

Historically, Haryana revises minimum wages **every 6 months** (effective 1 January and 1 July, or 1 April and 1 October), tracking CPI inflation. Under the Code on Wages, this pattern is expected to continue, with the maximum interval being **5 years** between revisions.

## Skill category definitions (Haryana)

The skill categorisation is published in Schedule to the Haryana minimum wages notification:

### Unskilled (₹15,220.71/month)

Simple manual labour requiring no formal training. Examples:
- Helper / peon / office attendant
- Cleaner / sweeper / housekeeping (direct or via contractor)
- Watchman (entry-level / unarmed)
- Field labour
- Warehouse loader / unloader

### Semi-Skilled (₹16,780.74/month)

Requires some training (school + on-the-job) and limited responsibility. Examples:
- Data-entry operator (non-IT)
- Filing clerk
- Driver (LMV)
- Junior assistant in office work
- Salesman / showroom assistant
- Junior security personnel

### Skilled (₹18,500.81/month)

Significant training; technical aptitude. Examples:
- Programmer / junior software developer
- Senior accountant / book-keeper
- Senior administrative assistant
- System administrator / IT support engineer
- Plumber / electrician (qualified)
- Senior driver (HCV / specialised)

### Highly-Skilled (₹19,425.85/month)

Extensive training, experience, decision-making responsibility. Examples:
- Senior software developer / architect
- Senior IT engineer
- Project lead (technical)
- Senior accountant (qualified CA / equivalent)
- Senior security officer / supervisor

For Technijian Panchkula, most engineers and senior professionals will fall in **Skilled** or **Highly-Skilled** category.

## Equal wages and trainees

### Equal wages (Code on Wages Chapter V)

- **No discrimination on grounds of gender** in wages or recruitment for the same / similar work.
- Re-titling roles to avoid this is itself an offence.

### Trainees

- Trainees may be paid at **75% of the relevant skill-category wage**.
- Cannot be paid less than the **Unskilled** rate.
- Training period: **maximum 1 year**.
- After 1 year, must be paid at full skill-category rate or formally be classified as an apprentice under the Apprentices Act 1961 (different scheme).

### No splitting of wages

The Haryana notification reaffirms that minimum wages **cannot be split** into allowances. The whole wage must be paid as the basic. This aligns with the Code on Wages §2(y) "wages" definition.

---

## Working hours and overtime

For minimum-wage employees in Haryana:
- **9 hours/day, 48 hours/week** maximum (per state S&E Act).
- **Overtime: at least double the ordinary rate** (Code on Wages §13).
- **OT cap: 50 hours per quarter**.

---

## Payment of wages

- Wage period: not exceeding **1 month**.
- Monthly wage: payable **before 7th** of next month.
- Mode: predominantly bank transfer / electronic for Technijian.
- Payslip: mandatory for each wage period.

---

## Penalties for under-payment

Under **§54 of the Code on Wages, 2019**:
- First offence: fine up to **₹50,000**.
- Repeat within 5 years: fine up to **₹1,00,000** + imprisonment up to 3 months.
- Failure to maintain wages register: fine up to **₹10,000**.

## Special considerations

### IT/ITeS sector — does Haryana have a separate IT/ITeS rate?

**No.** The 9-April-2026 Haryana notification applies a uniform set of skill-category rates across all sectors. There is **no separate IT/ITeS-specific minimum-wage notification** at present in Haryana.

Some other states (e.g., Karnataka, Tamil Nadu) maintain sector-specific schedules; Haryana's approach is sector-neutral.

### Contract labour parity

A contract worker engaged at a Technijian Panchkula office must be paid **at least the same rate** as a directly-employed worker doing similar work, under the OSH Code's contract-labour chapter (§§57+) and the Code on Wages Chapter V (equal remuneration). Contractors who pay less are in violation, and **principal-employer liability** flows back to Technijian.

### Apprentices

Apprentices' stipend is governed by the Apprentices Act 1961 (central) — minimum stipend rates are different (typically lower than minimum wage but with structured progression). See [02-central-laws/apprentices-and-child-labour.md](../02-central-laws/apprentices-and-child-labour.md).

### Wage definition (50% rule)

Under the Code on Wages §2(y) and §2 of the Social Security Code, the "wage" definition that cascades to:
- Minimum wage compliance,
- EPF contribution base,
- ESI contribution base,
- Gratuity calculation,
- Bonus calculation,
- Equal remuneration,

requires **Basic + DA ≥ 50% of total CTC**. Allowances exceeding 50% are added back to wages. Technijian's payroll structure must comply — see [02-central-laws/code-on-wages.md](../02-central-laws/code-on-wages.md).

## Practical compliance checklist for Technijian Panchkula

| # | Requirement | Reference |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Lowest-paid Panchkula employee earns ≥ ₹15,220.71/month (Unskilled) | 9-Apr-2026 notification |
| 2 | Skill-category mapping documented for each role (Unskilled / Semi-Skilled / Skilled / Highly-Skilled) | HR job description + payroll mapping |
| 3 | Trainees paid ≥ 75% of category rate (and ≥ Unskilled) | Code on Wages |
| 4 | Equal wages for men and women in similar roles | Chapter V |
| 5 | Wages not split into allowances | Notification |
| 6 | Basic + DA ≥ 50% of CTC | Code on Wages §2(y) |
| 7 | Wage register (Form G or code-aligned) maintained | Punjab S&E Rules / Code rules |
| 8 | Payslip issued for every wage period | Code on Wages |
| 9 | Monthly salary paid before 7th of next month | Code on Wages §17 |
| 10 | Overtime tracked: ≤50 hrs/quarter, paid at 2× rate | Code on Wages §13 |
| 11 | Re-verify rates each January/April/October cycle | Haryana practice |
