# Haryana — Women Employees in Night Shifts

**Last verified:** 2026-05-06.
**Primary instrument:** Haryana Notification dated **8-May-2025**, issued under the Punjab Shops and Commercial Establishments Act 1958 (as adopted by Haryana) and aligned with §43 of the OSH Code, 2020.

---

## Background

The Punjab Shops and Commercial Establishments Act, 1958 (default rule) **prohibits employment of women between 8 PM and 6 AM**.

Haryana amended **Rule 15 of the Punjab Shops and Commercial Establishments Rules, 1958** by notification dated **27-Sep-2003** to **exempt** specified sectors from the night-shift prohibition, subject to conditions. The exempted sectors then included:

- Information Technology (IT) establishments
- IT-Enabled Services (ITeS) establishments
- Banking establishments
- 3-star or higher hotels
- 100% Export-Oriented Units (EOUs)

Subsequent amendments tightened conditions. The most recent and operative version is the **8-May-2025 notification**, which:
- Adds **Logistics** and **Warehousing** to the exempted sectors.
- Updates conditions for safety, consent, transport, batch size, medical access.
- Brings POSH Act compliance front-and-centre as a precondition.

---

## Definition of "night shift"

For purposes of this notification: **8:00 PM to 6:00 AM** is the night shift period.

---

## Sectors eligible for the exemption (post 8-May-2025)

1. Information Technology (IT) establishments.
2. IT-Enabled Services (ITeS) establishments — includes BPOs, KPOs, technical support.
3. Banking establishments.
4. 3-star or higher hotels.
5. 100% Export-Oriented Units.
6. **Logistics establishments** (added in 2025).
7. **Warehousing establishments** (added in 2025).

Technijian's Panchkula IT/ITeS office is **eligible**.

---

## Conditions for exemption — full list

The following conditions must **all** be met. They apply continuously throughout the duration of the exemption.

### 1. Application to the Authority (advance permission)

- Apply to the **Labour Commissioner, Haryana / Chief Inspector of Shops** **at least 1 month** before the date on which the exemption is sought.
- Application form prescribed by the Labour Department.
- Exemption granted is valid for a period of **1 year** (renewable).

### 2. Written consent of the woman employee

- Each woman employed in night shift must give **written consent** in advance.
- Consent must be **specific to night-shift working** and **revocable** at any time without retaliation.
- Consent may be obtained at the time of joining (in offer letter / annexure) **but** the woman must retain the right to revoke for any specific shift / period.
- Employer must submit a declaration to the Labour Commissioner certifying that consent has been obtained.

### 3. Group of at least 4 women per night shift

- During night shift (8 PM – 6 AM), women employees must work in a **group of at least 4 women** (i.e., minimum 4 women on premises during any night shift).
- **Waiver for senior IT/ITeS women**: The 4-woman minimum is waived for **senior women employees in the IT/ITeS sector who earn over ₹1,00,000 per month**, provided they consent to night work.
  - This waiver is sector-specific (IT/ITeS only) and salary-based — the waiver does not extend to junior IT/ITeS women earning <₹1L/month, or to non-IT/ITeS sectors.

### 4. Lighting & illumination

- **Sufficient lighting** must be provided:
  - Inside the workplace premises;
  - In surrounding areas accessible to women (parking, walkways, restrooms, elevators);
  - Throughout the duration of the night shift.

### 5. Adequate security personnel

- **Security personnel** must be deployed:
  - Inside the workplace;
  - At entry/exit points;
  - In areas frequented by women (restrooms, cafeteria, parking).

### 6. Medical facility

- Either:
  - An **on-site doctor or female nurse** during night shift, OR
  - A **tie-up with a nearby hospital** for emergencies, with documented coordination.
- Either arrangement must be operational throughout the night shift.

### 7. Anti-sexual-harassment compliance

- The employer must **deter sexual harassment** at the workplace.
- The **POSH Act 2013** must be **fully complied with**:
  - Constituted Internal Committee (IC) at the establishment.
  - IC members trained.
  - All employees (men and women) sensitised.
  - Posted IC contact details.
  - Annual Report filed.
- Without functioning POSH compliance, the women's-night-shift exemption can be **revoked**.

### 8. Transport

- Although not always specified in headline, it has been treated as essential and is generally required by Labour Department under safety conditions:
  - **Door-to-door transport** for women employees during night shift, both home → office and office → home.
  - The transport must be **employer-arranged** (or via approved vendor with verified driver background).

### 9. Restroom & rest facility

- Separate, secure women's restrooms.
- Designated rest area with seating.
- Locker / personal storage.

### 10. Records

- Roster / shift schedule maintained.
- Consent forms maintained.
- Travel logs maintained (if employer-arranged transport).
- Security incident logs.

## Common pitfalls

- **Implicit consent**: Asking a woman to "tick the box" in the joining-form is not enough. Specific, informed consent for night-shift work, with right to revoke, is required.
- **Forgetting renewal**: Exemption is valid 1 year. Set a calendar reminder at T+11 months.
- **Single-woman night shift**: Even with consent, having a single woman alone in the office during night shift is not compliant unless she meets the senior-IT/ITeS-₹1L+ waiver.
- **Hospital tie-up not formalised**: Verbal arrangement with a nearby hospital is not sufficient — documented tie-up letter required.
- **POSH IC constituted but not active**: A non-functional IC (no meetings, no annual report) can void the night-shift exemption.
- **Cab driver not background-verified**: Many incidents have been traced to outsourced transport vendors. Background verification is implicit in the safety conditions.

---

## Penalties

Failure to comply with the conditions can result in:
- **Revocation of the exemption certificate** by the Labour Commissioner.
- Penalty under §26 of the Punjab S&E Act (₹500–₹2,000 + daily continuing penalty).
- Penal action under §43 of OSH Code 2020 (which mandates safety conditions for women's night work).
- POSH Act penalties (₹50,000) if IC default surfaces.
- Civil suit / industrial dispute if a woman employee is injured during night shift due to non-compliance.

---

## Related provisions in OSH Code 2020

The OSH Code §43 (central) provides the enabling framework that:
- Permits women to work between 7 PM and 6 AM (note: state rule starts at 8 PM, central from 7 PM — comply with the more restrictive of the two; for Haryana, the **8 PM threshold of the state notification** is the operative cut-off).
- Requires consent.
- Requires safety measures, holiday & shift policy.
- Restricts hazardous processes.

The state notification is the operational rulebook for Haryana implementation.
