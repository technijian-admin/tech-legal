Dear Mr. Uniyal,

This letter is issued with reference to your Offer / Appointment Letter dated 10 May 2022, your Confirmation Letter on file with the Company, and the provisions of the Industrial Relations Code, 2020 ("IR Code") that govern your employment with Technijian IT Services Pvt. Ltd. (the "Company").

## 1. Employment Status

1.01 You joined the Company on **10 May 2022** as **Client Support Engineer**, and your services were duly confirmed by way of a written Confirmation Letter, which is recorded in the Company's "Confirmation Letters Given" register on file with HR.

1.02 As on the date of this letter, you have rendered approximately **four (4) years** of continuous service with the Company within the meaning of Section 2(zh) of the IR Code.

1.03 You are a "workman" within the meaning of Section 2(zr) of the IR Code, and the provisions of Sections 70 and 83 of the IR Code apply to your separation.

## 2. Business Rationale — *Bona Fide* Cost-Reduction Retrenchment

2.01 Following the operational impact of the cyber-security incident of September 2024 (the "Malware Incident"), the Company experienced sustained business disruption that materially affected its India delivery function.

2.02 In Q4 2024 and Q1 2025, the Company suffered the loss of **four (4) major client accounts**, resulting in a sustained reduction in billable revenue at the India operating unit.

2.03 In light of the foregoing, the Board of Directors has determined that the present India headcount is unsustainable and has resolved upon a structural rightsizing of the India delivery function as a *bona fide* cost-reduction measure. This is a recognised and lawful ground for retrenchment under Section 70 of the IR Code, well-supported by the principles set out by the Hon'ble Supreme Court in *Workmen of Sudder Office, Cinnamara v. Management* (1971) 4 SCC 26, *Hindustan Lever Ltd. v. Their Workmen* (1973) 1 SCC 813, *Western India Match Co. Ltd. v. Workmen* (1973) 2 SCC 105 and *Parry & Co. Ltd. v. P.C. Pal* (1970) AIR 1334.

2.04 As a result of this rightsizing, the position you presently occupy is being eliminated.

## 3. Notice of Retrenchment

3.01 Pursuant to Section 70(a) of the IR Code, this letter constitutes **one (1) month's written notice of retrenchment**, with the Company providing you **twenty-three (23) days served as paid notice from 09 May 2026 to 31 May 2026**, plus an additional **seven (7) days' salary in lieu of notice** to ensure full satisfaction of the one-month notice requirement.

3.02 The effective date of retrenchment ("Termination Date") is close of business on **31 May 2026**.

3.03 You are placed on paid leave-of-absence ("paid LOA") from **09 May 2026** through the Termination Date. During this paid LOA you are required to take up to twenty-five (25) days of your accrued Earned Leave balance, in the manner advised by HR. Any residual EL balance shall be encashed in the F&F under Section 6 below.

3.04 Your Company email access, VPN, and physical premises access will stand revoked with effect from **09 May 2026**.

3.05 The Company has, contemporaneously with this notice, served notice upon the appropriate Government / Labour Department under Section 70(c) of the IR Code in respect of this retrenchment.

## 4. Retrenchment Compensation under Section 70(b)

4.01 Pursuant to Section 70(b) of the IR Code, you are entitled to retrenchment compensation equal to **fifteen (15) days' average pay for every completed year of continuous service or any part thereof in excess of six months**.

4.02 Calculation:

| Component | Value |
|---|---:|
| Last drawn monthly salary (gross) | ₹38,585 |
| Completed years of continuous service (10 May 2022 → 31 May 2026) | 4 years |
| Retrenchment compensation = ₹38,585 ÷ 2 × 4 | **₹77,170** |

(Computed on the simplified statutory formula equivalent to 15 days' wages per completed year. Final amount may be recomputed on Basic + Dearness Allowance pursuant to the definition of "wages" under Section 2(zzr) of the IR Code; any adjustment, if applicable, will be reflected in the F&F.)

## 5. Re-skilling Fund Contribution under Section 83

5.01 Pursuant to Section 83 of the IR Code, the Company shall, within the prescribed period, contribute to the Worker Re-skilling Fund an amount equal to **fifteen (15) days' wages last drawn by you immediately before the date of your retrenchment**.

5.02 Calculation:

| Component | Value |
|---|---:|
| Daily wage = ₹38,585 ÷ 30 | ₹1,286.17 |
| Re-skilling Fund contribution = 15 × ₹1,286.17 | **₹19,293** |

5.03 This contribution is paid by the Company to the Government-administered Worker Re-skilling Fund and does not form part of the F&F payable to you.
