# Punjab Shops and Commercial Establishments Act, 1958 — As Adopted by Haryana

**Last verified:** 2026-05-06.

The Punjab Shops and Commercial Establishments Act, 1958 was adopted by Haryana at its formation in 1966 and continues as the **primary state-level statute** governing Technijian's Panchkula office. Haryana has subsequently amended the Act and Rules (notably Rule 15 on women's night-shift work) by state notifications.

For the equivalent Chandigarh UT page, see [03-chandigarh-ut/shops-and-establishments.md](../03-chandigarh-ut/shops-and-establishments.md). Most provisions are similar; this page highlights **Haryana-specific differences and recent notifications**.

---

## Coverage

Same definitions of "commercial establishment" and "shop" as in Chandigarh — Technijian's Panchkula office is a **commercial establishment**.

### Excluded establishments

- Premises in a factory under the Factories Act 1948 / OSH Code 2020.
- Government / Reserve Bank.
- Religious / charitable trusts.
- Some classes of railway / postal premises.

---

## Registration (§§ 13–14)

### Timeline

- Within **30 days** of starting business in Haryana.

### Form & filing

- **Form A** (Statement to be furnished by Employer of Establishment) + supporting documents.
- Filed with the **Inspector of Shops, Haryana / District Labour Commissioner, Panchkula**.
- Online portal: [hrylabour.gov.in](https://hrylabour.gov.in/) (Online Services → Establishment Registration).

### Documents

- Identity / address proof of employer.
- PAN of establishment.
- Lease deed / property-ownership document.
- Photo of establishment.
- Manager's appointment letter (if any).
- Employee count + designation list.

### Fee

- Sliding scale by employee count:
  - 1–10 employees: ₹100
  - 11–25 employees: ₹500
  - 26–50 employees: ₹1,000
  - 51–100 employees: ₹2,000
  - 101–500 employees: ₹5,000
  - 501+ employees: ₹10,000
- Verify current fee schedule at hrylabour.gov.in.

### Validity

- Issued for **3 years** (§14 as amended by Haryana).
- Renewal (Form B) before **31-March** of the year of expiry.

### Notification of changes (§14(3))

- Change in particulars (address, ownership, headcount, name) must be notified within **15 days**.

### Display

- Registration certificate must be **prominently displayed** at the establishment.

---

## Working Hours

### General rule (§7)

- **9 hours/day, 48 hours/week** maximum.
- Spread-over (including breaks) ≤ **12 hours/day**.

### Rest interval (§8)

- 30 minutes after 5 hours of continuous work.

### Overtime (§9)

- Beyond 9/day or 48/week.
- Paid at **at least twice** the ordinary wage rate (Code on Wages 2019).
- Maximum overtime: **50 hours per quarter**.

### Opening / closing time

- General rule: **No establishment shall open before 9 AM nor close after 7:45 PM** (Haryana notification).
- IT/ITeS, banking, hotels (3-star+), 100% EOUs, logistics & warehousing (per 2025 update) and similar 24x7 sectors are **exempted** from the fixed opening/closing time.
- April 2026: Haryana Government further relaxed operating hours for shops and establishments — see Haryana Labour notification (April 2026).

### IT/ITeS implication

A Technijian Panchkula IT/ITeS office can operate **24x7** if it complies with:
- Maximum 9-hour individual day, 48-hour week (per employee).
- Rest interval, weekly off.
- Specific Haryana exemption notification on opening hours (verify current).
- Women's night-shift conditions (see [women-night-shift.md](women-night-shift.md)).

---

## Weekly Off (§11)

- Every employee gets at least **one full day off** per week.
- For a 24x7 establishment, the day off can be staggered — different employees off on different days, but the establishment's own closure rule (one day's closure per week unless exempted) requires care.
- IT/ITeS exempted from establishment-closure rule with continuous-process exemption.

---

## Annual / Earned Leave (§§ 28–30)

- Eligibility: **240 days of work** in a calendar year.
- Quantum: **1 day per 20 days worked** ≈ 15 days/year.
- Carry-forward: max **30 days** accumulated.
- Encashment on cessation: mandatory for unused leave.

---

## Sick Leave & Casual Leave

Per Punjab S&E Rules as adopted in Haryana:
- **Sick leave**: 7 days/year (with medical certificate for >2 days).
- **Casual leave**: 7 days/year.
- **Earned / privilege leave**: 15 days/year (as above).

These minima can be exceeded by employer policy; Technijian's HR handbook should specify the actual entitlement.

---

## Public Holidays

Governed by the **Haryana Industrial Establishments (National and Festival Holidays) Act, 1971** (separate Haryana act). See [holidays-leave-welfare-fund.md](holidays-leave-welfare-fund.md).

| Type | Mandatory holidays |
|---|---|
| **National holidays** | 3 — Republic Day, Independence Day, Gandhi Jayanti |
| **Festival holidays** (employer chooses from notified list) | 4–5 (more than Punjab; Haryana lists slightly higher count) |
| **Restricted holidays** (employee may opt) | Several |

Total mandatory paid public holidays: typically **8 per year** in Haryana.

The list is notified annually by Haryana Labour Department; specific dates depend on lunar calendar.

If an employee works on a public holiday: pay double wage OR substitute holiday within 30 days.

---

## Women — Night Shifts

The default rule of the Punjab S&E Act (extended to Haryana) prohibits women from working between **8 PM and 6 AM**. Haryana has amended Rule 15 of the Punjab S&E Rules to **exempt** specified sectors (including IT/ITeS) **subject to conditions and consent**.

The latest Haryana notification is dated **8-May-2025** and significantly tightens / clarifies the conditions (see [women-night-shift.md](women-night-shift.md)).

---

## Children / Adolescents

- Below 14: not employable in any role.
- 14–18: can work in non-hazardous roles only; max 6 hours/day; no work between 7 PM and 8 AM (state rule); annual medical certificate.

## Records & Registers

| Register | Form | Subject |
|---|---|---|
| Register of employment & wages | Form G | Names, designations, hours, wages |
| Register of leave | Form H | Leave earned, taken, balance |
| Register of fines | Form K | Fines imposed |
| Notice of weekly closure | Form (display) | Day(s) of closure |
| Register of accidents | OSH-aligned | Accidents, dangerous occurrences |

---

## Inspections

The Chief Inspector of Shops Haryana and District Labour Commissioners conduct inspections. Under the OSH Code, inspections are now **risk-based and randomised** ("Inspector-cum-Facilitator" model).

---

## Penalties (§ 26)

| Default | Penalty |
|---|---|
| Failure to register | ₹500–₹2,000 + ₹100/day continued |
| Excess hours / no weekly off | ₹500–₹2,000 (first); doubled (repeat) |
| Failure to grant leave | ₹500–₹1,000 |
| False statements | ₹1,000 + imprisonment 1 month |
| Obstruction | ₹500 |

---

## April-2026 relaxation of operating hours

The Government of Haryana (April 2026) granted **exemptions from fixed working hours** under the Shops & Establishments Act for additional categories. Verify the latest notification at [hrylabour.gov.in](https://hrylabour.gov.in/) for current scope and any IT/ITeS-specific extensions.
