# Haryana State Employment of Local Candidates Act, 2020 — STATUS: STRUCK DOWN

**Last verified:** 2026-05-06.
**Status (operative):** **NOT IN FORCE.** The Punjab and Haryana High Court declared the Act **unconstitutional** by judgment dated **17-November-2023**. Status quo as of 2026-05: not enforceable; Haryana Government appeal to the Supreme Court, if any, has not resulted in a stay or reversal as of the verification date.

---

## Why Technijian needs to know about this Act

The Haryana State Employment of Local Candidates Act, 2020 (HSELC Act) was enacted to **mandate 75% of new private-sector jobs (paying ≤ ₹30,000/month) to local Haryana candidates**. The Act was politically controversial, would have imposed significant compliance burdens on private employers, and was challenged in court immediately upon notification.

Even though it has been struck down, this page documents the act and its current status because:
- The Haryana Government may seek to revive it through legislative amendment or appeal.
- Other Indian states (notably Andhra Pradesh, Madhya Pradesh, Karnataka) have considered similar legislation.
- HR teams may receive enquiries (or face commercial pressure) about "local hiring" that draw on the spirit of this Act.

---

## What the Act provided (history, for record)

### Key provisions

- **75% reservation** of "new employments" with gross monthly wages up to **₹30,000** for **Haryana domiciles** in:
  - Companies, societies, trusts, partnership firms, LLPs.
  - With principal place of business / registered branch in Haryana.
  - Engaging 10 or more persons.
- **Domicile** = a person who has been a resident of Haryana for 15 years or registered with employment exchange in Haryana.
- **Compliance**:
  - Employers had to register on a designated portal.
  - Submit quarterly reports of local-hire ratios.
  - Recruit through the state employment exchange.
- **Exemptions**: Limited; agricultural workers, domestic servants, teachers, etc.

### Penalties (under the Act, when in force)

- Per-employee non-compliance: **₹10,000 to ₹2 lakh**.
- Continuing offence: ₹1,000 per day per offence.
- Cancellation of registration / blacklisting.

### Effective date (as originally notified)

- Notified to come into force on **15-Jan-2022**.
- Implementation phased: registration window opened, compliance reporting was due to begin in 2022.

## Litigation history

### Constitutional challenge

- Industry bodies (Faridabad Industries Association, Manesar Industries Welfare Association, etc.) filed writ petitions before the **Punjab and Haryana High Court** challenging the Act on multiple grounds:
  1. **Article 14** — equality before law; differentiating by domicile is impermissible classification.
  2. **Article 16(2)** — no discrimination in employment based on residence (the central caveat in Article 16(3) permits Parliament — not states — to make limited residential reservations).
  3. **Article 19(1)(g)** — right to practice any profession, occupation, trade, or business; the Act unreasonably restricted private employers' freedom of choice.
  4. **Federal scheme** — interference with central regulation of inter-state migration of labour.

### High Court judgment (17-November-2023)

The P&H High Court (a 2-judge bench) **struck down the entire Act as unconstitutional**, holding:

- The Act violated **Articles 14 and 16(2)** of the Constitution.
- "Domicile" alone is **not a valid or reasonable classification** for reservation in private employment.
- The Supreme Court's prior rulings consistently reject domicile-based reservations except in narrow circumstances under Article 16(3) (which is reserved for **central** laws and only for **public** employment).
- The Act was held to be **ineffective from the date it came into force** (i.e., the strike-down was retrospective / declaratory, not just prospective).

### Supreme Court appeal

- Haryana State Government indicated intent to appeal.
- As of 2026-05-06, **no Supreme Court ruling has reversed or stayed** the P&H HC decision. The Act remains struck down.
- It is theoretically possible that:
  - Supreme Court may grant a stay (allowing partial enforcement during appeal).
  - Supreme Court may reverse the P&H HC (reviving the Act).
  - Haryana legislature may re-enact a modified version.

Until such an event, the Act is **not enforceable**.

## What if the Act is revived?

Should the Supreme Court reverse the P&H HC, OR Haryana re-enacts the law, the following compliance actions become live:

1. Register on the Haryana State Employment portal.
2. Identify all employees with gross monthly wages ≤₹30,000 (likely junior IT/ITeS, support, admin).
3. Track Haryana-domicile vs non-Haryana-domicile in this band.
4. Prefer Haryana domiciles in new hiring up to 75% of new positions in this wage band.
5. File quarterly reports on portal.
6. Maintain hiring records demonstrating bona fide effort.
7. Apply for **exemption** if local candidates of required skill are unavailable.

## Why this hasn't recurred (so far)

The 2023 P&H HC reasoning is well-grounded in established constitutional jurisprudence (e.g., *Pradeep Jain v. Union of India*, *Ahmedabad Municipal Corporation v. Sandya*). For a state-level domicile reservation in private employment to survive constitutional challenge, the legislature would need to:
- Re-frame the basis (perhaps tying it to "local skill development" rather than pure domicile).
- Limit it to specific sectors with documented pressing social need.
- Provide robust exemption / availability-of-skill mechanism.

Such re-engineering is non-trivial. Most policy observers consider the law unlikely to be revived in its 2020 form.
