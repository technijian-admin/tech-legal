# Jurisdictional Map — Which Laws Apply Where

**Last verified:** 2026-05-06

For Technijian operations in **Chandigarh (UT)** and **Panchkula (Haryana)**, three layers of law operate concurrently:

1. **Central (national) laws** — apply uniformly across India.
2. **State / UT laws** — apply to establishments situated in that state/UT. Chandigarh UT and Haryana have **distinct** state-level instruments even though they share a border.
3. **Local rules and notifications** — issued by the labour commissioner / chief inspector of shops / municipal authority of the specific jurisdiction.

---

## Geographic context

| Location | Political status | Labour-law regime |
|---|---|---|
| **Chandigarh** | Union Territory (capital of both Punjab and Haryana, but administered directly by Government of India through an Administrator) | Central laws + **Punjab Acts as extended to Chandigarh** + Chandigarh Administration notifications |
| **Panchkula** | District in Haryana state, immediately east of Chandigarh | Central laws + **Haryana state acts** (many derived from the Punjab base, with Haryana amendments) |
| **Mohali** *(reference only)* | District in Punjab state, immediately west of Chandigarh | Central laws + Punjab state acts (out of scope for this knowledge base, included only because the tri-city area is sometimes treated as one labour market) |

> **Important:** Although Chandigarh, Panchkula and Mohali form a contiguous metropolitan area, they sit in **three different labour jurisdictions**. An establishment is governed by the laws of the location where it is **physically registered**, not where its employees reside. A Chandigarh-registered office that hires a Panchkula-resident employee follows **Chandigarh** rules; a Panchkula-registered office follows **Haryana** rules.

## Layer 1 — Central laws (apply in both Chandigarh & Panchkula)

### The Four Labour Codes (in force from 21-Nov-2025)

| Code | Subsumes | Detailed page |
|---|---|---|
| **Code on Wages, 2019** | Minimum Wages Act 1948, Payment of Wages Act 1936, Payment of Bonus Act 1965, Equal Remuneration Act 1976 | [02-central-laws/code-on-wages.md](02-central-laws/code-on-wages.md) |
| **Industrial Relations Code, 2020** | Industrial Disputes Act 1947, Trade Unions Act 1926, Industrial Employment (Standing Orders) Act 1946 | [02-central-laws/industrial-relations-code.md](02-central-laws/industrial-relations-code.md) |
| **Code on Social Security, 2020** | EPF & Misc. Provisions Act 1952, ESI Act 1948, Payment of Gratuity Act 1972, Maternity Benefit Act 1961, Employees' Compensation Act 1923, and 4 more | [02-central-laws/social-security-code.md](02-central-laws/social-security-code.md) |
| **Occupational Safety, Health and Working Conditions Code, 2020 (OSH Code)** | Factories Act 1948, Contract Labour Act 1970, Inter-State Migrant Workmen Act 1979, and 10 more | [02-central-laws/osh-code.md](02-central-laws/osh-code.md) |

### Special central acts that survive the codification

| Act | Why it still applies |
|---|---|
| **Sexual Harassment of Women at Workplace (POSH) Act, 2013** | Not subsumed; mandatory ICC for any employer with ≥10 employees |
| **Apprentices Act, 1961** | Survives; mandatory apprenticeship engagement once ≥30 workers |
| **Child Labour (Prohibition and Regulation) Act, 1986** (as amended 2016) | Survives; total ban under 14, restricted 14–18 |
| **Maternity Benefit Act, 1961** (substantive entitlements) | Subsumed under Social Security Code, but most rules carry forward; 26-week paid leave continues |
| **Trade Unions Act, 1926** | Subsumed under IR Code |
| **Industrial Employment (Standing Orders) Act, 1946** | Subsumed under IR Code |
| **Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023** | Applies to processing of HR data (employee personal information) |
| **Income-tax Act, 1961** (TDS on salaries — out of scope) | TDS, salary structuring; coordinate with finance |
| **Information Technology Act, 2000** (peripheral) | Workplace electronic communications; cybersecurity duties |

## Layer 2 — Chandigarh Union Territory–specific laws

Chandigarh UT does not have its own labour legislature. Instead, **Punjab state acts have been extended to Chandigarh** with subsequent Chandigarh-Administration notifications.

| Instrument | What it governs | Detailed page |
|---|---|---|
| **Punjab Shops and Commercial Establishments Act, 1958** (as extended to Chandigarh UT) | Working hours, weekly off, leave, registration of every shop/commercial establishment | [03-chandigarh-ut/shops-and-establishments.md](03-chandigarh-ut/shops-and-establishments.md) |
| **Punjab Labour Welfare Fund Act, 1965** (as extended) | Statutory contributions to Labour Welfare Fund | [03-chandigarh-ut/minimum-wages-and-welfare-fund.md](03-chandigarh-ut/minimum-wages-and-welfare-fund.md) |
| **Chandigarh Industrial Disputes (Procedure) Rules, 1968** | Procedural framework for industrial disputes (now to be read with IR Code 2020) | [03-chandigarh-ut/README.md](03-chandigarh-ut/README.md) |
| **Chandigarh Administration Notifications under Code on Wages** | Local minimum wages notified by Chandigarh UT Administration | [03-chandigarh-ut/minimum-wages-and-welfare-fund.md](03-chandigarh-ut/minimum-wages-and-welfare-fund.md) |
| **National & Festival Holidays Act, 1965** (Punjab; extended) | Mandatory paid holidays | [03-chandigarh-ut/shops-and-establishments.md](03-chandigarh-ut/shops-and-establishments.md) |

UT registrations:
- **EPFO** Regional Office, Chandigarh
- **ESIC** Sub-Regional Office, Chandigarh
- **Labour Department, Chandigarh Administration** (Shops & Establishments)
- **Chandigarh Administration, Industrial Tribunal–cum–Labour Court**

## Decision tree — "Which act do I check?"

```
Is the question about ...
├── Wages, bonus, equal pay, payment timing?
│   └── Code on Wages 2019 + state min-wage notification (Chandigarh UT or Haryana)
│
├── Hours of work, weekly off, leave, holidays?
│   └── State S&E Act (Punjab S&E Act extended/adopted) + Holidays Act
│       (NOT the OSH Code — IT/ITeS offices are commercial establishments, not factories)
│
├── EPF / ESI / gratuity / maternity?
│   └── Code on Social Security 2020 (administered by EPFO/ESIC under that code)
│
├── Termination, retrenchment, lay-off, standing orders?
│   └── Industrial Relations Code 2020
│       (Note: white-collar IT/ITeS staff above ₹18,000/month wages are usually
│        outside the "worker" definition and get governed by contract + S&E Act)
│
├── Sexual harassment / ICC / workplace safety for women?
│   └── POSH Act 2013 + state-level rules
│
├── Women working night shifts (8 PM – 6 AM)?
│   ├── Chandigarh: Punjab S&E Rules (extended) + Chandigarh notifications
│   └── Panchkula: Haryana notification dated 8-May-2025 (IT/ITeS exemption applies)
│
├── Apprentices / interns?
│   └── Apprentices Act 1961 (mandatory once ≥30 workers)
│
├── Contract / fixed-term staff or contractor labour?
│   └── OSH Code 2020 (replaces Contract Labour Act) + state contract-labour rules
│
├── Employee personal data / HR records / monitoring?
│   └── Digital Personal Data Protection Act 2023
│
└── Disputes (employee files claim with labour commissioner / tribunal)?
    └── Industrial Relations Code 2020 — adjudication via state tribunal
```
