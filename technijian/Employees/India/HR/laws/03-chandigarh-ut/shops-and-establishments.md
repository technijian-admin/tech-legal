# Punjab Shops and Commercial Establishments Act, 1958 — As Applied to Chandigarh UT

**Last verified:** 2026-05-06.

The Punjab Shops and Commercial Establishments Act, 1958 (the "Punjab S&E Act") was extended to the Union Territory of Chandigarh shortly after Chandigarh was carved out of Punjab in 1966. It is the **primary state-level statute** governing Technijian's Chandigarh office.

---

## Coverage

### What is a "commercial establishment"? (§2(4))

> "Commercial establishment" means any premises wherein any business, trade or profession is carried on for profit and includes journalistic and printing establishments, contractors and auditors' establishments, quarries and mines not governed by the Mines Act, educational and other institutions run for private gain, and premises in which business of banking, insurance, stocks and shares, brokerage or produce exchange is carried on, but does not include a shop or commercial establishment attached to a factory.

A typical IT/ITeS office (software development, BPO, support) is squarely within this definition.

### What is a "shop"? (§2(34))

> "Shop" means any premises where goods are sold either by retail or wholesale, or where services are rendered to customers and includes an office, a store-room, godown, warehouse, or workhouse or work place...

A retail showroom, a service outlet, etc.

### Combined effect

Either as a "shop" (where services are rendered) or "commercial establishment", a Technijian office in Chandigarh must register under this Act.

### Exemptions

- Government / Government-owned offices.
- Premises that are part of a factory under the Factories Act 1948 / OSH Code 2020.
- Religious institutions.
- Establishments of the Reserve Bank of India.
- Some classes of railway / postal premises.

## Registration (§13–14)

### When to register

- **Within 30 days of starting business** (§14(1)).
- The employer files Form A (Statement to be furnished by Employer of Establishment) along with the prescribed fee.

### What's filed

Form A includes:
- Name of the employer.
- Name and postal address of the establishment.
- Names of the manager (if any).
- Number of persons employed.
- Category of establishment (shop / commercial establishment).
- Date of opening.
- Such other particulars as may be prescribed.

### Where to file

- Inspector of Shops, Chandigarh Administration — physical filing OR
- Chandigarh Labour Department online portal: [chandigarh.gov.in/labour](https://chandigarh.gov.in/) (link via Department of Labour). Verify portal URL before relying.

### Fee structure

- Sliding scale based on number of employees:
  - 1–10 employees: ~₹100–₹500 per registration period.
  - 11–25 employees: ~₹500–₹1,000.
  - 26–50 employees: ~₹1,000–₹2,000.
  - 51+ employees: ~₹2,000+ (verify current fee — revised periodically by Chandigarh Administration).

### Registration certificate (§14(2))

- A registration certificate is issued by the Inspector.
- Must be **prominently displayed** at the establishment.

### Validity & renewal

- Registration is valid for **3 years** (per Punjab S&E Act amendments; Chandigarh follows the same).
- Must be **renewed before 31 March** of the year of expiry.
- Late renewal attracts late fees and may lead to penalty proceedings.

### Notice of changes (§14(3))

Employer must notify any change (closure, change of ownership, change of address, change in headcount) **within 15 days**.

## Working Hours

### Maximum hours (§7)

- **Maximum 9 hours per day**.
- **Maximum 48 hours per week**.

### Spread-over (§7(2))

- Total spread-over (including rest intervals) **shall not exceed 12 hours per day**.

### Rest intervals (§8)

- After **5 hours of continuous work**, a rest interval of **at least 30 minutes**.

### Overtime (§9)

- Hours beyond 9/day or 48/week are overtime.
- Overtime wage = **at least twice the ordinary wage rate** (per the Code on Wages 2019, harmonised across India).
- Overtime cannot exceed **50 hours in any quarter** (under Punjab S&E Rules).

### Opening / closing time (§10)

- **No establishment shall open before 9 AM or close after 8 PM** (general rule).
- Some classes (chemists, hospitals, hotels, telecom, IT/ITeS, transport, aviation) are exempted by notification.
- IT/ITeS in Chandigarh has historically been granted exemption from fixed opening/closing hours, in line with Punjab notifications.

---

## Weekly Off (§11)

- Every employee must be allowed **at least one full day off** in a week.
- Establishment must remain closed for at least one day in a week (unless exempted as a continuous-process / 24x7 establishment, common for IT/ITeS).
- Wages cannot be deducted for the weekly off day.

---

## National & Festival Holidays

The Punjab Industrial Establishments (National and Festival Holidays) Act, 1965 (extended to Chandigarh) requires:

| Category | Number of paid holidays per year |
|---|---|
| **National holidays** (mandatory) | **3** — Republic Day (26 Jan), Independence Day (15 Aug), Mahatma Gandhi Jayanti (2 Oct) |
| **Festival holidays** (selected from the notified list) | **4** — chosen by the employer from the state list of festivals |
| **Total** | **7 paid holidays per year** |

The festival list is notified annually by Chandigarh Administration. Common festival holidays:
- Holi
- Ram Navami
- Mahavir Jayanti
- Good Friday
- Buddha Purnima
- Eid-ul-Fitr
- Eid-ul-Zuha
- Janmashtami
- Dussehra (Vijayadashami)
- Diwali
- Guru Nanak Jayanti
- Christmas

The employer must publish the list of holidays for the calendar year **at the establishment** before 31 December of the previous year.

If a holiday falls on a weekly off, no compensatory holiday is required.

If an employee works on a holiday, they are entitled to **double wages** OR **a substitute holiday** within 30 days.

## Annual Leave with Wages (Earned / Privilege Leave) (§§ 28–30)

### Eligibility

- Every employee who has worked for **at least 240 days** in a calendar year is entitled to leave with wages.

### Quantum

- **1 day for every 20 days of work** (≈ 5% of attendance) → maximum ~15 days per year.
- For young persons (under 18): **1 day for every 15 days** → ~24 days/year.

### Carry-forward / accumulation

- Maximum **30 days** of carry-forward.
- Excess lapses unless encashed.
- Encashment on resignation / termination is mandatory for unused leave.

### Application & approval

- Apply in advance (typically **at least 15 days before** for non-emergency).
- Employer may refuse only on bona fide grounds (e.g., business exigency).

---

## Sick Leave & Casual Leave (state rules)

The Punjab S&E Act (and corresponding Chandigarh rules) provides:

| Leave type | Quantum |
|---|---|
| **Sick leave (with pay)** | **7 days** per year (with medical certificate for >2 days) |
| **Casual leave (with pay)** | **7 days** per year |
| **Privilege / Earned leave (with pay)** | As above, ~15 days/year on full attendance |

These minima can be exceeded by employer's policy. Technijian's HR handbook should specify total annual leave entitlement at or above these thresholds.

## Special provisions

### Women's working hours (§14, Punjab S&E Rules)

- Earlier rule: women not employed before 6 AM or after 8 PM.
- **Exemption** for IT/ITeS establishments granted by amendment to Rule 15 of Punjab S&E Rules 1958 (notification dated 27-Sep-2003), allowing women to work between 8 PM and 6 AM with safeguards.
- For Chandigarh UT: similar exemption notifications by Chandigarh Administration historically issued. Verify current notification before relying.
- Conditions (similar to Haryana 8-May-2025 notification — see [04-haryana-panchkula/women-night-shift.md](../04-haryana-panchkula/women-night-shift.md)):
  - Written consent of the woman.
  - Group of at least 4 women per shift (waivable for senior IT/ITeS women earning ≥₹1 lakh/month with consent).
  - Adequate lighting, security, transport (employer-provided to / from home).
  - Medical facility (on-site doctor or tie-up with hospital).
  - POSH Act compliance (functioning IC).

### Children / young persons (§§22–24)

- No employment below 14 years (echoes the Child Labour Act).
- Adolescents (14–18): max 6 hours / day; no work between 7 PM and 8 AM (subject to OSH Code central provisions).

## Records & Registers (§§ 31–33)

| Register | Content | Form |
|---|---|---|
| **Register of employment & wages** | Names, designations, hours worked, wages | Form G |
| **Register of leave with wages** | Leaves earned, taken, balance | Form H |
| **Notice of weekly closure** | Day of weekly closure | Display + filed |

Plus: register of fines (Form K), register of advances, accident register.

---

## Inspections

Inspectors of Shops are appointed by the Chandigarh Administration. They have powers (under §32) to:
- Enter the establishment at any reasonable time.
- Inspect registers, records, notices.
- Examine the employer / employees.
- Take copies of records.
- Issue improvement / prohibition notices.

Inspection-cum-Facilitator role under the OSH Code modernises this — inspections are now risk-based and randomised.

---

## Penalties (§26)

| Offence | Penalty |
|---|---|
| Failure to register | Up to **₹500–₹2,000** + ₹100/day continued |
| Excess hours / no weekly off | Up to **₹1,000** for first; doubled for repeat |
| Failure to grant leave | Up to **₹1,000** |
| False statements in registers | Up to **₹1,000** + imprisonment |
| Obstructing inspector | Up to **₹500** |

These pre-code penalties continue under the savings clause until Chandigarh issues code-aligned rules.
