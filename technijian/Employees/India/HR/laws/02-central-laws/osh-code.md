# Occupational Safety, Health and Working Conditions Code, 2020 (OSH Code)

**Status:** In force from **21-Nov-2025**.
**Last verified:** 2026-05-06.
**Repeals (13 acts, key ones):** Factories Act 1948 · Contract Labour (Regulation and Abolition) Act 1970 · Inter-State Migrant Workmen Act 1979 · Mines Act 1952 · Plantations Labour Act 1951 · Working Journalists Act 1955 · Sales Promotion Employees Act 1976 · Beedi & Cigar Workers Act 1966 · Cine Workers and Cinema Theatre Workers Act 1981 · Motor Transport Workers Act 1961 · Dock Workers (Safety) Act 1986 · Working Journalists (Wages) Act 1958 · Building & Other Construction Workers (RECS) Act 1996.

---

## What it does

Consolidates the **occupational safety, health, and working conditions** law for India. Key topics:
- Definitions of factory, mine, plantation, motor transport undertaking, building/construction, sales-promotion, etc.
- Duties of employers and employees regarding safety.
- Working hours, rest, and overtime for *factories*.
- Welfare facilities (canteen, first aid, crèche, drinking water).
- **Contract labour** (the chapter that replaces the 1970 Act).
- **Inter-state migrant workers** (the chapter that replaces the 1979 Act).
- Special provisions for women (night work).

For Technijian's IT/ITeS offices in Chandigarh / Panchkula, the **most relevant** parts are:

1. **Whether the office is a "factory" or a "commercial establishment"** — IT/ITeS offices are **not factories**, so factory-specific working-hours/leave provisions do not apply directly. Working hours / weekly off / leave are governed by the relevant **state Shops and Establishments Act**.
2. **Contract labour rules** — if Technijian engages a contractor (security, housekeeping, IT-services contractor) with ≥50 contract workers.
3. **Welfare facilities** — canteen / crèche thresholds.
4. **Women's night-shift rules** at the central level (now permissive with consent + safety).

## Is Technijian's office a "factory"?

### Definition (§2(w))

A "factory" means any premises (including the precincts) where:
- (a) **10 or more workers** are working, or were working on any day of the preceding 12 months, **and** in any part of which a manufacturing process is carried on **with the aid of power**, OR
- (b) **20 or more workers** are working etc., where a manufacturing process is carried on **without the aid of power**.

Excludes: mines, mobile units of armed forces, railway running shed, hotel/restaurant/eating place, etc.

### Definition of "manufacturing process" (§2(z))

Process for:
- Making, altering, repairing, ornamenting, finishing, packing, oiling, washing, cleaning, breaking up, demolishing, OR otherwise treating any article;
- Pumping oil, water, sewage;
- Generating, transforming, transmitting power;
- Composing types for printing, printing, book-binding;
- Constructing, reconstructing, repairing, refitting, finishing or breaking up ships or vessels;
- Preserving / storing any article in cold storage.

### IT/ITeS application

Information Technology services (software development, support, BPO, KPO) is **not a "manufacturing process"** under §2(z). Therefore an IT/ITeS office is **not a "factory"** even if 10+ employees work with computers.

**Practical conclusion:** Technijian's IT/ITeS offices in Chandigarh and Panchkula are **commercial establishments** under the relevant state Shops and Establishments Act, not factories under the OSH Code. This is the operative regime for working hours, leave, weekly off.

(Note: if Technijian operates a hardware-assembly / data-centre / printing-press / mailing operation, that part might qualify as a factory and trigger separate coverage. Each premises is assessed on its facts.)

## Chapter II — Registration

### One-stop registration (§3)

Every "establishment" (broader concept than factory; includes commercial establishments) must register **within 60 days** of the OSH Code's commencement, OR within 60 days of becoming a covered establishment.

- Online portal–based; replaces multiple registrations under 13 repealed acts.
- Single registration with the integrated portal + in some cases the state portal.

### Establishment thresholds for OSH Code coverage

| Type of establishment | Threshold |
|---|---|
| Factory (with power) | 10 workers |
| Factory (without power) | 20 workers |
| Mine | Any |
| Plantation | 10 hectares + 10 workers (or 20+ workers regardless) |
| Motor transport undertaking | 5+ vehicles |
| Building / construction | 10+ workers |
| Other establishments (commercial, service) | **10 workers** |

So even a 10-employee Technijian commercial office triggers OSH Code registration.

## Chapter III — Duties of Employers and Employees

### Employer duties (§6)

- Provide a workplace that is safe and without risk to health, including:
  - Plant, machinery, work systems
  - Premises with safe means of access and egress
  - Information, instruction, training, supervision
  - Welfare facilities
  - Free annual health examination (where prescribed)
- Notify accidents, dangerous occurrences, occupational diseases.

### Employee duties (§13)

- Take reasonable care of own and others' safety.
- Cooperate with employer in compliance.
- Use safety devices.
- Report dangers.

### Information & training (§7)

- Free annual health check-ups for workers in hazardous processes (not relevant for ordinary IT/ITeS).
- Information about hazards.
- Safety committee in factories with ≥250 workers.

## Chapter IV — Hours of Work, Annual Leave, Holidays

### Important note

This chapter applies primarily to **factories, mines, plantations, motor transport, building and construction**. It largely does **NOT** apply to commercial / service-sector establishments — those are governed by the **state Shops and Establishments Act**.

So for Technijian Chandigarh / Panchkula offices:
- **Hours of work, weekly off, leave** = state S&E Act (Punjab/Haryana).
- **Working women in night shift** = state S&E rules + the OSH Code's permissive central provision (below).
- **Welfare facilities (canteen, crèche)** = OSH Code thresholds carry over to commercial establishments through state rules.

### General hours framework (for factories — illustrative only)

- Maximum 48 hours/week, 9 hours/day (or 12 hours/day if averaged over the quarter).
- Rest interval of at least 30 min after 5 continuous hours.
- Weekly rest day (24 consecutive hours).
- Overtime: at least **double the normal wage**.

### Annual leave with wages (factories)

- 1 day for every 20 days worked, after 240 days in a year.
- Maximum 30 days carry-forward.

(IT/ITeS in Chandigarh/Panchkula uses the **state S&E Act formulae** — see [03-chandigarh-ut/shops-and-establishments.md](../03-chandigarh-ut/shops-and-establishments.md) and [04-haryana-panchkula/shops-and-establishments.md](../04-haryana-panchkula/shops-and-establishments.md).)

## Chapter V — Welfare Facilities

These thresholds apply across factories AND commercial establishments (via state rules):

| Facility | Threshold |
|---|---|
| **Drinking water, washing, urinal, latrine** | All establishments |
| **First aid box** | All establishments; one box per 150 workers |
| **Canteen** | ≥100 workers / employees |
| **Crèche** | **≥50 employees** (workplace or shared) |
| **Welfare officer** | ≥250 workers in factories |
| **Ambulance / OHC (occupational health centre)** | ≥250 / 500 workers (industry-specific) |

**Crèche threshold is critical for Technijian** — at 50 employees, a crèche must be provided. Can be on-site, in a co-tenant building, or via a third-party arrangement (paid by Technijian).

## Chapter VI — Provisions on Women

### Permissive night work (§43)

> No woman shall be required to work in any establishment except between the hours of 6 a.m. and 7 p.m.; provided that the appropriate Government may permit employment of women between 7 p.m. and 6 a.m. **subject to conditions of safety, holidays and working hours, and her consent**.

This is the **central enabling provision**. It effectively says: women may work between 7 PM and 6 AM provided (a) conditions of safety / hours, and (b) **consent** of the woman.

States/UTs implement this via their Shops and Establishments rules (since IT/ITeS is a commercial establishment, not a factory). Haryana's notification is dated **8-May-2025** — see [04-haryana-panchkula/women-night-shift.md](../04-haryana-panchkula/women-night-shift.md).

### Adequate safeguards (§43(2))

The OSH Code mandates:
- Adequate safety measures (lighting, transport, security).
- Holiday-and-shift policy.
- Consent of the woman in writing.

### Restrictions on hazardous processes (§43(2))

Women cannot be required to work in operations involving certain hazardous processes / dangerous machinery, except as notified.

## Chapter XI — Contract Labour

Replaces the Contract Labour (Regulation and Abolition) Act 1970.

### Applicability (§57)

- Applies to every establishment in which **20 or more contract workers** are employed, **or were employed on any day in the preceding 12 months**.
- **Threshold raised to 50 contract workers in the OSH Code central rules** (when finalised) — major change reducing compliance burden for smaller engagements.

### Registration of principal employer (§57)

- Principal employer must register their establishment with the licensing officer.
- Contractor must obtain a **licence** if engaging ≥50 contract workers.

### Prohibition

- Government may notify particular activities as not to be carried out through contract labour.
- Core activity vs. non-core: contract labour generally permitted in non-core (housekeeping, security, canteen, IT support, etc.) but prohibited / restricted in **core** activities of the establishment.

### Welfare obligations

Contractors must provide:
- Drinking water, latrines, washing facilities, restrooms.
- First-aid box.

Principal employer has secondary liability if the contractor fails.

### Wages

- Contractor pays in presence of an authorised representative of the principal employer.
- Wage period not exceeding 1 month.
- Where the contractor fails to pay, principal employer is liable.

### Implications for Technijian

If Technijian engages 50+ contract workers (housekeeping + security + IT contractors combined) at one location:
- Verify each contractor has a licence.
- Maintain register of contractors and contract workers.
- Ensure welfare obligations met (often delegated to contractor but liability flows back).
- Wages parity: contract workers must receive at least the same wages and benefits as direct employees doing similar work.

## Chapter XII — Inter-State Migrant Workers (ISMWs)

Replaces the Inter-State Migrant Workmen Act 1979.

### Applicability

- Establishments and contractors who recruit / employ workers from **another state**.

### Key obligations (when ≥10 ISMWs)

- Registration with licensing officer.
- **Displacement allowance** = 50% of monthly wages or ₹100, whichever is higher.
- **Journey allowance** + free to-and-fro travel.
- Suitable conditions of work (no discrimination vs. local workers).
- Wages parity — same rates as local workers for same work.
- Notification to home state and host state.

### IT/ITeS relevance

Less common to formally fall within ISMW regime in IT/ITeS because:
- Hiring is usually direct, not through a recruiter,
- Workers relocate voluntarily for higher pay,
- Threshold is ≥10 ISMWs in a single establishment via a single contractor.

But: if Technijian hires a contractor that brings 10+ workers from another state for a project, this chapter triggers.

## Chapter XIII — Audio-Visual & Other Special Workers

Provisions for:
- Audio-visual workers (replacing Cine Workers Act).
- Sales promotion employees.
- Working journalists.

Not directly relevant for typical IT/ITeS operations.

## Penalties (Chapter XIV)

| Offence | Penalty |
|---|---|
| Failure to register | Up to ₹50,000 |
| Failure to provide welfare facilities | Up to ₹2,00,000 |
| Failure to maintain canteen / crèche | Up to ₹50,000 + repeat ₹2,00,000 |
| Failure on safety provisions causing injury | Up to ₹3 lakh + imprisonment up to 2 years |
| Failure on safety causing death | Min ₹5 lakh + imprisonment up to 2 years |
| Contravention of contract labour provisions | Up to ₹1,00,000 |
| Repeat offences | Doubled |

## Records & registers

The central rules (when finalised) prescribe the format of:
- Registers of workers, wages, accidents, leave.
- Returns (annual / half-yearly).
- Display of abstracts of the Code.

The pre-code rules (Factories Rules 1950, Contract Labour Rules 1971, etc.) **continue as transitional rules** until replaced.

## Practical implications for Technijian

| Topic | Action |
|---|---|
| Register Chandigarh / Panchkula office under OSH Code | Within 60 days of crossing 10 workers (likely already past). Use state portal. |
| Crèche at 50 employees | Identify on-site or partner crèche option before next hiring spike. |
| Canteen at 100 employees | Plan canteen / catering arrangement. |

| Annual workplace safety audit | Conduct regardless of statutory threshold; document. |
