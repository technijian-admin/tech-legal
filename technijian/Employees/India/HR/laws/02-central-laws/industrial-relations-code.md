# Industrial Relations Code, 2020

**Status:** In force from **21-Nov-2025**.
**Last verified:** 2026-05-06.
**Repeals:** Industrial Disputes Act 1947 · Trade Unions Act 1926 · Industrial Employment (Standing Orders) Act 1946.

---

## What it does

Consolidates the law on:
- Industrial disputes (settlement, conciliation, adjudication),
- Trade unions (recognition, registration, rights),
- Standing Orders (employment conditions for workmen),
- Lay-off, retrenchment, transfer, closure of establishments,
- Strikes and lockouts.

For Technijian's IT/ITeS operations, the code is **most relevant** for:
- Termination procedures (notice, reasons, retrenchment compensation),
- Dispute resolution if an employee files a claim,
- The boundary between "worker" and "managerial/supervisory employee".

---

## Coverage — who is a "worker"?

Critical concept. Most code obligations attach only to "workers" (formerly "workmen").

### Definition (§2(zr))

A "worker" is any person employed in any industry **to do any manual, unskilled, skilled, technical, operational, clerical, or supervisory work** for hire/reward, **excluding**:

- (a) Persons employed mainly in **managerial or administrative capacity**.
- (b) Persons employed in **supervisory capacity drawing wages exceeding ₹18,000/month** *or* such higher amount as may be notified.

Includes: working journalists, sales-promotion employees, fixed-term employees, persons employed by contractors.
Excludes: Armed Forces; police; prisons.

### Practical implication for Technijian

| Role | "Worker" status |
|---|---|
| Software engineer, QA, support engineer (operational/technical work) | **Worker** under the code |
| Team lead with reporting authority but earning <₹18,000/month | Worker (rare in IT/ITeS) |
| Team lead earning >₹18,000/month and primarily supervisory | **Not a worker** (excluded) |
| Manager / VP / director (managerial capacity) | **Not a worker** |
| Receptionist / office admin (clerical) | Worker |
| Independent contractor (true freelancer) | Not a worker (no employer-employee relationship) |

**Most senior IT/ITeS staff are excluded** from the worker definition because supervisory wage exceeds ₹18,000 or they are in managerial roles. They are governed by **contract + service rules**, not the protective provisions of the IR Code. They can still file civil suits but not industrial-tribunal claims.

**Technijian's developers, support staff, and junior engineers earning <₹18,000/month or in non-supervisory roles ARE workers** and benefit from code protections.

## Chapter I & II — Trade Unions

### Registration (§§5–10)

- Any 7+ workers can form a trade union.
- Registration with state Registrar of Trade Unions.
- Registration confers immunity from civil suits for legitimate union activity.

### Recognition of "negotiating union" / "negotiating council" (§14)

The code formally recognises a **single union** or **council** to negotiate with the employer:

- If a single union has **51% or more** of workers as members → it is the **sole negotiating union**.
- If no single union meets 51% → a **negotiating council** is constituted with one representative per union holding ≥20% of workers.

### Implication for Technijian

Trade-union activity in IT/ITeS in India is uncommon but not unknown (e.g., NITES — Nascent Information Technology Employees Senate). Technijian's HR should:
- Not interfere with workers' right to associate.
- Engage with any registered union representing ≥10% of workforce.
- Recognise the negotiating union/council if one is constituted.

## Chapter III — Bipartite Forums

### Works Committee (§3)

- Mandatory in any establishment with **≥100 workers**.
- Equal representation of employer and workers.
- Promotes amity at the workplace; first-line forum for grievances.

### Grievance Redressal Committee (§4)

- Mandatory in any establishment with **≥20 workers**.
- Up to 10 members; equal employer-worker representation; ≥1 woman.
- Term: 1 year.
- Considers individual employee grievances. Decision within 30 days.
- Aggrieved worker may then approach the conciliation officer / industrial tribunal.

**Technijian's Indian offices with ≥20 employees must constitute a GRC.** This is in addition to the POSH ICC (which handles only sexual-harassment complaints).

## Chapter IV — Standing Orders

Standing orders are the **rulebook** governing conditions of employment for workers — classification, hours, holidays, leave, attendance, suspension, termination, grievances, misconduct, etc.

### Applicability (§28, §30)

- Mandatory in any establishment with **≥300 workers** (raised from 100 under the old 1946 Act).
- Below 300 workers: Standing Orders are **optional** — establishment can adopt **Model Standing Orders** (notified by Govt) or rely on its own service rules + employee handbook + appointment letter.

### Model Standing Orders

The Central Government has notified Model Standing Orders for:
- Manufacturing
- Mines
- Service sector (covers IT/ITeS)

A copy of the relevant Model Standing Orders is automatically deemed adopted in establishments below 300 workers that haven't certified their own. Technijian's Indian offices (likely <300 workers per location) can rely on the **service-sector Model Standing Orders** + employee handbook + contract.

### Certification & display (§§29, 31)

- If Standing Orders are certified, employer must give a copy to every worker on appointment and display them prominently.

---

## Chapter V — Notice of Change (§40)

Before changing service conditions specified in Schedule III (wages, hours, leave, allowances, occupational classification), employer must give **21 days written notice** to workers.

Schedule III items in IT/ITeS context:
- Wage payment intervals,
- Wage period,
- Allowances,
- Hours of work,
- Leave with wages,
- Withdrawal of customary concession.

---

## Chapter VII — Strikes and Lockouts (§§62–63)

In a "public utility service", strikes/lockouts require 14-day notice. In other industrial establishments, **60 days advance written notice** to employer (§62).

Illegal strikes / lockouts: penalties under §86.

## Chapter X — Lay-Off, Retrenchment & Closure

### Definitions

| Term | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Lay-off** (§2(t)) | Failure / refusal / inability of employer to give employment due to shortage of coal, power, raw material, breakdown, accumulation of stocks — **without termination**. |
| **Retrenchment** (§2(zh)) | Termination of services of a worker by employer **for any reason whatsoever**, except: dismissal as punishment after disciplinary inquiry; voluntary retirement; superannuation; non-renewal of fixed-term; continued ill-health. |
| **Closure** (§2(j)) | Permanent shutdown of the establishment / undertaking. |

### Lay-off compensation (§70)

- Worker who has completed **at least 1 year continuous service** and is laid off is entitled to compensation equal to **50% of basic wages + DA** for the days laid off.
- Maximum 45 days of compensation in any 12-month period (after which employer can retrench instead).

### Retrenchment procedure (§70)

For retrenchment of a worker who has completed ≥1 year continuous service:

1. Give **1 month's notice** in writing stating reasons (or pay 1 month's wages in lieu);
2. Pay **retrenchment compensation = 15 days' average pay × completed years of service**;
3. Notify the appropriate government / authority.

**Last-in-first-out rule (§71):** retrench in reverse order of seniority within the category, unless agreed otherwise.

### Government permission threshold (§78)

- Establishments with **≥300 workers** require **prior Government permission** for lay-off / retrenchment / closure.
- Below 300: only notice + compensation; no prior permission needed.

This is a **major change**: under the old IDA, the threshold was 100 workers. Most Technijian Indian offices are below 300 → only notice + compensation rules apply.

---

## Chapter XI — Closure (§82)

For closure of an undertaking:
- ≥300 workers: 60-day notice to government; prior permission required.
- <300 workers: 60-day notice to workers + appropriate government.

Closure compensation = same as retrenchment compensation.

## Chapter XII — Dispute Resolution

### Conciliation Officer (§43)

- Appointed by appropriate Government.
- Investigates, facilitates settlement.
- Submits failure / success report within 14 days.

### Industrial Tribunals (§44)

- Each tribunal has **two members** (judicial + administrative).
- Adjudicates disputes referred by Government or directly filed.

### Adjudication process

1. Worker files grievance with employer's GRC.
2. If unresolved, worker raises industrial dispute.
3. Conciliation Officer attempts settlement (14 days).
4. If failure, dispute goes to Industrial Tribunal.
5. Tribunal award is binding for 1 year (or longer if specified).
6. Appeal lies to High Court (writ jurisdiction); rarely to Supreme Court.

### Limitation (§53)

- An aggrieved worker must approach the conciliation officer within **3 years** of the cause of action (this codifies what was previously common-law practice).

### Recovery of money (§83)

- Money owed to a worker (wages, retrenchment compensation, gratuity, etc.) can be recovered from the employer as **arrears of land revenue** through the appropriate government — fast-track recovery without civil suit.

---

## Chapter XIII — Miscellaneous

### Re-skilling Fund (§83)

- Employer must contribute **15 days' wages per retrenched worker** to a state Re-skilling Fund.
- Fund used to provide retraining and re-skilling to retrenched workers.

This is a **new obligation** under the IR Code (no equivalent in old IDA).

### Penalties (§86)

| Offence | Penalty |
|---|---|
| Illegal strike / lockout | Worker: ₹1,000–₹10,000 + 1 month; Employer: ₹50,000–₹1,00,000 + 1 month |
| Contravention of standing orders | Up to ₹1,00,000 |
| Failure to retrench properly (no notice / compensation) | Up to ₹2,00,000 |
| Fraudulent registration of trade union | Up to ₹2,000 |
| Other contraventions | Up to ₹1,00,000 |

## Practical implications for Technijian

| Scenario | What to do |
|---|---|
| Fire a "worker" employee (operational role, <₹18,000 supervisory or non-supervisory) | Notice + retrenchment compensation; document reason; comply with last-in-first-out (or justify departure). Below 300 workers, no Govt permission needed. |
| Fire a "non-worker" (manager / senior with high salary supervisory) | Governed by **employment contract** — typically 30/60/90-day notice as per appointment letter; gratuity if eligible. IR Code protections do **not** apply. |
| Restructure / change wage components | Issue 21-day notice under §40 if it affects Schedule III items. |
| Employee grievance | Route to Grievance Redressal Committee (mandatory ≥20 workers); decision in 30 days. |
| ≥300 workers anywhere? | Standing Orders + Govt permission for retrenchment/closure required. (Likely not applicable to Technijian Chandigarh/Panchkula offices.) |
| Trade union activity | Permit; do not interfere; recognise negotiating union if 51%+ membership; cannot dismiss for union activity. |
