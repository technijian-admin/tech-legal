# Apprentices Act, 1961 + Child Labour (Prohibition and Regulation) Act, 1986 (as amended 2016)

Two related acts that survive the labour-code codification and govern the engagement of young workers (apprentices, trainees, minors).

**Last verified:** 2026-05-06.

---

# Part A — Apprentices Act, 1961

**Status:** In force; not subsumed under the four labour codes.

## What it does

Regulates the engagement of **apprentices** — persons undergoing apprenticeship training in a designated trade or in a graduate / technician / vocational course. Designed to formalise and protect the apprenticeship relationship and to ensure that India develops a skilled workforce.

Administered by the **Directorate General of Training (DGT)** under the Ministry of Skill Development and Entrepreneurship.

## Key concepts

### Apprentice (§2(aa))

A person who is undergoing apprenticeship training in pursuance of a contract of apprenticeship.

### Categories of apprentices (§2)

- **Trade apprentice** — undergoing training in a "designated trade".
- **Graduate apprentice** — engineering or technology graduate undergoing training related to their field.
- **Technician apprentice** — diploma holder in engineering or technology.
- **Technician (vocational) apprentice** — completed +2 / vocational stream.
- **Optional trade apprentice** — apprenticeship in a trade not designated.

For IT/ITeS like Technijian, the most relevant categories are **graduate apprentices** (B.E./B.Tech computer science) and **optional trade apprentices** (BCA, MCA, technical diploma, BSc IT, etc.).

## Mandatory engagement (§§3–11A)

- An establishment with **≥30 employees** (apprentices counted separately) must engage apprentices.
- The number of apprentices to be engaged is between **2.5% and 15% of total strength** (the "apprenticeship band"), of which:
  - 5% reserved for fresher / non-engaged candidates.
  - Of the apprenticeship intake, a sub-percentage is reserved for SC/ST/OBC and women in line with national skill-development priorities.

Below 30 employees: voluntary.

## Apprenticeship contract (§4)

- Must be in writing in the prescribed form.
- Registered with the Apprenticeship Adviser (state government).
- Cannot exceed maximum prescribed period (typically 6 months to 36 months depending on category).

## Stipend (§13, §13A)

- Minimum monthly stipend prescribed centrally; revised periodically.
- Currently (2024 rates, updated periodically):
  - School pass-outs (10th): ₹5,000–₹6,000
  - ITI pass-outs: ₹6,000–₹7,000
  - Diploma holders: ₹7,000–₹8,000
  - Graduate / technician: ₹8,000–₹9,000
- 25% of stipend may be reimbursed by Government to the employer under the National Apprenticeship Promotion Scheme (NAPS).

## Hours of work, leave, weekly off

- Apprentices' hours, leave, weekly off prescribed by the employer **within the bounds of the relevant law applicable to other employees**.
- Trade apprentices: 42–48 hrs/week.
- Graduate / technician apprentices: as per office staff at same establishment.

## Statutory benefits — what apprentices DO NOT get

- **Apprentices are NOT employees** under most other labour laws.
- They are NOT entitled to:
  - Bonus under Code on Wages
  - Gratuity under Social Security Code
  - PF under Social Security Code (debatable; generally excluded)
  - Lay-off / retrenchment compensation under IR Code
  - Coverage under Industrial Disputes / IR Code as workers
- They ARE protected by:
  - Workmen's Compensation / Employees' Compensation provisions (now Social Security Code) — for employment injuries.
  - Specific apprentice protections under the Apprentices Act.

## End of training

- On successful completion, apprentice receives a **certificate of apprenticeship** issued by the National Council for Vocational Training.
- Employer is **not obligated** to absorb the apprentice into permanent employment, but in practice is encouraged to.
- Many establishments use apprenticeship as a structured pre-hiring channel.

## Penalties (§30)

- Failure to engage required apprentices: fine up to ₹500 per day per default.
- Failure to comply with provisions: fine up to ₹500.
- Engaging an apprentice in contravention: fine up to ₹500.

(These small penalties are out of date and rarely enforced; primary risk is reputational and the loss of ability to access NAPS subsidies.)

## Implications for Technijian

If Technijian's Indian office headcount **crosses 30 employees**, an apprenticeship engagement plan becomes legally mandatory:
- Register with the National Apprenticeship Training Scheme portal: [apprenticeshipindia.gov.in](https://apprenticeshipindia.gov.in/).
- Identify designated trade / graduate trade apprenticeship slots.
- Engage 2.5% to 15% of headcount as apprentices.
- Pay minimum stipend.
- Maintain apprentice register and submit returns.

This is also a useful talent-pipeline mechanism for fresher engineers / BCA/MCA students.

# Part B — Child Labour (Prohibition and Regulation) Act, 1986 (as amended 2016)

**Status:** In force; not subsumed under any code.

## What it does

The 2016 amendment **renamed** the act to **"Child and Adolescent Labour (Prohibition and Regulation) Act, 1986"** and divided minors into two categories:

| Category | Age | Permitted work |
|---|---|---|
| **Child** | **Below 14 years** | **Total prohibition** on employment in any occupation or process. Sole exception: helping family or family enterprise (after school hours) and child artists in audiovisual entertainment industry. |
| **Adolescent** | **14–18 years** | Permitted EXCEPT in **hazardous occupations and processes** listed in the Schedule. |

## Total prohibition for children (under 14)

- §3: No child shall be employed in any occupation or process.
- Exceptions (§3(2)):
  - Helping family or family enterprise (other than hazardous), after school hours, on holidays.
  - Working as artist in audio-visual industry, including advertisement, films, TV serials or sports activities (except circus), subject to safety conditions and continuation of education.

## Restricted hazardous work for adolescents (14–18)

- §3A: An adolescent shall not be employed in any hazardous occupations or processes set out in the Schedule.
- Schedule includes: mining, manufacturing of explosives / matches / pesticides / chemicals / glass / leather / soap, work near furnaces, automobile workshops, mechanised fishing boats, petrol pumps, beedi making, carpet weaving, ceramic / pottery industry, slaughterhouses, food processing in chemicals exposure, work in confined spaces, etc.

For IT/ITeS — none of the listed hazardous processes typically arise in office software work.

**However**: Technijian should never employ a person under 14 (in any role), and adolescents (14–18) may only be employed in non-hazardous roles, with restricted hours as per state rules.

## Hours and conditions for adolescents

- Working hours not exceeding 6 hours/day (with 1-hour rest interval after 3 hours).
- No work between 7 PM and 8 AM.
- No overtime.
- Weekly rest day.
- Annual leave with wages.

## Health & safety

- Adolescents engaged must be medically fit (medical certificate every year).
- Establishment must keep a register of adolescents.

## Penalties (§14)

- Employing a child (under 14) in any occupation: imprisonment **6 months to 2 years**, or fine **₹20,000 to ₹50,000**, or both.
- Repeated offence: imprisonment **1 to 3 years**.
- Employing an adolescent (14–18) in a hazardous occupation: imprisonment **6 months to 2 years**, or fine **₹20,000 to ₹50,000**, or both.
- Parents / guardians: only liable from **second** offence onwards (penalty up to ₹10,000).

## Rehabilitation Fund

- Section 14B (introduced by 2016 amendment): a **Child and Adolescent Labour Rehabilitation Fund** is constituted at the district level.
- Employer found guilty of employing a child must deposit **₹20,000 per child** into the fund.
- Used for rehabilitation, education and welfare of rescued children.

## Implications for Technijian

- **Never employ a person below 14 years** in any capacity, including unpaid internships or "help out" arrangements.
- **Do not engage 14–18 year-olds** in IT/ITeS roles unless:
  - The role is non-hazardous (typically office work qualifies).
  - Hours are within 6/day, no night work.
  - Medical certificate of fitness obtained annually.
  - Register maintained.
- **Verify age at hiring**: collect Aadhaar, PAN, school/college certificate.
- **Apprentices**: while the Apprentices Act allows engagement at 14+ for trade apprenticeships, the Child & Adolescent Labour Act's hazardous-process restrictions apply concurrently.
