# HR Topic — POSH (Sexual Harassment Prevention) Compliance — Operational SOP

**Last verified:** 2026-05-06.

Operational SOP for the **POSH Act 2013** at Technijian Chandigarh / Panchkula. For substantive law see [02-central-laws/posh-act-2013.md](../02-central-laws/posh-act-2013.md).

---

## Why POSH compliance is non-negotiable

- Mandatory at **≥10 employees** — Technijian's Indian offices clearly exceed.
- Penalty for default: **₹50,000** per default; doubles on repeat + cancellation of registration.
- POSH compliance is a **precondition** for the Haryana women's-night-shift exemption — failing here can void other operational permissions.
- Brand and ESG impact: a serious POSH incident can devastate reputation, recruitment pipeline, and client trust.

---

## Setup checklist (one-time, then reviewed annually)

### 1. Constitute Internal Committee (IC)

For **each** Indian office (Chandigarh and Panchkula separately), constitute an IC by **written order signed by the employer** (CEO / India Head / Authorised Signatory).

#### Composition

| Role | Required attribute | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Presiding Officer** | Senior **woman** employee at the workplace | If no senior woman available, nominate from another office (with consent) |
| **Member 1** | Employee committed to women's cause / social-work / legal background | |
| **Member 2** | Same | |
| **External Member** | NGO / association / individual familiar with sexual harassment issues | Engagement letter + paid-per-meeting basis |

#### Rules

- **Minimum 4 members** total (Presiding Officer + 3, including 1 external).
- **At least 50% women** total.
- **Term: 3 years** (renewable by another order).
- IC chair / presiding officer signs IC orders / reports.

#### Practical members

- Presiding Officer: e.g., the senior-most woman in the office (HR Director, India Operations Head).
- Internal members: HR manager, employee representative (cross-functional).
- External member: engaged via local women's-rights NGO or legal practitioner. Sample organisations:
  - Vishakha Foundation
  - SAYRAH (Society for Awareness of Yatharth Rights and Alleviation of Harassment)
  - Women in Need Foundation
  - Local women lawyers' bar associations

### 2. Train the IC

- Within 30 days of constitution.
- Topics:
  - POSH Act 2013 + Rules 2013.
  - Definition of sexual harassment (broad and inclusive).
  - Inquiry procedure (natural justice, evidence handling).
  - Confidentiality.
  - Compensation factors.
  - Reporting + annual report.
- Refresh **annually**.
- External vendors / law firms commonly conduct certification programmes.

### 3. Train all employees

- **All employees** (men and women, all levels) must attend POSH sensitisation.
- Frequency: annual at minimum; ideally on joining + annual refresher.
- Method: in-person workshop preferred for ≥20 attendees; e-learning module acceptable for routine refresher.
- Records: signed attendance / completion certificate per employee in HR file.

### 4. Publish the POSH Policy

In Technijian's employee handbook, dedicated POSH policy section covering:

- Definition of sexual harassment (verbatim from §2(n) and §3).
- Workplace scope (broad).
- Zero-tolerance commitment.
- IC composition + contact details (email ID, drop-box, alternate channel).
- Complaint procedure (steps, timelines, conciliation option).
- Confidentiality.
- No retaliation guarantee.
- Action on proven complaint.
- Action on false complaint.
- Annual report and review mechanism.
- Grievance escalation if IC default.

### 5. Display + intranet

| # | Display item | Where |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | POSH Act statutory abstract | Notice board |
| 2 | IC composition (names, roles, contact) | Notice board + intranet + email signature |
| 3 | Complaint procedure | Same |
| 4 | Penal consequences of harassment | Same |
| 5 | Confidentiality undertaking | Notice board |

### 6. Establish complaint channels

- **Dedicated email**: e.g., posh@technijian.com — monitored by IC Presiding Officer.
- **Physical drop-box** (sealed) at HR area / IC chair office.
- **Direct phone** to Presiding Officer.
- **Anonymous tip-line** (third-party) as supplemental.

Complainants must be free to choose any channel; HR cannot insist on a specific intake.

## Receiving and handling a complaint

### When a complaint is received

| # | Action | Deadline | Owner |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Acknowledge receipt in writing | Same day | IC chair |
| 2 | Verify timeliness (within 3 months of incident; extendable up to another 3 months) | Day 1–2 | IC chair |
| 3 | If aggrieved woman requests, attempt **conciliation** | As mutually agreed | IC |
| 4 | If conciliation fails / not requested → initiate formal inquiry | Day 7 | IC |
| 5 | Issue notice to respondent + attach complaint | Day 7–10 | IC |
| 6 | Receive respondent's reply | Day 10–20 | IC |
| 7 | Conduct inquiry: hearings, witness depositions, document review | Day 20–80 | IC |
| 8 | Provide both parties final hearing | Day 80 | IC |
| 9 | Complete inquiry **within 90 days** | Day 90 | IC |
| 10 | Furnish report to employer | Within **10 days** of inquiry completion | IC |
| 11 | Action on report (disciplinary, compensation) | Within **60 days** of receipt of report | Employer |
| 12 | Record action; respond to both parties | Same | HR |
| 13 | Maintain confidentiality throughout | Continuous | All |

### Interim measures (§12) during inquiry

- Transfer of either party.
- Leave to aggrieved (paid; up to 3 months).
- Restraint on respondent (not contacting complainant, not attending site).
- Other reasonable measures.

### Confidentiality (§16)

- Identity of aggrieved, respondent, witnesses, contents — **not to be made public**.
- Penalty for breach: ₹5,000.
- Document storage: locked cabinet / restricted-access folder; need-to-know access only.

### Action on proven complaint (§13(3))

- Treat as misconduct under service rules.
- Disciplinary action: warning, censure, withholding promotion / increments, demotion, transfer, **termination**, blacklist for re-hire.
- Compensation to aggrieved (recoverable from respondent's salary or paid by respondent).
- Counselling / community service for respondent.

### Action on false / malicious complaint

- Disciplinary action against complainant (rarely invoked; courts very protective of bona fide complaints).
- **Mere inability to substantiate** the complaint is **NOT** equivalent to false / malicious — disciplinary action only if **mala fides** independently established.

## Annual report

The IC must prepare an **annual report** containing:

- Number of complaints received.
- Number of complaints disposed.
- Number of pending complaints over 90 days.
- Number of workshops / awareness programmes.
- Number of awareness sessions for IC.
- Action taken by employer.

### Filing

- Submitted to **District Officer** (DC, Panchkula / Chandigarh) by **prescribed date** (typically end of January for previous calendar year).
- Filing receipt retained.
- Director's report / company's annual return (Companies Act 2013 § 134(3)) discloses POSH compliance.

## Common pitfalls

| Pitfall | Risk |
|---|---|
| IC has only 3 members | Composition non-compliant; ICC orders may be invalidated |
| External member on retainer expired but still listed | Composition defect; complaints handled on this composition challenged |
| Annual training pushed to "next quarter" repeatedly | Penalty + women's-night-shift exemption risk |
| POSH policy not updated for §2(n) explicit categories | Complaints not categorized properly |
| Inquiry exceeds 90 days | Procedural defect; respondent may challenge |
| Action delayed beyond 60 days | Employer non-compliance; aggrieved may approach Local Committee |
| Confidentiality breach (e.g., gossip in HR team) | ₹5,000 fine + harm to aggrieved |
| Implicit retaliation against complainant (transferred, withheld promotion) | Civil suit + reputation |
| Single IC for multiple administrative units | Geographic-coverage defect |

---

## When the complaint involves the employer / senior leadership

If the respondent is the **employer himself** or someone the IC cannot fairly investigate (e.g., the employer is the only senior person):

- The aggrieved woman may file directly with the **Local Committee** constituted by the District Officer.
- IC chair (or designated person) facilitates this referral.
- Confidentiality and protection from retaliation continue.
