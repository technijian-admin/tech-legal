# HR Topic — Termination and Notice

**Last verified:** 2026-05-06.

Operational guide for ending the employment relationship — by employer or employee — at Technijian Chandigarh / Panchkula.

---

## Conceptual map

The Indian termination regime depends on:

1. Whether the employee is a **"worker" or not** under IR Code 2020.
2. Whether the **establishment has ≥300 workers** (triggers Government permission for retrenchment / closure).
3. Whether termination is **voluntary (resignation)**, **for cause (disciplinary)**, **without cause (retrenchment)**, **on superannuation**, or **death/disablement**.
4. The contractual notice period in the offer letter / appointment letter.
5. Sector-specific overlays (e.g., POSH, anti-discrimination).

---

## "Worker" vs. "Non-worker" — IR Code 2020 §2(zr)

For Technijian Indian operations:

| Role | Probable status |
|---|---|
| Software engineer, QA, support engineer (operational/technical) | **Worker** |
| Administrative staff, receptionist, office boy | **Worker** |
| Junior team lead (<₹18K supervisory) | Worker |
| Mid-level engineer earning above ₹18K, primarily individual contributor | Worker (operational/technical) |
| Senior engineer / architect with supervisory role earning >₹18K/month and primarily supervisory | **Not a worker** |
| Project manager, account manager (managerial / administrative capacity) | **Not a worker** |
| Directors / VPs / Officers | Not a worker |

> **Non-workers** are governed by **contract + service rules**; protections under IR Code retrenchment/standing orders/conciliation **do not apply** (they may pursue civil suits).
> **Workers** get the full IR Code protections.

## Voluntary resignation

### Employee gives notice

- Notice period as per **offer letter / appointment letter**.
- Standard practice: 30 days (junior/mid) or 60–90 days (senior).

### Acceptance of resignation

- Employer **must accept** or specifically respond.
- Cannot withhold acceptance to "force" employee to stay.

### Employer may waive notice

- If both parties agree, notice can be shortened.
- Employee remains entitled to all benefits up to last day.

### Pay-in-lieu

- Employee can buy out notice by paying **basic salary for unserved days**.
- Employer can relieve early and pay basic for unserved days (rare; usually not required).

### Required actions on resignation

| # | Action | Deadline |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Acknowledge resignation in writing | Within 7 days |
| 2 | Calculate exit dues (final salary, EL encashment, gratuity, etc.) | Before last working day |
| 3 | Issue relieving letter | On last working day |
| 4 | Issue experience / service certificate | On last working day |
| 5 | Final settlement (within 2 working days of cessation per Code on Wages §17) | Within 2 days |
| 6 | Issue Form 16 (final) | By 15-June of next FY |
| 7 | Update EPFO (UAN exit), ESIC (IP exit) | Within 1 month |
| 8 | Surrender of company assets (laptop, ID card, access) | On last working day |
| 9 | DPDP — initiate erasure of non-essential personal data after retention period | Per DPDP retention schedule |

## Termination for cause (disciplinary)

### Grounds

Standard misconduct grounds (per Service Rules / Code of Conduct):
- Theft, fraud, dishonesty
- Wilful insubordination
- Habitual absence
- Drunkenness during work
- Riotous / disorderly behaviour
- Violation of confidentiality / IP terms
- Breach of POSH
- Material misrepresentation in CV
- Physical / sexual harassment
- Working for competitor / moonlighting (without disclosure)

### Procedure (workers under IR Code; same principles apply by contract for non-workers)

1. **Charge sheet / show-cause notice** issued.
2. **Inquiry**: independent inquiry officer; principles of natural justice; opportunity to defend with assistance of co-worker / counsel.
3. **Inquiry report** with findings.
4. **Show-cause on punishment**.
5. **Decision** (warning, suspension, demotion, or termination).
6. Right of appeal to designated authority.

### Termination for misconduct

- Notice not required (since termination is for cause, not retrenchment).
- **Gratuity may be forfeited** for wilful damage / loss / theft / moral turpitude (Social Security Code).
- Final settlement still due (salary up to date, EL encashment, etc.).

### POSH-related termination

- If IC has concluded harassment proven, termination is among the available actions.
- Procedure: comply with §13 of POSH Act; act within 60 days of IC report.

## Retrenchment (termination without cause; for "workers" only)

### When applicable (IR Code §70)

- Termination of services of a worker by employer for any reason whatsoever **except**:
  - Voluntary resignation,
  - Superannuation,
  - Non-renewal of fixed-term contract,
  - Continued ill-health,
  - Disciplinary dismissal after inquiry.

### Mandatory procedure (IR Code §70)

For a worker who has completed **≥1 year continuous service**:

1. **Notice in writing**: minimum **1 month** stating reasons. (OR pay 1 month's wages in lieu.)
2. **Retrenchment compensation**: **15 days' average pay × completed years of service**.
3. **Notice to appropriate Government**: as prescribed.

### Last-in-first-out rule (§71)

- Retrench in **reverse order of seniority** within the affected category.
- Departure from this requires recorded reasons.

### Government permission (§78)

- **<300 workers**: NO prior Govt permission needed. Notice + compensation suffice.
- **≥300 workers**: Prior Govt permission required (raised from 100 by IR Code 2020).

For Technijian Chandigarh / Panchkula likely <300 workers per location → no permission, but notice + compensation are mandatory.

### Re-skilling Fund (IR Code §83)

- Employer must contribute **15 days' wages per retrenched worker** to a state Re-skilling Fund.
- Used for retraining the retrenched workers.

## Termination of "non-workers" (managers, senior staff)

For non-workers:

- **Governed by employment contract** (offer letter, appointment letter, service rules).
- Notice period as per contract (30/60/90 days).
- No statutory retrenchment compensation (which applies only to workers).
- Gratuity if ≥5 years (1 year for fixed-term) — Social Security Code.
- All other final-settlement items same as voluntary resignation.

### Best practice: include "without-cause" termination clause

Most modern Indian employment contracts include a clause:
> "Either party may terminate this employment without cause by giving 60 days' written notice in advance, or by paying basic salary in lieu of notice."

This makes the legal position predictable for non-workers.

## Probationary termination

- During probation: shorter notice as per offer letter (typically 15 days from either side).
- All statutory benefits up to last day.
- Reasoning recorded (avoid employee claims of discrimination / arbitrary termination).

---

## Fixed-term employment expiry

- IR Code §2(o) recognises fixed-term employment.
- Expiry of FTE term = **non-renewal**, **not** retrenchment.
- No retrenchment compensation due (since it falls within the "non-renewal" exception of §2(zh)).
- **Gratuity is due** if ≥1 year of service (FTE special rule).
- Final settlement same as voluntary resignation.

> **Caveat**: If FTE is repeatedly renewed for substantial periods, tribunals may reclassify as permanent → triggering retrenchment protections. Best practice: issue genuine FTEs with clear scope.

## Closure of establishment

If Technijian decides to close its Chandigarh or Panchkula office:

### Procedure (IR Code §82)

1. **60-day notice** to all workers + appropriate Government.
2. Closure compensation = same as retrenchment (15 days' avg pay × years of service).
3. **≥300 workers**: prior Govt permission required.
4. <300 workers: no permission, just notice + compensation.

## Lay-off (temporary)

If Technijian temporarily cannot provide work (no termination):

### IR Code §70 (lay-off compensation)

- Worker with ≥1 year continuous service:
  - **50% of (Basic + DA)** for laid-off days,
  - Maximum 45 days in any 12-month period.
- After 45 days, employer must either resume work, retrench, or extend lay-off (employee's choice).

## Death / disablement

### Death in service

- Last salary up to date of death.
- EL encashment.
- **Gratuity** (no minimum-service condition for death) — paid to nominee.
- EPS pension to widow / dependents.
- EDLI insurance lump sum (linked to EPF salary; generally up to ₹7L).
- ESIC dependents' benefit (if covered).
- Group accident / life insurance (if Technijian has such cover).

### Permanent disablement during service

- Last salary up to date of disablement.
- Gratuity (no minimum service).
- Employees' Compensation (Social Security Code Chapter VII) — schedule-based.
- ESIC permanent disablement benefit (if covered).
- Eligibility for re-deployment in suitable role.

---

## Special protections

### Pregnant employees

- Cannot be dismissed during pregnancy or maternity leave (Maternity Benefit chapter, Social Security Code §65).
- Termination during this period is illegal except for gross misconduct established through fair inquiry.

### Whistleblower / POSH complainant

- Cannot be terminated as retaliation (POSH Act + general jurisprudence).
- Complaint pre-dating termination shifts burden of proof onto the employer.

### Disability

- Cannot be terminated solely on grounds of disability acquired during employment.
- Reasonable accommodation must be considered (Rights of Persons with Disabilities Act 2016).

## Common pitfalls

| Pitfall | Risk |
|---|---|
| Treating "worker" as "non-worker" and skipping retrenchment compensation | IR Code §86 fine + reinstatement / back-wages |
| Forgetting last-in-first-out for retrenchment | Tribunal challenge |
| Final settlement not within 2 working days | Code on Wages §54 fine |
| Gratuity not paid within 30 days | Interest @ 8–10% + penalty |
| Form 16 delayed past 15-June | TDS Section 272A fine |
| Termination during pregnancy | Maternity chapter penalty + civil suit |
| Termination retaliation against POSH complaint | POSH Act + Aureliano Fernandes line of cases |
| Withholding experience certificate | Civil suit + reputational damage |
| Not reporting under Re-skilling Fund | IR Code violation |
