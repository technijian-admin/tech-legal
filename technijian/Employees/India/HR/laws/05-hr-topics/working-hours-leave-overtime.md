# HR Topic — Working Hours, Leave, and Overtime

**Last verified:** 2026-05-06.

Operational guide consolidating all rules on hours, weekly off, leave, holidays, and overtime applicable to Technijian Chandigarh and Panchkula offices.

---

## Source of law

For IT/ITeS commercial establishments (not factories), hours / leave / overtime are governed by:

| Source | Application |
|---|---|
| **State Shops & Establishments Act** (Punjab S&E Act, extended to Chandigarh and adopted by Haryana) | Primary — hours, weekly off, EL/SL/CL, opening hours |
| **Code on Wages, 2019** | Overtime rate (≥2× ordinary) — central |
| **Holidays Act** (Chandigarh: 1965; Haryana: 1971) | National & festival holidays |
| **Code on Social Security, 2020** | Maternity, sick leave (in some interpretations) |
| **OSH Code, 2020** | Welfare facilities, generic safety |

---

## Working hours

### Limits per employee (both Chandigarh & Panchkula)

| Parameter | Limit |
|---|---|
| Daily hours of work | **9 hours** maximum |
| Weekly hours of work | **48 hours** maximum |
| Spread-over (incl. breaks) | **12 hours** maximum |
| Continuous work without break | **5 hours** (then 30 min rest) |
| Rest interval | **30 minutes minimum** |

### Establishment opening / closing hours

- Default: 9 AM to 7:45 PM (Haryana) / 9 AM to 8 PM (Chandigarh).
- IT/ITeS, banking, hotels, 100% EOU, logistics, warehousing — exempted from fixed opening/closing hours under state notifications. Operate 24x7 if needed.

### Shift / continuous-process exemption

- For 24x7 operations:
  - Maintain 3 / 4 shifts per day.
  - Each individual employee still bound by 9-hour day, 48-hour week, weekly off.
  - Establishment closure rule (one day per week) is generally exempted by notification for IT/ITeS.

### Women in night shift

- Default: prohibited 8 PM – 6 AM.
- Exempt with conditions: see [04-haryana-panchkula/women-night-shift.md](../04-haryana-panchkula/women-night-shift.md) (Haryana) and equivalent Chandigarh notification.

### Adolescents (14–18)

- Maximum 6 hrs/day.
- No work between 7 PM and 8 AM.
- 30-min rest after 3 continuous hours.
- Weekly off mandatory.

---

## Weekly off

- **One full day off per week** for every employee.
- For 24x7 operations: stagger weekly offs so different employees off on different days; the establishment itself remains open.
- Wages cannot be deducted for the weekly off.

---

## Public holidays

| Type | Chandigarh UT | Haryana (Panchkula) |
|---|---|---|
| **National (mandatory)** | 3 (Republic Day, Independence Day, Gandhi Jayanti) | 3 (same) |
| **Festival (employer chooses from notified list)** | 4 | 5 |
| **Total mandatory** | **7** | **8** |
| **Restricted holidays (employee opt-in)** | a few additional | a few additional |

### Working on a holiday

- Pay **double the wage** for that day, OR
- Provide a **substitute holiday with wages within 30 days**.

### Holiday list publication

- Annual list published at establishment **before 31 December** of the prior year.
- Filed with state inspector / DLC.

---

## Earned / privilege leave (annual leave)

| Parameter | Both jurisdictions |
|---|---|
| Eligibility | After **240 days** worked in a calendar year |
| Quantum | **1 day per 20 days worked** ≈ 15 days/year |
| Adolescents (14–18) | **1 day per 15 days worked** ≈ 24 days/year |
| Maximum carry-forward | 30 days |
| Encashment on cessation | Mandatory for unused EL |
| Application | Min 15 days advance for non-emergency |
| Refusal grounds | Bona fide business exigency only; if refused, must be allowed if applied again |

---

## Sick leave

- **7 days per year** with full pay (state rule, both jurisdictions).
- Medical certificate from registered medical practitioner required for >2 days.
- Many employers grant 10–12 days/year.
- Cannot be substituted for casual leave or annual leave.

---

## Casual leave

- **7 days per year** with pay (state rule).
- For unforeseen / personal reasons.
- Cannot be combined with EL beyond limited continuous days.
- Lapses on year-end (no carry-forward unless policy allows).

---

## Maternity leave (Code on Social Security 2020, Chapter VI)

### Eligibility

- Worked at least **80 days** in the establishment in the 12 months preceding expected delivery.

### Quantum

| Situation | Paid leave |
|---|---|
| First 2 children | **26 weeks** (8 prenatal + 18 postnatal) |
| 3rd child onwards | **12 weeks** |
| Adoption (child <3 mo) | 12 weeks |
| Surrogacy (commissioning mother) | 12 weeks |
| Miscarriage / medical termination | **6 weeks** |
| Tubectomy | 2 weeks |
| Pregnancy / delivery–related illness | Additional **1 month** |

### Wages during leave

- Average daily wage for 3 calendar months preceding leave.
- Paid by employer.

### Nursing breaks

- 2 breaks/day until child is **15 months old**, in addition to regular intervals.

### Crèche

- Mandatory at **≥50 employees**.

### Work-from-home

- After 26 weeks, employee may agree with employer for WFH if nature of work allows.

### Penalties

- Failure to pay maternity benefit: imprisonment up to 6 months / fine up to ₹50,000 / both.

---

## Paternity leave

- **Not statutorily mandated** for private sector.
- Many employers offer **5–15 days** paid paternity leave as policy.
- Public sector: 15 days under Central Civil Services rules.

Recommendation: include in Technijian's HR policy as 5–15 paid days.

---

## Other leave (typically by HR policy)

- Bereavement leave.
- Compensatory off (for working on weekly off / holiday).
- Wedding leave.
- Sabbatical (for senior employees).
- Study leave.
- Volunteer / CSR leave.

These are not statutorily mandated; the employer's handbook governs.

## Overtime

### Definition

- Hours beyond **9/day** OR **48/week** (whichever is exceeded first).
- For shift workers: hours beyond the rostered shift duration that align with daily / weekly limits.

### Rate (Code on Wages 2019, §13)

- **At least twice (2×) the ordinary rate of wages**.

### Ceiling on overtime

- Maximum **50 hours per quarter** (state S&E Act / OSH Code rules).

### Overtime in IT/ITeS practice

- Many IT/ITeS employers absorb overtime into the salary (defining it as part of CTC).
- This is technically non-compliant if overtime is regularly required and not separately compensated.
- Best practice: define base hours; track overtime; pay at 2× rate or grant compensatory off.

### Compensatory off

- Cannot replace overtime pay for "workers" under the IR Code unless agreed in employment contract.
- For non-workers (managerial/supervisory above ₹18K), compensatory off is acceptable.

---

## Specific leave rules for "workers"

For employees who qualify as "workers" under the IR Code (operational / clerical / supervisory under ₹18K), additional protections apply:
- Termination requires notice + retrenchment compensation if applicable.
- Cannot deny statutory leave without grounds.
- Annual leave / earned leave is a vested right.

---

## Travel time and on-call time

### Travel

- Travel from home to office: not work hours.
- Travel during work for client meetings / projects: counts as work.
- Travel for company-sponsored training: counts as work.

### On-call

- On-call hours not at workplace, but reachable: typically not counted as work hours.
- If on-call requires immediate response and restricts personal liberty: may count as work hours.
- Best practice: define on-call rate / allowance separately.

| Register | Purpose | Form |
|---|---|---|
| Attendance register / biometric log | Hours, attendance | OSH-aligned / S&E |
| Leave register (per employee) | Entitlement, taken, balance | Form H (S&E) |
| Wages register | Wages, deductions | Form G (S&E) |
| Overtime register | OT hours, rate, payment | OSH-aligned / S&E |
| Holiday list (calendar year) | Display copy | State Holidays Act |
| Maternity leave / benefit register | Maternity beneficiaries | Social Security Code rules |

## Common compliance gaps

| Gap | Risk |
|---|---|
| Overtime not separately tracked / paid at 2× | Code on Wages §54 fine ₹50K + back-pay liability |
| Working >48 hours/week regularly | S&E Act fine + EHS risk + tribunal claims |
| Less than 30-min break after 5 hrs | S&E Act fine |
| Skipping weekly off for "deadlines" | S&E Act fine + worker claims |
| Treating maternity leave as part of EL | Maternity Benefit chapter violation; criminal liability |
| Forcing women to work nights without consent | Punjab S&E Rule 15 violation; possible POSH crossover |
| Carrying EL beyond 30 days w/o encashment on exit | Wage-claims / labour-court |
| Not publishing holiday list before 31-Dec | Holidays Act fine |
| Crèche missing at 50+ employees | Maternity / OSH violation |
