## Critical context (read before anything else)

### The legal landscape changed dramatically on 21-Nov-2025

The **four consolidated Labour Codes** were notified into force on **21-Nov-2025**:

1. **Code on Wages, 2019** — replaces Minimum Wages Act, Payment of Wages Act, Payment of Bonus Act, Equal Remuneration Act.
2. **Industrial Relations Code, 2020** — replaces Industrial Disputes Act, Trade Unions Act, Industrial Employment (Standing Orders) Act.
3. **Code on Social Security, 2020** — replaces EPF Act, ESI Act, Payment of Gratuity Act, Maternity Benefit Act, Employees' Compensation Act, and others.
4. **Occupational Safety, Health and Working Conditions Code, 2020 (OSH Code)** — replaces Factories Act, Contract Labour Act, Inter-State Migrant Workmen Act, and others.

29 prior central labour laws were **repealed** on the same date.

> **Caveat:** While the codes are in force, **operationalisation requires central + state rules**. Draft central rules were published 30-Dec-2025; final notification was expected ~1-Apr-2026. **Most states (including Haryana and Chandigarh UT) are still publishing/finalising their state rules.** During the transition, employers must continue to comply with the new code language *and* monitor state-level rule notifications.

See [01-labour-codes-overview.md](01-labour-codes-overview.md) for full timeline & implications.

### Special acts that survive the codification

Some employment-adjacent acts were **not** subsumed and continue to apply directly:

- **Sexual Harassment of Women at Workplace (Prevention, Prohibition and Redressal) Act, 2013** ("POSH Act") — see [02-central-laws/posh-act-2013.md](02-central-laws/posh-act-2013.md)
- **Apprentices Act, 1961** — see [02-central-laws/apprentices-and-child-labour.md](02-central-laws/apprentices-and-child-labour.md)
- **Child Labour (Prohibition and Regulation) Act, 1986** (as amended 2016) — same file
- **Maternity Benefit (Amendment) Act, 2017** provisions — partly subsumed, but the 26-week paid leave entitlement carries forward through the Social Security Code
- **Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023** — relevant for HR data; see [02-central-laws/dpdp-and-hr-data.md](02-central-laws/dpdp-and-hr-data.md)
- **Income-tax Act, 1961** (TDS on salaries — out of scope here, but coordinate with finance)

### What's specific to Chandigarh & Panchkula

- **Chandigarh** is a **Union Territory** — administered directly by the Government of India through an Administrator. For labour matters, Chandigarh historically extended **Punjab state laws** (e.g., the Punjab Shops and Commercial Establishments Act, 1958). UT labour department: Chandigarh Administration, Department of Labour.
- **Panchkula** is a city in **Haryana state**. Haryana adopted the same Punjab Shops and Commercial Establishments Act, 1958 at its formation in 1966 and has since amended it. Haryana also has its own state-specific welfare-fund act, festival-holidays act, and rules on women's night-shift work. State labour department: hrylabour.gov.in.

## Document conventions

- All amounts in **Indian Rupees (INR / ₹)** unless otherwise stated.
- All dates in **ISO format (YYYY-MM-DD)**.
- "Establishment" = factory, shop, or commercial establishment as relevant. Technijian Chandigarh/Panchkula offices are typically **commercial establishments** (not factories), so the **Shops and Establishments Act of the relevant jurisdiction** is the primary governing instrument for working hours, leave, and registrations — alongside the central Codes.
- "S&E Act" = Shops and Establishments Act (state-specific name varies).
